# Conversion Rate Diagnostics

## Purpose
Audit conversion rates at every funnel stage against benchmarks, identify root causes of underperformance across traffic quality, page experience, offer, and trust, and prioritise interventions by expected impact.

---

## When to Use This Skill
- Conversion rates are below benchmark or declining
- A new funnel or landing page is underperforming
- Before investing in paid traffic to ensure the funnel is ready
- As a regular quarterly audit to prevent gradual decay

---

## Briefing Checklist
Before starting, confirm:
1. **Business objective** — Conversion improvement, funnel audit, or pre-launch readiness?
2. **Which funnel stage** — Full funnel or a specific stage (ad → landing page, trial → paid)?
3. **Data access** — Analytics, session recording, heatmap, and CRM data available?
4. **Benchmarks** — Industry or internal baseline conversion rates to compare against?
5. **Output format** — Audit report, prioritised fix list, or A/B test roadmap?

---

## Process

### Step 1 — Map the Conversion Funnel
Define every stage in the conversion path and its corresponding metric:

| Stage | Metric | Current Rate | Benchmark | Gap |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Ad → Landing page | CTR | —% | —% | — |
| Landing page → CTA click | Click rate | —% | —% | — |
| CTA → Form start | Start rate | —% | —% | — |
| Form start → Submit | Completion rate | —% | —% | — |
| Submit → MQL | Qualification rate | —% | —% | — |
| MQL → SQL | Acceptance rate | —% | —% | — |
| SQL → Closed | Win rate | —% | —% | — |

### Step 2 — Identify the Largest Gaps
Prioritise the stage with the biggest gap between current rate and benchmark. A 5% improvement at a high-volume stage is worth more than 30% improvement at a low-volume stage.

Quantify the revenue impact of improving each stage by 10%:
- Calculate how many additional customers result from the improvement
- Multiply by average revenue per customer
- This becomes the ROI case for the CRO investment

### Step 3 — Diagnose Root Causes
For each underperforming stage, investigate four root cause categories:

**Traffic Quality**
- Is the audience reaching this stage the right ICP?
- Are ad audiences or organic keywords sending unqualified visitors?
- Is there a mismatch between the ad promise and the landing page content?

**Page Experience**
- Is the page loading quickly (< 3 seconds)?
- Is the value proposition clear above the fold?
- Is the page mobile-optimised?
- Is the copy written for the audience at this stage?
- Are there unnecessary distractions or navigation options?

**Offer**
- Is the offer compelling for this stage in the buyer journey?
- Is the ask proportionate to the level of trust established?
- Is the offer clearly differentiated from alternatives?
- Is risk-reversal present (free trial, guarantee, no credit card)?

**Trust**
- Are social proof elements present (reviews, logos, case studies)?
- Is the brand credible and recognisable to this audience?
- Are security and privacy signals visible on forms?
- Is the page free from contradiction with the ad or email that sent the visitor here?

### Step 4 — Use Qualitative Data
Supplement analytics with:
- Session recordings (where do users drop off or hesitate?)
- Heatmaps (what do users click, where do they stop scrolling?)
- Form analytics (which fields cause abandonment?)
- Customer interviews (what stopped you from signing up / buying?)

### Step 5 — Prioritise Interventions
Score each intervention using ICE:

| Issue | Impact (1–10) | Confidence (1–10) | Ease (1–10) | ICE Score |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| — | — | — | — | — |

Run highest ICE score interventions first. Document as test hypotheses before implementing.

---

## Output Formats

### CRO Diagnostic Report
- Funnel stage map with conversion rates and benchmarks
- Root cause analysis by stage (traffic, experience, offer, trust)
- Qualitative findings summary
- Prioritised intervention list with ICE scores

### Optimisation Prioritisation Matrix
- Ranked interventions by ICE score
- Expected impact and implementation effort
- Test hypothesis for each

### A/B Test Roadmap
- 90-day testing calendar
- Hypothesis, control, variant, metric for each test
- Sequenced to avoid conflicts and build on learnings

---

## Common Mistakes to Avoid
- **Fixing pages before diagnosing traffic quality.** If the wrong audience is arriving, the page is irrelevant.
- **Optimising the wrong stage.** Fix the largest gap, not the most visible one.
- **Making changes without testing.** Intuitive fixes can reduce conversion — always test.
- **Ignoring mobile.** If >50% of traffic is mobile, mobile experience is often the primary issue.
- **Short test durations.** Insufficient data leads to false conclusions — run tests for at least one full business cycle.

---

## Quality Check
Before submitting, ask:
- [ ] Are all funnel stages mapped with current and benchmark rates?
- [ ] Is the biggest conversion gap identified and sized in revenue terms?
- [ ] Are root causes assessed across all four categories (traffic, experience, offer, trust)?
- [ ] Are qualitative data sources (recordings, interviews) incorporated?
- [ ] Is every intervention documented as a testable hypothesis with an ICE score?

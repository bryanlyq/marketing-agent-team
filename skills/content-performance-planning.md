# Content Performance Planning

## Purpose
Define content KPIs at every funnel stage, build reporting frameworks that connect content activity to business outcomes, and use performance data to continuously improve the content strategy — so content investment is accountable, not assumed.

---

## When to Use This Skill
- Content is produced without clear success metrics
- Reporting focuses on vanity metrics (page views, social followers) rather than business outcomes
- No clear link between content activity and pipeline or revenue
- Scaling content investment and needing to justify it to leadership
- Quarterly strategy review requiring a content performance assessment

---

## Briefing Checklist
Before starting, confirm:
1. **Business objectives** — What must content contribute to? (Pipeline, retention, brand, product adoption?)
2. **Content programme scope** — What content types and channels are in scope?
3. **Analytics infrastructure** — What tracking and attribution is in place? (GA4, CRM, MAP?)
4. **Attribution model** — How is marketing contribution to pipeline and revenue tracked?
5. **Audience for reporting** — Who will consume this reporting? (CMO, board, content team, agency?)

---

## The Content Performance Problem

Most content programmes measure the wrong things:
- **Too far upstream:** Page views and impressions show content is being consumed; they don't show whether it's driving business outcomes
- **Too disconnected from revenue:** Content teams track engagement; sales teams track pipeline; nobody connects them
- **Too siloed by format:** Blog metrics, social metrics, and email metrics reported separately obscure the compound effect of content

The goal is a measurement framework that connects content activity → audience engagement → lead generation → pipeline influence → revenue attribution.

---

## Content KPI Framework by Funnel Stage

| Stage | Content Job | KPIs |
|---|---|---|
| **Awareness (TOFU)** | Drive reach and brand recognition | Unique visitors, organic search impressions, social reach, branded search volume |
| **Engagement (TOFU/MOFU)** | Attract and hold attention | Time on page, scroll depth, pages per session, repeat visits, email subscribers |
| **Consideration (MOFU)** | Build credibility and preference | Content downloads, webinar registrations, newsletter open rate, return visitor rate |
| **Conversion (BOFU)** | Drive leads and pipeline | Lead volume, content-attributed MQLs, conversion rate from content to lead |
| **Pipeline influence** | Accelerate deals | Content touched by opportunities, content used in sales cycles |
| **Revenue attribution** | Connect content to closed revenue | Revenue influenced by content, pipeline sourced by content |
| **Retention (post-purchase)** | Drive adoption and reduce churn | Feature adoption rate post-content, NPS among engaged content consumers, churn rate differential |

Never report only one layer of this hierarchy. Top-of-funnel metrics without bottom-of-funnel context make content look useful but not valuable. Bottom-of-funnel metrics without top-of-funnel context undervalue brand-building content.

## Process

### Step 1 — Define Success Metrics for Each Content Pillar
For each content pillar in the ecosystem, define:
- **Primary KPI:** The metric that defines whether this pillar is working
- **Secondary KPIs:** Supporting metrics that explain performance
- **Guardrail metric:** A metric that should not degrade

Example (SEO pillar):
- Primary KPI: Organic leads per month from SEO-targeted content
- Secondary KPIs: Organic sessions, keyword rankings, time on page, scroll depth
- Guardrail: Bounce rate should not increase significantly with traffic volume growth

### Step 2 — Set Baselines and Targets
For each KPI:
- What is the current baseline? (Measured over a trailing 3-month average)
- What is the target for the next 90 days / 12 months?
- What would exceptional performance look like?

Targets must be specific and time-bound. "More organic traffic" is not a target.

### Step 3 — Build the Attribution Model
Define how content will be attributed to pipeline and revenue:

**Attribution models:**

| Model | How It Works | Best For |
|---|---|---|
| **First touch** | 100% credit to the first content touchpoint | Understanding what creates awareness |
| **Last touch** | 100% credit to the final pre-conversion touchpoint | Understanding what converts |
| **Linear** | Equal credit across all touchpoints | Balanced view of content contribution |
| **Content-influenced** | Any revenue that touched content gets flagged | Demonstrating content's presence in the pipeline |

For most content teams, a **content-influenced** model is the most practical and actionable:
- Tag all content assets with UTM parameters
- Track content consumption events in MAP/CRM
- Report on pipeline and revenue where at least one content asset was consumed

### Step 4 — Build the Reporting Framework

**Weekly content team report (operational):**
- New content published (title, channel, pillar)
- Traffic and engagement for recent content
- Email performance (open rate, CTR, conversions)
- SEO ranking movement for tracked keywords
- Social performance highlights

**Monthly content performance report (strategic):**
- Organic traffic trend (vs prior month and prior year)
- Content-attributed leads (MQLs sourced from content)
- Top performing content by organic traffic, leads, and engagement
- Pipeline influenced by content touches
- Content-to-lead conversion rate by pillar and format
- Test and experiment results
- Recommendations for the next month

**Quarterly content strategy review:**
- Performance vs targets (primary and secondary KPIs)
- Pillar-level performance: which pillars are delivering and which aren't?
- Format performance: what content formats are generating the best ROI?
- ROI assessment: cost per content-attributed lead; cost per content-influenced £ of pipeline
- Strategic recommendations: which pillars to accelerate, pause, or rebalance?

### Step 5 — Build the Content ROI Model
Define cost inputs and business outputs:

**Inputs:**
- Content team cost (salaries, prorated to content function)
- Agency and freelance spend
- Production costs (design, video, tools)
- Distribution spend (paid amplification, syndication)

**Outputs:**
- Organic sessions and leads generated
- Pipeline influenced
- Revenue attributed
- Retained customers with content touchpoints

**ROI calculation:**
> Content ROI = (Pipeline influenced × Win rate × Average deal value) − Total content investment) ÷ Total content investment

This model is imperfect — content attribution is always partial — but it provides an evidence-based case for investment relative to return.

### Step 6 — Create the Optimisation Loop
Use performance data to continuously improve:
- What content is underperforming? Why? (Optimise or retire)
- What content is overperforming? Why? (Replicate the pattern)
- Which pillars are under-producing? (Increase investment or reframe the angle)
- Which formats are most efficient? (Concentrate production there)

Set a monthly review habit: look at data, identify the top opportunity, brief one piece of content that targets it.

## Output Formats

### Content Performance Dashboard
Tracks at three levels:
1. **Business impact:** Pipeline influenced, revenue attributed, leads generated
2. **Funnel contribution:** MQL volume from content, content-to-lead conversion rate
3. **Engagement health:** Organic traffic, time on page, email engagement, social performance

### Monthly Content Report Template
- Executive summary (3–5 bullets: what changed and why it matters)
- Traffic and engagement section
- Lead generation section
- Pipeline and revenue attribution section
- Top content performers
- Underperformers and recommended actions
- Next 30-day focus

### Content ROI Model
Spreadsheet model showing:
- Total content investment (by month)
- Organic leads attributed to content
- Pipeline influenced by content
- Estimated revenue attribution
- Cost per content-attributed lead
- Content ROI %

### Quarterly Strategy Review Presentation
- KPI performance vs targets
- Pillar-by-pillar performance
- Format efficiency analysis
- Strategic recommendations for next quarter
- Budget allocation proposal based on performance

## Common Mistakes to Avoid
- **Reporting only on traffic.** Traffic without conversion data does not demonstrate content value.
- **Attribution without UTM hygiene.** If assets aren't tagged consistently, attribution breaks down immediately.
- **Measuring all content the same way.** Brand awareness content and demand generation content have fundamentally different success metrics. Apply the right framework to each.
- **No baseline before setting targets.** Without a baseline, targets are guesses and results are uninterpretable.
- **Reporting that isn't connected to decisions.** A performance report that doesn't change what gets produced next month was a reporting exercise, not a learning exercise.

---

## Quality Check
Before submitting, ask:
- [ ] Are KPIs defined at every funnel stage — not just traffic and engagement?
- [ ] Is there an attribution model connecting content to pipeline and revenue?
- [ ] Are baselines established before targets are set?
- [ ] Does the reporting framework answer the question: "Is content investment worth it?"
- [ ] Is there an optimisation loop — does performance data change what gets produced next?

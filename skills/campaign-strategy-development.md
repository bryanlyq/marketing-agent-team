# Campaign Strategy Development

## Purpose
Build integrated campaign strategies with a clear narrative, channel plan, and measurement framework — so every campaign has a defined story to tell, a structured way to tell it, and a method to know if it worked.

---

## When to Use This Skill
- Planning a major campaign (product launch, seasonal push, brand offensive)
- Rebuilding a campaign programme that lacks strategic coherence
- Briefing an agency or internal creative team
- Aligning sales and marketing on a go-to-market push
- Quarterly or annual campaign calendar planning

---

## Briefing Checklist
Before starting, confirm:
1. **Business objective** — What outcome does this campaign need to drive?
2. **Audience** — Which segment or persona is the primary target?
3. **Timeline** — What are the campaign start, peak, and end dates?
4. **Budget** — Total available budget and any channel constraints?
5. **Creative assets** — What exists? What needs to be produced?
6. **Measurement baseline** — What are the current metrics this campaign should move?

---

## Campaign Strategy Components

A complete campaign strategy has seven components. All must be defined before briefing creative or buying media.

```
1. OBJECTIVE — What the campaign must achieve
2. AUDIENCE — Who it's designed to reach and influence
3. NARRATIVE — The story or idea that unifies all executions
4. CHANNEL PLAN — Where and how the narrative is delivered
5. CONTENT PLAN — What assets are needed for each channel
6. MEASUREMENT PLAN — How success will be tracked
7. TIMELINE — When each element goes live
```

---

## Process

### Step 1 — Define the Campaign Objective
Apply the SMART framework. One primary objective, maximum two secondary objectives.

Examples by campaign type:
- **Brand awareness:** Increase unaided awareness among [segment] from X% to Y% by [date]
- **Demand generation:** Generate [X] MQLs from [segment] at <£[CPL] within [period]
- **Product launch:** Drive [X] trial sign-ups from [ICP] within 30 days of launch
- **Retention:** Reduce churn in the [cohort] segment from X% to Y% within 90 days

### Step 2 — Define the Target Audience
Don't campaign to everyone. Define:
- **Primary audience:** The segment with the highest potential return
- **Secondary audience:** A supporting segment (e.g., influencers, champions, referrers)
- **Audience insight:** What does this audience care about right now? What is their emotional state?
- **Moment of relevance:** Why should this audience pay attention to this campaign, at this time?

### Step 3 — Develop the Campaign Narrative
The narrative is the unifying idea that holds all channel executions together. It is not the tagline — it is the strategic brief that informs the tagline.

Define:
- **Campaign territory:** The emotional or intellectual space the campaign occupies
- **Core tension:** The problem or belief being challenged
- **Campaign promise:** What the audience will take away
- **Proof:** The evidence that makes the promise credible

A campaign without a narrative produces disconnected executions that cancel each other out.

### Step 4 — Build the Channel Plan
For each channel, define:
- Role in the campaign (awareness, consideration, conversion, retargeting)
- Format and creative approach
- Budget allocation
- KPI and target

| Channel | Role | Format | Budget | KPI | Target |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| LinkedIn Ads | Awareness | Video + single image | £X | CPM, reach | X impressions, X% frequency |
| Paid Search | Capture | Text ads | £X | CPC, conversion rate | X conversions at <£Y |
| Email | Nurture | 3-part sequence | £X | Open rate, CTR | X% open, X% click |
| Landing Page | Convert | Dedicated LP | £X | Conversion rate | X% |

### Step 5 — Build the Content Plan
List every asset needed for the campaign:

| Asset | Channel | Format | Owner | Due Date | Status |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Campaign hero video | Paid social, website | 60s video | Creative team | [date] | Brief due |
| Landing page | Web | Long-form LP | Web + copy | [date] | In progress |
| Email sequence (3 parts) | Email | HTML email | Content | [date] | Not started |
| LinkedIn carousel | LinkedIn | 8-card carousel | Design | [date] | Not started |

### Step 6 — Sequence the Campaign Timeline
Map the campaign across pre-launch, launch, peak, and close-out phases:

**Pre-launch (2–4 weeks before)**
- Audience warm-up (organic content, email priming)
- Asset production and QA
- Tracking and pixel verification
- Sales briefing and enablement

**Launch (Week 1–2)**
- Full channel activation
- Daily performance monitoring
- Sales outreach aligned to campaign audience

**Peak (Week 3–6)**
- Optimise: pause underperformers, scale winners
- Mid-campaign reporting
- Retargeting activation

**Close-out (Final week)**
- Scale down spend
- Retargeting to unconverted engaged audience
- Campaign capture: last push to conversion

**Post-campaign**
- Performance report vs objectives
- Lessons learned documentation
- Asset library update

### Step 7 — Define the Measurement Plan
For each objective, define:
- Primary KPI and target
- Measurement method (CRM, GA4, MAP, brand survey)
- Reporting cadence (daily during campaign; final report within 5 days of close)
- Decision rules: what triggers a mid-campaign change?

---

## Output Formats

### Campaign Strategy Document
- Executive summary (objective, audience, narrative, budget)
- Full strategy across all seven components
- Channel plan table
- Content asset list
- Campaign timeline
- Measurement plan

### Creative Brief
- Campaign narrative and territory
- Audience and insight
- Message hierarchy
- Tone and style guidance
- Asset specifications per channel
- Deadline and approval process

### Campaign Calendar
Visual timeline showing:
- All channels and their active periods
- Asset production deadlines
- Key milestones (launch, peak, close-out)
- Reporting checkpoints

### Campaign Performance Dashboard
- KPIs by channel
- Pacing vs target
- Conversion funnel metrics
- Budget vs spend

---

## Common Mistakes to Avoid
- **Campaigns without a narrative.** Disconnected executions across channels look like different campaigns and confuse the audience.
- **Too many objectives.** A campaign trying to build awareness, generate leads, and retain customers simultaneously does none of them well.
- **Channel plan before audience insight.** Choose channels after understanding where the audience is, not before.
- **No pre-launch warming.** Cold audiences converting cold messages is rare. Build before you push.
- **Measurement set up after launch.** Attribution tracking must be in place before a single ad runs.

---

## Quality Check
Before submitting, ask:
- [ ] Is there one primary campaign objective, written to SMART standard?
- [ ] Does the campaign narrative unify all channel executions into a single coherent idea?
- [ ] Does the channel plan include role, format, budget, and KPI for every channel?
- [ ] Is every required asset listed with an owner and a deadline?
- [ ] Is measurement tracking confirmed as live before the campaign launches?

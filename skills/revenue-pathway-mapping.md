# Revenue Pathway Mapping

## Purpose
Define the routes through which a customer moves from first contact to revenue — and from revenue to expansion and advocacy — so growth levers are visible, measurable, and optimisable.

---

## When to Use This Skill
- Revenue growth is unclear in its source or driver
- Diagnosing where the biggest revenue leakage is occurring
- Building a full-funnel marketing and sales strategy
- Modelling the impact of strategic changes on revenue
- Identifying expansion and retention opportunities within the existing base

---

## Briefing Checklist
Before starting, confirm:
1. **Business model** — SaaS, transactional, subscription, marketplace, professional services?
2. **Revenue streams** — New logo, expansion, renewals, services?
3. **Data availability** — CRM pipeline data, cohort data, LTV models?
4. **Primary objective** — Diagnose leakage, model growth scenarios, or design interventions?
5. **Time horizon** — Is this for quarterly planning or long-term modelling?

---

## Revenue Pathway Framework

Map revenue across five phases:

```
ACQUISITION → ACTIVATION → REVENUE → EXPANSION → ADVOCACY
```

| Phase | Definition | Revenue Lever |
|---|---|---|
| **Acquisition** | New prospects enter the funnel | Volume, CAC, channel mix |
| **Activation** | Prospect converts to customer; achieves first value | Conversion rate, time-to-value |
| **Revenue** | Recurring or transactional revenue is generated | ARPU, contract value, churn rate |
| **Expansion** | Existing customer increases spend | Upsell rate, cross-sell rate, NRR |
| **Advocacy** | Customer refers, reviews, or champions | Referral rate, review volume, PR value |

Each phase has distinct drivers, metrics, and owner responsibilities.

## Process

### Step 1 — Map Current Revenue Sources
Break down total revenue by:
- **New logo** — First-time customers
- **Expansion** — Upsell and cross-sell from existing customers
- **Renewal** — Retained recurring revenue
- **Reactivation** — Lapsed customers returning

For most mature SaaS or subscription businesses, expansion and renewal revenue should exceed new logo revenue. If it doesn't, the business is on a treadmill.

### Step 2 — Map the Acquisition Path
Trace the path from first touch to first revenue:

- What channels generate the highest quality leads?
- What is the conversion rate from lead → MQL → SQL → opportunity → won?
- What is the average time from first touch to revenue?
- What are the primary drop-off points?

Calculate:
- Blended CAC (total marketing + sales cost ÷ new customers in period)
- CAC by channel (to identify where acquisition is most efficient)
- Payback period (CAC ÷ monthly gross margin per customer)

### Step 3 — Map the Activation Path
Define what first value looks like and how quickly customers reach it:
- What is the "aha moment" — the point where the customer first gets clear value?
- What percentage of customers reach the aha moment within 30/60/90 days?
- What correlates with customers who activate quickly vs slowly?

Activation failures are often the biggest hidden revenue leakage in the funnel.

### Step 4 — Map the Expansion Path
Identify expansion vectors for existing customers:
- **Upsell** — Higher tier, more capacity, advanced features
- **Cross-sell** — Adjacent products or modules
- **Seat expansion** — More users within the same account
- **Geographic expansion** — Additional markets or regions

For each vector, document:
- Eligibility criteria (when is a customer ready to expand?)
- Trigger (what event or behaviour signals expansion opportunity?)
- Conversion rate and average uplift
- Who owns the motion (marketing, CS, or sales)?

### Step 5 — Map the Retention and Churn Path
Define the churn risk framework:
- What are the leading indicators of churn (usage decline, support tickets, missed QBRs)?
- At what point in the lifecycle does churn most commonly occur?
- What interventions reduce churn most effectively?
- What is the win-back rate for churned customers?

Calculate:
- Gross revenue retention rate (MRR/ARR retained before expansion)
- Net revenue retention rate (MRR/ARR retained after expansion and churn)
- Churn rate by cohort, segment, and acquisition channel

### Step 6 — Build the Revenue Model
Combine the pathway data into a revenue model showing:
- Volume inputs at each stage
- Conversion and retention rates
- Revenue outputs
- Growth levers and their projected impact

Model three scenarios: base, optimistic (key lever improves 20%), conservative (key lever declines 20%).

---

## Output Formats

### Revenue Flow Diagram
Visual showing:
- Acquisition funnel (leads → customers) with volumes and conversion rates
- Activation phase with time-to-value and activation rate
- Revenue base with ARPU and retention rate
- Expansion vectors with rates and average uplift
- Churn and reactivation flows

### Growth Lever Analysis
Table identifying each growth lever with:
- Current performance
- Industry benchmark (where available)
- 10% improvement scenario impact on revenue
- Effort to improve: Low / Medium / High
- Owner

### Revenue Scenario Model
Spreadsheet or table showing:
- Base case revenue trajectory (12–24 months)
- Optimistic scenario (which lever improved?)
- Conservative scenario (which lever declined?)
- Key assumptions and sensitivities

---

## Common Mistakes to Avoid
- **Focusing only on acquisition.** In subscription models, retention and expansion often generate more revenue than new logo acquisition.
- **No activation metric.** If you don't measure time-to-value, you can't optimise it.
- **Gross vs net retention confusion.** Gross retention measures churn; net retention measures whether the existing base is growing. Both matter.
- **CAC without payback period.** A £500 CAC might be great or terrible depending on the payback period and LTV.
- **Building the model before cleaning the data.** Garbage in, garbage out. Audit data quality before modelling.

## Quality Check
Before submitting, ask:
- [ ] Does the map cover all five phases: acquisition, activation, revenue, expansion, advocacy?
- [ ] Are conversion rates and revenue metrics populated with real data, not assumptions?
- [ ] Are the biggest revenue leakage points identified and ranked?
- [ ] Is there a growth lever analysis that quantifies the impact of improvement at each stage?

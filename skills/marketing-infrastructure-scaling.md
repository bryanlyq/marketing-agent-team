# Marketing Infrastructure Scaling

## Purpose
Design marketing operations to scale efficiently with business growth, build process documentation and SOPs for repeatable work, and define team structure, agency mix, and capability development plans.

---

## When to Use This Skill
- Scaling from startup to growth stage (first dedicated marketing hires)
- Rapid headcount growth creating coordination and quality problems
- Preparing for a new funding round, market expansion, or product launch
- Post-merger integration of marketing functions
- Persistent operational inefficiency despite adequate budget

---

## Briefing Checklist
Before starting, confirm:
1. **Growth stage** — Where is the business now and where does it need to be in 12–24 months?
2. **Current team** — Headcount, structure, agency vs. in-house split, and capability gaps?
3. **Operational pain points** — Where does the current infrastructure break down?
4. **Budget context** — Headcount budget, agency budget, and tools budget available?
5. **Output format** — Org design, process documentation, capability plan, or all three?

---

## Process

### Step 1 — Audit the Current Infrastructure
Assess the three components of marketing infrastructure:

**People:**
- Current team structure and reporting lines
- In-house vs. agency vs. freelance split
- Capability inventory: what skills exist and what's missing?
- Bandwidth assessment: where is the team over- or under-stretched?

**Process:**
- Which workflows are documented vs. tribal knowledge?
- Where are handoffs breaking down between team members or functions?
- What work is being repeated without a standard template?
- Where is approval or review creating bottlenecks?

**Technology:**
- See Technology Stack Evaluation skill
- Key question here: does the tech stack support the team's processes, or are people working around it?

### Step 2 — Design the Target Operating Model
Define what the marketing function needs to look like to support the next phase of growth:

**Organisational design principles:**
- Centralise brand and messaging; decentralise execution where possible
- Match team structure to go-to-market motion (product-led, sales-led, channel-led)
- Build centres of excellence for high-skill, high-leverage functions (analytics, SEO, performance)
- Keep strategy in-house; outsource execution where speed or specialist depth is needed

**Team structure options:**

| Model | When to Use |
|---|---|
| Functional (CMO → channel heads) | >10 person team; clear channel ownership needed |
| Segment/market-based | Multi-product or multi-geography; GTM differs by segment |
| Pod model (campaign teams) | High campaign velocity; cross-functional execution needed |
| Hybrid | Most common at 5–15 person stage |

### Step 3 — Define In-House vs. Agency vs. Freelance Mix
For each marketing capability, decide:

| Capability | In-House | Agency | Freelance | Rationale |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Strategy and planning | ✓ | — | — | Must not be outsourced |
| Brand and creative | — | ✓ | — | Specialist depth, flexible volume |
| Performance marketing | ✓ | — | — | Daily optimisation requires internal ownership |
| SEO | ✓ | — | ✓ | Strategy in-house; content production via freelance |
| PR and earned media | — | ✓ | — | Relationship-dependent |

### Step 4 — Build Core Process Documentation
Prioritise documenting high-frequency and high-stakes processes:

**Priority SOPs for most marketing teams:**
- Campaign briefing and approval process
- Content production workflow (brief → draft → review → publish)
- MQL qualification and handoff to sales
- Weekly and monthly reporting process
- Agency briefing and feedback process
- New hire onboarding to marketing systems

Each SOP should include:
- Process owner
- Step-by-step workflow with roles and responsibilities
- Tools used at each step
- Definition of done / quality checklist
- Escalation path for exceptions

### Step 5 — Build the Capability Development Plan
Identify skill gaps and define the plan to close them:

| Skill Gap | Business Impact | Solve Via (hire/train/agency) | Timeline |
|---|---|---|---|
| — | H/M/L | — | — |

Prioritise gaps that constrain the highest-impact initiatives on the roadmap.

### Step 6 — Define Scaling Triggers
Avoid scaling prematurely. Define the specific signals that trigger each expansion:

| Signal | Scaling Action |
|---|---|
| MQL volume exceeds team capacity | Hire demand generation manager |
| Attribution model unreliable due to channel complexity | Hire marketing analyst |
| Agency creative brief cycle >2 weeks | Bring in-house designer |
| Campaign QA errors increasing | Add campaign manager role |

## Output Formats

### Marketing Ops Framework
- Current state audit (people, process, technology)
- Target operating model
- In-house vs. agency vs. freelance design
- Core SOP library (prioritised)
- Capability development plan

### Org Design Recommendation
- Recommended structure and rationale
- Headcount plan (current → 12-month target)
- Role definitions and hiring priority
- Scaling triggers

### Process Documentation (SOPs)
- Step-by-step workflow for each priority process
- Roles and responsibilities (RACI)
- Tools and templates used
- Quality checklists

---

## Common Mistakes to Avoid
- **Scaling headcount without fixing process.** More people in a broken process creates more chaos.
- **Over-outsourcing strategy.** Agencies can execute strategy; they cannot set it. Keep strategic decision-making in-house.
- **Documenting processes that don't exist yet.** Document what you do, then improve it — not the ideal process you haven't run.
- **Hiring specialists before generalists at early stage.** A team of specialists with no generalist coordination breaks down at handoffs.
- **No scaling triggers.** Growing without criteria for when to hire or restructure leads to either under- or over-investment.

---

## Quality Check
Before submitting, ask:
- [ ] Are people, process, and technology all addressed in the assessment?
- [ ] Is the team structure matched to the go-to-market motion?
- [ ] Is the in-house vs. agency mix justified by capability and cost?
- [ ] Are the highest-frequency processes documented with clear ownership?
- [ ] Are scaling triggers defined so growth decisions are data-driven, not reactive?

# Demand Generation Strategy

## Purpose
Design a full-funnel demand programme that creates, captures, and converts demand — balancing brand-building for long-term growth with performance marketing for near-term pipeline.

---

## When to Use This Skill
- Building or rebuilding the marketing engine from scratch
- Pipeline is insufficient to meet revenue targets
- Over-reliance on one channel creating fragility
- Moving from inbound-only to a blended demand model
- Scaling demand gen with a new budget or headcount

---

## Briefing Checklist
Before starting, confirm:
1. **Revenue target** — What pipeline or revenue does demand gen need to support?
2. **ICP** — Who are we generating demand from?
3. **Current state** — What's already working? Where are the gaps?
4. **Budget** — What's the total investment available, and how is it currently allocated?
5. **Sales capacity** — How many SQLs can the sales team handle per month?
6. **Time horizon** — Is this a 90-day sprint or a 12-month programme?

---

## Demand Generation vs Lead Generation

| | Demand Generation | Lead Generation |
|---|---|---|
| **Goal** | Create and expand the market; build category awareness | Capture existing demand; collect contact information |
| **Timeframe** | Long-term brand and pipeline investment | Short-term conversion |
| **Metric** | Share of voice, unaided awareness, pipeline influence | MQLs, CPL, conversion rate |
| **Risk of imbalance** | Invisible to buyers who are ready to purchase | Pipeline dries up when demand-gen investment stops |

Best-in-class programmes do both. The Binet & Field 60/40 heuristic: 60% brand (demand creation), 40% performance (demand capture). Adjust based on category maturity and competitive intensity.

---

## Demand Waterfall

Define the stages and conversion targets before designing the programme:

```
Impressions / Reach
    └── Engaged Audience (content consumption, ad engagement)
            └── Marketing Qualified Lead (MQL)
                    └── Sales Accepted Lead (SAL)
                            └── Sales Qualified Lead (SQL)
                                    └── Opportunity
                                            └── Closed-Won Revenue
```

For each stage, define:
- Volume target
- Conversion rate assumption
- Owner (marketing or sales)
- SLA (how quickly should handoffs happen?)

Work backwards from revenue target to set MQL volume requirements.

## Process

### Step 1 — Audit the Current Demand Engine
Before building, assess what exists:
- Where does current pipeline come from?
- What is the blended CAC by channel?
- Where are the biggest conversion gaps in the funnel?
- What content assets exist? Which perform?
- What automation and nurture is in place?

### Step 2 — Define Demand Types
Not all demand is the same. Identify the mix:

| Type | Description | Programme Implication |
|---|---|---|
| **Problem-unaware** | Buyer doesn't know they have the problem | Education-led content; category marketing |
| **Solution-unaware** | Buyer knows the problem but not our solution | Brand campaigns; thought leadership |
| **Competitor-aware** | Buyer is evaluating alternatives | Comparison content; sales enablement |
| **Ready-to-buy** | High intent; actively shopping | Search capture; BDR outreach; retargeting |

### Step 3 — Design the Programme Architecture

For each demand type, assign:
- **Channels** — Where will we reach this audience?
- **Content** — What will move them to the next stage?
- **Conversion mechanism** — What action do we want them to take?
- **Nurture path** — How do we manage them until sales-ready?

### Step 4 — Set Channel Mix
Allocate across:

**Demand Creation (brand)**
- Paid social (awareness campaigns, dark social)
- Sponsorships and partnerships
- Podcast and media placements
- Thought leadership and PR
- Events and community

**Demand Capture (performance)**
- Paid search (branded + category keywords)
- SEO (high-intent organic)
- Review sites (G2, Capterra)
- Retargeting
- BDR outbound

**Demand Conversion (bottom-of-funnel)**
- Demo and trial flows
- Sales enablement content
- Case studies and proof
- Pricing and comparison pages

### Step 5 — Define MQL Criteria and SLAs
Align with sales on:
- What constitutes a marketing-qualified lead
- Lead scoring model (BANT, engagement score, ICP fit)
- Time-to-follow-up SLA from marketing to SDR/AE
- Feedback loop: how does sales communicate lead quality back to marketing?

### Step 6 — Set Measurement Framework
Define KPIs at each funnel stage:
- **Leading indicators** (pipeline created, MQL volume, engagement rate)
- **Lagging indicators** (pipeline to revenue conversion, CAC, ROI)
- Reporting cadence: weekly operational metrics; monthly strategic review

## Output Formats

### Demand Gen Strategy Document
- Situation and objective
- Demand waterfall with targets
- Programme architecture by demand type
- Channel mix and budget allocation
- Content and campaign plan
- MQL criteria and SLAs
- Measurement framework

### Funnel Architecture Diagram
Visual showing:
- Stages from impression to closed-won
- Volume at each stage
- Conversion rates (actual and target)
- Channel inputs per stage

### Channel Mix Recommendation
Table with:
- Channel
- Role in funnel (create, capture, convert)
- Budget allocation
- Primary KPI
- Rationale

### 90-Day Demand Gen Roadmap
Sequenced plan showing:
- Weeks 1–4: Foundation (ICP, messaging, tracking)
- Weeks 5–8: Programme activation (campaigns, content, automation)
- Weeks 9–12: Optimise and scale (test results, reallocation, expansion)

## Common Mistakes to Avoid
- **Over-indexing on capture, ignoring creation.** Paid search and retargeting capture existing demand — they don't create new demand.
- **MQL volume as the primary metric.** Volume without quality metrics misleads investment decisions.
- **No content for the middle funnel.** Most demand gen programmes have TOF awareness and BOF conversion content. The consideration stage is usually neglected.
- **Sales and marketing misaligned on MQL definition.** If sales doesn't trust the leads, the programme breaks.
- **Building without a measurement baseline.** You can't optimise what you didn't measure before you started.

Before submitting, ask:
- [ ] Is there a working demand waterfall with volume and conversion targets?
- [ ] Does the channel mix balance demand creation and demand capture?

- [ ] Does the content plan cover all funnel stages, including mid-funnel?
- [ ] Is there a measurement framework with leading and lagging KPIs?

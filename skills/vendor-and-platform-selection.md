# Vendor and Platform Selection

## Purpose
Define selection criteria for marketing technology, agency, and media partners, run structured evaluation processes, and build vendor management frameworks for ongoing performance oversight.

---

## When to Use This Skill
- Selecting a new marketing technology platform (MAP, CRM, analytics, etc.)
- Choosing an agency partner (creative, media, SEO, PR, demand gen)
- Reviewing and replacing an underperforming vendor or platform
- Standardising the vendor selection process across the marketing function

---

## Briefing Checklist
Before starting, confirm:
1. **What is being selected** — Technology platform, agency, or media partner?
2. **Business problem** — What need or gap is this selection solving?
3. **Stakeholders** — Who must be involved in evaluation and sign-off?
4. **Constraints** — Budget range, implementation timeline, integration requirements?
5. **Switching costs** — What is the cost and complexity of replacing the current solution?

---

## Process

### Step 1 — Define the Problem Before Evaluating Solutions
Do not start with a vendor shortlist. Start with:
- What specific problem or capability gap are we solving?
- What does success look like 12 months after implementation?
- What are the must-have requirements vs nice-to-have?
- What will this solution need to integrate with?

### Step 2 — Define Evaluation Criteria
Build a weighted scoring model. Categories and example criteria:

**For Technology Platforms:**

| Criterion | Weight |
|---|---|
| Functional fit (must-have requirements met) | 30% |
| Integration capability with existing stack | 20% |
| Ease of implementation and onboarding | 15% |
| Total cost of ownership (licence + implementation + maintenance) | 15% |
| Vendor stability and roadmap | 10% |
| Support quality and SLA | 10% |

**For Agency Partners:**

| Criterion | Weight |
|---|---|
| Relevant capability and demonstrated expertise | 30% |
| Track record in category / with similar clients | 25% |
| Cultural fit and communication style | 15% |
| Pricing and commercial model | 15% |
| Team quality (day-to-day contacts, not just pitch team) | 15% |

### Step 3 — Build a Longlist and Shortlist
Longlist: 6–10 candidates identified through:
- Internal team knowledge and recommendations
- Peer network referrals
- Analyst reports (Gartner Magic Quadrant, G2, Forrester Wave)
- LinkedIn and industry research

Shortlist: 3–4 candidates who pass an initial screening against must-have criteria.

### Step 4 — Issue an RFP (Request for Proposal)
A structured RFP enables consistent comparison. Include:
- Company and project overview
- Problem statement and objectives
- Specific questions to answer (capabilities, integrations, pricing, references)
- Evaluation criteria and process timeline
- Required deliverables (demo, proposal, commercial offer)

### Step 5 — Evaluate Shortlisted Vendors

**Structured demo:** Use a consistent scenario for all vendors, not their standard pitch.
- Provide the same use case to each
- Ask each vendor to demonstrate the same workflow
- Include day-to-day users in the evaluation, not just buyers

**Reference checks:** Speak to 2–3 existing customers who match your profile:
- What problem did you solve with this vendor?
- What has gone wrong and how did they handle it?
- Would you choose them again?

**Scoring:** Apply the weighted criteria to each vendor and produce a ranked comparison.

### Step 6 — Negotiate and Select
- Challenge the initial commercial offer — there is almost always flexibility
- Define SLAs, penalties, and exit clauses before signing
- Confirm implementation plan, timeline, and key contacts in writing
- Identify internal owner responsible for the vendor relationship

### Step 7 — Build a Vendor Management Framework
Post-selection, define:
- **Quarterly performance reviews** — Is the vendor delivering against agreed outcomes?
- **Escalation path** — Who to contact if issues aren't resolved at day-to-day level?
- **Renewal criteria** — What must the vendor achieve to earn contract renewal?
- **Exit plan** — What is the switching cost and timeline if the vendor underperforms?

---

## Output Formats

### Vendor Selection Framework
- Problem statement and requirements
- Weighted evaluation criteria
- Scoring matrix with all shortlisted vendors

### RFP Template
- Company overview
- Problem and objective statement
- Capability and integration questions
- Pricing and commercial structure questions
- Reference requirements
- Timeline and process

### Evaluation Scorecard
- Vendor × criteria matrix with scores and weighting
- Summary ranking with rationale
- Recommended selection with risk flags

### Vendor Management Charter
- Agreed KPIs and SLAs
- Review cadence and reporting requirements
- Escalation protocol
- Renewal and exit conditions

---

## Common Mistakes to Avoid
- **Evaluating solutions before defining the problem.** Vendors will define the problem for you — and their definition will favour their product.
- **Letting the pitch team differ from the delivery team.** Always meet the people who will do the actual work.
- **Selecting on price alone.** Total cost of ownership includes integration, training, migration, and management time.
- **No exit clause.** Every contract should include clear termination conditions and data portability requirements.
- **No post-selection performance framework.** A vendor without accountability is a vendor without incentive.

---

## Quality Check
Before submitting, ask:
- [ ] Is the problem statement defined before the vendor shortlist?
- [ ] Are evaluation criteria weighted and applied consistently across all vendors?
- [ ] Have references been checked with customers in similar situations?
- [ ] Are SLAs, penalties, and exit clauses defined in the contract?
- [ ] Is there a vendor management framework for post-selection accountability?

# Social Media Strategy Design

## Purpose
Define platform priorities, content approach, and engagement strategy based on where the ICP actually is — so social media investment builds a genuine audience and contributes to measurable business outcomes, not just vanity metrics.

---

## When to Use This Skill
- Social presence exists but drives no business outcomes
- Unclear which platforms to prioritise
- Content is posted without a strategy connecting it to objectives
- Building a social presence for a new brand or entering a new platform
- Scaling from founder-led social to a team-managed programme

---

## Briefing Checklist
Before starting, confirm:
1. **Audience** — Which segments and personas are the priority? Where do they spend time?
2. **Objective** — Brand awareness, lead generation, community building, talent, or all?
3. **Current state** — What platforms are active? What is performing? What isn't?
4. **Resources** — What team capacity and budget is available for social?
5. **Tone** — What is the brand voice? Is the brand comfortable with personality and directness?

---

## Platform Selection Principle

**Don't be on every platform. Be excellent on the right ones.**

Platform selection follows audience presence — not platform popularity, team comfort, or competitor behaviour. A B2B SaaS brand has no strategic reason to be on TikTok unless the ICP is there. A DTC consumer brand has limited reason to invest in LinkedIn.

---

## Platform Profiles

### LinkedIn
- **Audience:** B2B professionals, decision-makers, executives, practitioners
- **Best for:** Thought leadership, demand creation, professional brand, hiring
- **Content that works:** Long-form posts with strong POV; carousels; native documents; video with captions
- **Organic reach:** Relatively strong compared to other platforms; algorithm rewards engagement velocity
- **Paid:** Best B2B targeting in social; high CPM but precise audience control

### Instagram
- **Audience:** Consumer, visual-led, lifestyle, 18–45
- **Best for:** Brand storytelling, community, DTC products, visual-led B2B
- **Content that works:** Reels (reach), carousel posts (engagement), Stories (relationship), UGC
- **Organic reach:** Declining for feed; strong for Reels
- **Paid:** Strong for consumer; visual creative quality is critical

### TikTok
- **Audience:** 18–34 primarily; growing older demographic
- **Best for:** Consumer brand building, entertainment, native creators
- **Content that works:** Authentic, entertaining short video; trends; tutorials
- **Organic reach:** Highest of any platform for new accounts; discovery-driven algorithm
- **Paid:** Strong reach; lower intent than search

### X (Twitter)
- **Audience:** Tech, media, finance, politics; engaged practitioners and commentators
- **Best for:** Thought leadership, real-time conversation, tech/fintech/media brands
- **Content that works:** Threads, commentary, hot takes, timely reactions, community engagement
- **Organic reach:** Volatile; rewards consistency and engagement
- **Paid:** Limited utility for most; useful for content amplification

### YouTube
- **Audience:** Broad; strongest for how-to, education, product, and entertainment
- **Best for:** SEO-driven content, product demos, long-form brand building, tutorials
- **Content that works:** Educational, entertaining, or documentary; SEO-optimised titles
- **Organic reach:** SEO-driven; compounding over time like blog content
- **Paid:** Pre-roll and in-feed for remarketing

### Facebook
- **Audience:** 35–65 primarily; strong community infrastructure
- **Best for:** Paid media (targeting), community groups, local/regional brands
- **Content that works:** Video, groups, events
- **Organic reach:** Very low for pages; strong for groups
- **Paid:** Strong targeting; declining organic utility

## Process

### Step 1 — Audience-to-Platform Mapping
For each ICP segment, identify:
- Which platforms are they actively on?
- Where do they consume industry content?
- Where do they have professional conversations?
- What type of content do they engage with on each platform?

Conduct this research via customer interviews, social listening, and review of where competitors' audiences engage.

### Step 2 — Set Platform Priorities
Based on audience mapping, assign each platform to a tier:

| Tier | Definition | Investment |
|---|---|---|
| **Tier 1 (Core)** | ICP is present; platform drives business outcomes | Regular cadence; paid support; dedicated resource |
| **Tier 2 (Support)** | ICP is present but secondary; occasional opportunity | Lighter cadence; repurposed content from Tier 1 |
| **Tier 3 (Monitor)** | ICP not strongly present or platform not proven | No active investment; monitor for change |

A startup should have 1–2 Tier 1 platforms. Even a well-resourced team should have no more than 2–3.

### Step 3 — Define Objectives and KPIs by Platform
For each Tier 1 platform:

| Objective | Platform KPI | Business KPI |
|---|---|---|
| Brand awareness | Impressions, reach, follower growth | Branded search volume, share of voice |
| Demand creation | Engagement rate, saves, profile visits | MQL influence, demo attribution |
| Lead generation | Link clicks, form completions | Leads, CPL |
| Community | Comment rate, DM volume, group activity | NPS, retention, referrals |

Set specific targets. "Build a LinkedIn presence" is not a KPI.

### Step 4 — Build the Content Strategy per Platform

For each Tier 1 platform, define:
- **Content pillars:** Which of the brand's content pillars apply on this platform?
- **Content mix:** What percentage of posts are educational / entertaining / promotional / engaging?
- **Format mix:** What formats will be used? (Text, video, carousel, stories)
- **Cadence:** How many posts per week at a quality standard the team can sustain?
- **Voice and tone:** How does the brand voice adapt for this platform?
- **Engagement protocol:** How quickly will the brand respond to comments and DMs?

### Step 5 — Define the Organic vs Paid Balance
Organic social builds relationships; paid social builds reach. Define for each platform:
- Is this platform primarily organic or paid?
- What organic content will be amplified with paid?
- What paid-only content will be run (e.g., dark posts for lead gen)?

### Step 6 — Build the Community Engagement Protocol
Social is not a broadcast channel. Define:
- Who monitors mentions, comments, and DMs?
- What is the response time SLA?
- What types of comments get a reply vs a like?
- How are negative comments or complaints handled?
- How does the brand engage proactively (commenting on others' content, joining conversations)?

## Output Formats

### Social Media Strategy Document
- Platform prioritisation and rationale
- Objectives and KPIs by platform
- Content strategy per platform (pillars, mix, cadence, voice)
- Organic vs paid approach
- Community engagement protocol
- Measurement framework

### Platform Playbook (per platform)
- Audience profile on this platform
- Content format guide
- Publishing cadence
- Hashtag and SEO guidance
- Engagement SLA
- Performance benchmarks

### Social Content Framework
- Content pillars for social
- Content ratio (educational : entertaining : promotional)
- Format options and production requirements
- Repurposing guide (how to adapt from primary content to social-native formats)

### Monthly Social Calendar
- Content pieces by platform by week
- Format and pillar tagging
- Campaign alignment
- Owner and status

## Common Mistakes to Avoid
- **Being on every platform.** Spreading effort across six platforms produces mediocrity on all of them. Excellence on two beats presence on six.
- **Same content on every platform.** A LinkedIn post shared directly to Instagram performs poorly on both. Native adaptation is non-optional.
- **Measuring followers over engagement.** Follower count is vanity. Engagement rate and business influence are what matter.
- **Social without a POV.** "Sharing helpful content" is not a strategy. Define what the brand stands for and what it will say that others won't.
- **No engagement protocol.** Social media with no active monitoring or response is a reputation risk, not an asset.

## Quality Check
Before submitting, ask:
- [ ] Is platform selection based on where the ICP is — not where the team is comfortable?
- [ ] Are Tier 1 platforms limited to 1–2 so quality can be maintained?
- [ ] Are objectives and KPIs defined per platform, connecting platform metrics to business outcomes?
- [ ] Is there a content adaptation plan for each platform (not just copy/paste)?
- [ ] Is there a community engagement protocol with a defined response SLA?

# Offer Strategy Development

## Purpose
Structure commercial offers to maximise perceived value, reduce friction, and increase conversion — so the right buyer says yes at the right moment in the funnel.

---

## When to Use This Skill
- Conversion rates are low despite strong traffic and lead quality
- Launching a new product, tier, or pricing model
- Designing a free trial, freemium, or proof-of-concept mechanic
- Building a campaign offer or seasonal promotion
- Restructuring existing offers to improve upsell paths

---

## Briefing Checklist
Before starting, confirm:
1. **Audience** — Who is this offer for? What stage of the buyer journey are they at?
2. **Objective** — Is this offer designed to acquire, activate, convert, expand, or retain?
3. **Product context** — What is being offered, and what's its current price and structure?
4. **Competitive context** — What offers do competitors use? What's the category norm?
5. **Constraints** — Margin floor, legal restrictions, sales team capacity, technology limits?

---

## Offer Components

Every offer has five dimensions. All must be aligned:

| Dimension | Question |
|---|---|
| **What** | What exactly is included? What is excluded? |
| **For whom** | Which segment, persona, or funnel stage? |
| **At what price** | What is the monetary cost — or is it free? |
| **With what terms** | Commitment, contract length, refund policy, risk reversal? |
| **Why now** | What creates urgency or specificity of timing? |

Weakness in any one dimension undermines the whole offer.

## Offer Mechanics

### Tiering and Anchoring
- Present 3 tiers: entry, core, premium (the "Goldilocks" structure)
- The premium tier anchors perception of value upward; most buyers select the middle
- Freemium or trial tier can anchor price downward and increase funnel volume
- Avoid too many tiers — complexity kills conversion

### Bundling
- Bundle complementary products or services to increase perceived value
- Bundling reduces direct price comparison with competitors
- Avoid bundling unrelated items — it dilutes the core value

### Trial and Risk Reversal
- Free trial reduces risk for the buyer and lowers the activation barrier
- Time-limited trials create urgency to evaluate
- Money-back guarantees shift perceived risk from buyer to seller
- POC/pilot mechanics work for high-ticket B2B where risk must be managed before full commitment

### Urgency and Scarcity
- Time-based: "Offer expires [date]" — only credible if the deadline is real
- Volume-based: "Limited to [X] customers" — only credible if the constraint is genuine
- Event-based: pricing changes on launch, at a conference, after a quarter ends
- False urgency destroys trust. Only create urgency that is real.

### Commitment Mechanics
- Annual vs monthly pricing: incentivise annual with a discount (typically 15–25%)
- Upfront payment discount reduces churn risk and improves cash flow
- Multi-year contracts for enterprise: trade lower ACV for longer commitment

## Process

### Step 1 — Define the Offer Objective
Each offer serves a specific purpose. Be explicit:
- **Acquisition offer** — Designed to get first-time buyers to say yes (low barrier, proof-of-value)
- **Activation offer** — Designed to move trial/freemium users to paid (remove the upgrade friction)
- **Expansion offer** — Designed to increase spend from existing customers (upsell, cross-sell)
- **Retention offer** — Designed to prevent churn or win back lapsed customers

Don't design one offer to do all of these at once.

### Step 2 — Audit Current Offers and Conversion Data
- What offers exist today? Where in the funnel?
- What are the conversion rates at each offer stage?
- Where do buyers stall, object, or abandon?
- What objections come up in sales calls when offers are presented?

### Step 3 — Map Perceived Value vs Perceived Risk
Buyers convert when perceived value > perceived risk + perceived price.

Levers to pull:
- **Increase perceived value:** Add relevant inclusions, strengthen the headline benefit, add social proof
- **Reduce perceived risk:** Trial, guarantee, case studies, pilot option
- **Reduce perceived price:** Reframe the unit (per day, per outcome), anchoring, payment plans

### Step 4 — Design Offer Variants
Build 2–3 offer structures for the primary conversion moment. Include:
- Tier structure and what each includes
- Pricing and terms
- Risk reversal mechanism
- Urgency mechanic (if applicable)
- Supporting copy and proof

### Step 5 — Define the Test Plan
- Which offer variant is the control?
- What is the primary metric? (Conversion rate, average order value, trial-to-paid rate)
- What sample size and duration is needed?
- What result determines the winning offer?

## Output Formats

### Offer Strategy Document
- Offer objective and target audience
- Value vs risk analysis
- Proposed tier structure
- Pricing and terms
- Risk reversal and urgency mechanics
- Supporting proof requirements
- Test plan

### Pricing Page Wireframe Brief
- Tier names and what each includes
- Feature comparison matrix
- Recommended "most popular" callout
- CTA copy and placement
- FAQ content (addresses top conversion objections)

### A/B Test Brief
- Hypothesis
- Control vs variant description
- Primary and secondary metrics
- Sample size and duration
- Decision criteria

## Common Mistakes to Avoid
- **Offers designed for the business, not the buyer.** An offer should solve the buyer's hesitation, not the business's revenue problem.
- **Too many options.** Choice paralysis is real. Three tiers maximum for most offers.
- **Urgency that isn't real.** Fake countdown timers and false scarcity destroy trust and long-term conversion.
- **No risk reversal.** Especially in a new category or for a high-ticket offer, buyers need risk removed before they commit.
- **Offer disconnected from the buyer stage.** A heavy enterprise offer presented to a cold prospect creates friction. A light trial offer presented to a warm, qualified prospect leaves money on the table.

## Quality Check
Before submitting, ask:
- [ ] Is the offer objective clearly defined (acquire, activate, expand, or retain)?
- [ ] Does the offer address the buyer's primary risk or hesitation?
- [ ] Is the pricing and tier structure clear and non-confusing?
- [ ] Is there a risk reversal mechanism?
- [ ] Is there a test plan to validate the offer before full rollout?

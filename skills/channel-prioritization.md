# Channel Prioritisation

## Purpose
Evaluate and rank marketing channels by their strategic fit, cost efficiency, and audience reach — so budget and team attention go to channels that genuinely move the needle, not the ones that feel safe or familiar.

---

## When to Use This Skill
- Setting or revisiting the annual marketing channel mix
- CAC is too high and efficiency needs to improve
- Launching into a new market or segment with a new audience
- Budget has been cut and priorities must be forced
- Current channel mix is over-concentrated and fragile

---

## Briefing Checklist
Before starting, confirm:
1. **Audience** — Where does the ICP actually spend time and seek information?
2. **Stage** — Are we prioritising for awareness, consideration, or conversion?
3. **Budget constraints** — What is the total available budget across channels?
4. **Current mix** — What channels are active today, at what spend, and with what results?
5. **Team capacity** — What skills and bandwidth exist to manage new channels?

---

## The Channel Selection Principle

**Start with the audience. End with the channel.**

Never choose a channel because it's trending, because a competitor uses it, or because the team is comfortable with it. Choose because it's where the ICP is — and where you can reach them at a cost that makes the acquisition economics work.

---

## Channel Evaluation Criteria

Score each channel candidate across five dimensions:

| Criterion | Description | Weight |
|---|---|---|
| **Audience fit** | Is the ICP present and reachable on this channel? | High |
| **Intent level** | Does this channel reach buyers at high purchase intent? | High |
| **Cost efficiency** | What is the expected CPL and cost-per-SQL? | High |
| **Competitive density** | How saturated is the channel with competitor activity? | Medium |
| **Scalability** | Can spend or effort increase without proportional CPL increase? | Medium |
| **Speed to result** | How quickly does this channel produce leads or pipeline? | Medium |
| **Team capability** | Can the team run this channel at a high standard? | Low |

Weight criteria based on the strategic context. A startup optimising for speed weights differently to an established business optimising for efficiency.

## Channel Categories and Profiles

### Paid Search (Google, Bing)
- **Best for:** Capturing high-intent demand; direct response
- **Strengths:** Intent targeting, immediate results, measurable
- **Weaknesses:** Expensive in competitive categories; no demand creation
- **CAC profile:** Medium–High; scales but with diminishing returns
- **Stage:** BOFU (conversion)

### Paid Social (LinkedIn, Meta, TikTok)
- **Best for:** Demand creation; brand awareness; targeted prospecting
- **Strengths:** Audience targeting precision; creative storytelling
- **Weaknesses:** Low direct intent; rising CPMs; difficult attribution
- **CAC profile:** Medium; higher in B2B on LinkedIn
- **Stage:** TOFU–MOFU (awareness and consideration)

### SEO and Organic Content
- **Best for:** Long-term inbound volume; authority building
- **Strengths:** Compounding returns; low marginal cost at scale
- **Weaknesses:** Slow to build; algorithm-dependent; requires sustained investment
- **CAC profile:** Low (at scale); high opportunity cost in early stages
- **Stage:** TOFU–MOFU

### Email Marketing
- **Best for:** Nurturing known contacts; lifecycle and retention
- **Strengths:** Low cost; owned channel; measurable; personalisation
- **Weaknesses:** Limited to existing database; deliverability constraints
- **CAC profile:** Very low for existing database
- **Stage:** MOFU–BOFU

### Events and Conferences
- **Best for:** Pipeline generation; brand credibility; enterprise relationships
- **Strengths:** High-quality conversations; relationship acceleration
- **Weaknesses:** High cost; difficult attribution; hard to scale
- **CAC profile:** High; but pipeline quality often higher
- **Stage:** MOFU–BOFU

### Referral and Partnerships
- **Best for:** High-quality low-cost acquisition
- **Strengths:** High trust; strong LTV:CAC; word-of-mouth compounding
- **Weaknesses:** Slow to build; unpredictable volume
- **CAC profile:** Very low; but limited control over volume
- **Stage:** BOFU

### Review Sites (G2, Capterra, Trustpilot)
- **Best for:** High-intent B2B buyers in evaluation mode
- **Strengths:** Captures active evaluators; builds credibility
- **Weaknesses:** Competitive visibility; review acquisition required
- **CAC profile:** Low–Medium; depends on category density
- **Stage:** BOFU

### Outbound SDR
- **Best for:** ABM; targeted account pursuit; creating demand in accounts with no inbound
- **Strengths:** Precise targeting; relationship-led
- **Weaknesses:** High cost; quality dependent on list and messaging
- **CAC profile:** High; but strong for enterprise ICP
- **Stage:** TOFU–MOFU for cold accounts

### PR and Earned Media
- **Best for:** Brand authority; category credibility; SEO domain authority
- **Strengths:** High credibility signal; compounding SEO value
- **Weaknesses:** Not directly measurable; unpredictable; slow
- **CAC profile:** Indirect; difficult to attribute directly
- **Stage:** TOFU

## Process

### Step 1 — Map ICP to Channels
For each ICP segment, identify:
- Where do they consume information?
- Where do they seek solutions?
- Which channels do they trust?
- Which channels do competitors use to reach them?

Don't assume. Use customer interview data, sales call insights, and buyer journey research.

### Step 2 — Audit Current Channel Performance
For each active channel:
- Monthly lead/MQL volume
- CPL and cost-per-SQL
- MQL → SQL conversion rate
- LTV:CAC by channel
- Trend: improving, stable, or declining?

### Step 3 — Score and Rank All Candidate Channels
Build a scoring matrix:

| Channel | Audience Fit | Intent Level | Cost Efficiency | Competitive Density | Scalability | Speed | Team Capability | Total Score |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Paid Search | 8 | 9 | 6 | 5 | 7 | 9 | 9 | Weighted total |
| LinkedIn Ads | 9 | 5 | 5 | 6 | 8 | 8 | 7 | ... |
| SEO | 8 | 7 | 9 | 6 | 9 | 3 | 7 | ... |

Score 1–10 per criterion. Apply weighting. Rank by weighted total.

### Step 4 — Build the Recommended Mix
Allocate budget and effort across:
- **Tier 1 (Invest):** 2–3 channels with highest score; receive majority of budget
- **Tier 2 (Maintain):** 2–3 channels with solid performance; receive steady investment
- **Tier 3 (Test):** 1–2 emerging or unvalidated channels; small budget for pilot

### Step 5 — Set Channel Caps and Triggers
For each channel:
- Maximum budget before expected CPL degradation
- Trigger to scale up (CPL below target; volume below target)
- Trigger to scale back (CPL above target; quality declining)

---

## Output Formats

### Channel Priority Matrix
Ranked table with scores, budget allocation, and rationale.

### Channel Mix Recommendation
- Tier 1/2/3 assignment
- Budget allocation by channel
- Volume and CPL expectations
- 90-day activation plan

### Channel Testing Roadmap
For Tier 3 pilots:
- Channel to test
- Hypothesis
- Budget and duration
- Success criteria
- Decision: scale, maintain, or kill

---

## Common Mistakes to Avoid
- **Channel-first thinking.** Never choose channels before defining the audience.
- **Equal budget across all channels.** Diversification for its own sake dilutes returns. Concentrate in what works.
- **Over-indexing on comfort.** Choosing channels the team already knows — not channels the audience uses.
- **CPL as the only metric.** A channel with a high CPL and high LTV might outperform a low-CPL channel with poor retention.
- **No Tier 3 / test budget.** Without testing, the channel mix never evolves.

---

## Quality Check
Before submitting, ask:
- [ ] Is every channel evaluated against the ICP, not just past performance?
- [ ] Is the scoring weighted by what matters most in the current strategic context?
- [ ] Is there a tier structure that concentrates investment rather than spreading it evenly?
- [ ] Is there a channel testing roadmap for emerging channels?
- [ ] Are channel caps defined to prevent over-saturation?

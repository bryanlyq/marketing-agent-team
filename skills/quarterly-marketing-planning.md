# Quarterly Marketing Planning

## Purpose
Run structured quarterly planning processes — review, reset, roadmap, and resource — to align quarterly plans to annual strategy and OKRs with contingency plans for underperformance scenarios.

---

## When to Use This Skill
- At the start of each quarter (planning)
- At the end of each quarter (review and reset)
- When annual strategy shifts require plan adjustment mid-year
- When preparing quarterly updates for leadership or investors

---

## Briefing Checklist
Before starting, confirm:
1. **Quarter in scope** — Which quarter is being planned?
2. **Annual OKRs** — What are the full-year targets marketing is accountable for?
3. **Prior quarter results** — What was achieved and what wasn't in Q-1?
4. **Resource inputs** — Budget, headcount, and any new capabilities available this quarter?
5. **Business context** — Any product launches, events, or business changes shaping the quarter?

---

## Process

### Phase 1 — Review (Prior Quarter)
Complete before planning the next quarter:

**Results vs targets:**

| OKR / KPI | Q-1 Target | Q-1 Actual | Variance | Root Cause |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| — | — | — | — | — |

**What worked:** Initiatives, channels, or tactics that outperformed — and why.

**What didn't:** Initiatives that underperformed — and the root cause, not just the symptom.

**Carry-forward learnings:** What changes should the next quarter's plan incorporate based on these learnings?

### Phase 2 — Reset (Context for Q)
Update understanding of the strategic context:
- Has the annual target changed?
- Are there new business priorities, product launches, or market developments?
- Has competitive activity shifted?
- What pipeline position are we entering the quarter with?

### Phase 3 — Roadmap (Quarter Plan)

#### Define Quarterly OKRs
Translate annual targets into quarterly commitments:

| Objective | Key Result | Target | Owner |
|---|---|---|---|
| Grow qualified pipeline | MQL volume | — | — |
| Improve conversion efficiency | Trial-to-paid rate | —% | — |
| Build brand in [segment] | Share of voice / brand search volume | — | — |

Limit to 3–5 key results. More is not more — it's confusion.

#### Define Initiatives by Priority Tier
**Tier 1 — Must complete (non-negotiable for target achievement)**
- List 3–5 initiatives with owners and completion criteria

**Tier 2 — Should complete (high value if capacity allows)**
- List 2–4 initiatives

**Tier 3 — Nice to have (only if Tier 1 and 2 are done)**
- List 1–2 initiatives

This tiering prevents everything becoming urgent and allows capacity management.

#### Build the Campaign and Activity Calendar
Map each initiative to the quarter by week:
- Campaign launch dates and live periods
- Content publication schedule
- Events, webinars, and field activities
- Reporting and review cadence

### Phase 4 — Resource (Budget and Capacity)

#### Budget Allocation
| Initiative / Channel | Q Budget | % of Total |
|---|---|---|
| — | — | —% |

Flag:
- Initiatives that are under-resourced relative to their target
- Budget that is uncommitted and available for reallocation mid-quarter

#### Capacity Planning
For each team member:
- Which Tier 1 initiatives do they own?
- What % of their time does this represent?
- Are there capacity conflicts to resolve?

Identify resource gaps and flag agency or freelance needs.

### Contingency Planning
Define the early warning triggers and response plan:

| Scenario | Trigger | Response |
|---|---|---|
| Pipeline below target at week 4 | MQL volume <70% of plan | Activate additional paid spend; review lead quality |
| Key campaign underperforms | Conversion rate <50% of benchmark | Pause and reassign budget; run test variants |
| Resource gap (illness, departure) | Key owner unavailable | Identify backup; descope to Tier 1 only |

---

## Output Formats

### Quarterly Marketing Plan Document
- Prior quarter review (results, learnings, carry-forwards)
- Q context reset (business priorities, pipeline position)
- Quarterly OKRs with targets and owners
- Initiative list (Tier 1/2/3)
- Campaign calendar
- Budget allocation
- Contingency triggers

### OKR Framework (condensed)
- 3–5 quarterly objectives with 1–3 key results each
- Owner per key result
- Measurement method and data source

### Q Review Presentation (leadership)
- Prior quarter scorecard
- Top 3 learnings
- Next quarter plan on one page
- Budget request / reallocation if required

---

## Common Mistakes to Avoid
- **Planning without reviewing.** Last quarter's learnings must inform this quarter's plan.
- **Too many OKRs.** If everything is a priority, nothing is. Limit to what can realistically move the needle.
- **No contingency plan.** Markets change — build decision triggers before you need them.
- **Budget allocated to initiatives without clear owners.** Money without a name attached to it doesn't move.
- **Quarterly plan disconnected from annual strategy.** Every quarterly initiative should trace to an annual objective.

---

## Quality Check
Before submitting, ask:
- [ ] Does the plan open with learnings from the prior quarter?
- [ ] Do quarterly OKRs add up to the annual target trajectory?
- [ ] Is every initiative in Tier 1 or 2 owned and resourced?
- [ ] Is there a contingency plan with specific triggers and responses?
- [ ] Would a team member reading this plan know exactly what to do on day one of the quarter?

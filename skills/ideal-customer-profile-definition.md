# Ideal Customer Profile (ICP) Definition

## Purpose
Define the specific type of customer most likely to buy, stay, expand, and advocate — so that sales and marketing effort concentrates on the highest-probability, highest-value opportunities.

---

## When to Use This Skill
- Go-to-market planning for a new product or market
- Sales and marketing misalignment on who to target
- High CAC or low conversion rates suggesting poor-fit targeting
- Preparing for outbound campaigns or ABM programmes
- Scaling a sales team that needs qualifying criteria

---

## Briefing Checklist
Before starting, confirm:
1. **Business objective** — Is this for acquisition targeting, ABM, or qualification?
2. **Data access** — Can we analyse closed-won deals, churn, and expansion accounts?
3. **Sales intelligence** — What patterns do AEs and SDRs observe in best-fit accounts?
4. **Current ICP** — Does one exist? What's working or failing about it?
5. **Output use** — Will this be used for scoring, outbound targeting, or messaging alignment?

---

## ICP vs Persona: The Distinction

| | ICP | Persona |
|---|---|---|
| **What it defines** | The type of *organisation or customer* to target | The *individual* within that organisation |
| **Used for** | Targeting, qualification, territory planning | Messaging, content, sales communication |
| **Primary question** | "Should we pursue this account?" | "How do we communicate with this person?" |
| **Variables** | Firmographic, behavioural, situational | Demographic, psychographic, role-based |

Build the ICP first. Personas layer on top.

---

## Process

### Step 1 — Mine Closed-Won Data
Analyse your best existing customers. Best = highest LTV, fastest time-to-value, lowest support burden, highest NPS, most likely to expand and refer.

For each account, capture:
- Industry / vertical
- Company size (employees, revenue)
- Geography
- Growth stage (startup, scale-up, enterprise, public)
- Technology stack and integrations used
- Buying trigger (what prompted them to look for a solution?)
- Time to close
- Deal size
- Expansion trajectory
- NPS or satisfaction score

### Step 2 — Identify Patterns in Best-Fit Customers
Look for clusters across:
- Firmographic variables (industry, size, stage)
- Situational variables (tech stack, team structure, growth rate)
- Behavioural variables (how they found you, how they evaluated, what tipped the decision)
- Outcome variables (fast adoption, high expansion, strong retention)

### Step 3 — Validate with Sales Intelligence
Interview AEs, CSMs, and SDRs. Ask:
- Which customers close fastest and with least friction?
- Which customers expand and refer?
- Which customers churn or cause support escalations?
- What signals in discovery tell you this is a good fit or bad fit?

### Step 4 — Define the ICP
Document across four dimensions:

**Firmographic Fit**
- Industry/vertical (be specific — not "tech" but "SaaS companies in fintech")
- Revenue range
- Employee count
- Geography
- Growth stage

**Situational Fit**
- Current tools in the category
- Team structure or capability maturity
- Business trigger (growth event, pain point, regulatory change)
- Budget cycle and procurement pattern

**Behavioural Fit**
- How they typically find solutions
- Evaluation behaviour (trial, demo, procurement)
- Decision-making structure (champion-led, committee, top-down)

**Outcome Fit**
- What success looks like for them
- Time horizon for value realisation
- Expansion potential

### Step 5 — Define Negative ICP
Explicitly state who is not a fit. This is as valuable as defining the ICP itself.
- Size too small or too large
- Industry with structural misfit
- Situations where your solution underperforms
- Buying patterns incompatible with your GTM model

### Step 6 — Build Scoring Criteria
Convert the ICP into a scoring rubric for SDRs, AEs, and marketing ops:
- 3-5 must-have criteria (disqualifying if absent)
- 3-5 strong-fit signals (weight positively)
- 3-5 red flags (weight negatively)

---

## Output Formats

### ICP Definition Document
- ICP summary (1 paragraph, plain language)
- Firmographic profile table
- Situational triggers and context
- Behavioural patterns
- Outcome expectations
- Negative ICP criteria

### ICP Scoring Rubric
- Criteria table with weighting
- Threshold score for MQL/SQL qualification
- Instructions for CRM field mapping

### ICP Validation Brief
- Methodology (data sources, sample size)
- Key patterns identified
- Confidence level and gaps
- Recommended refresh cadence

---

## Common Mistakes to Avoid
- **ICP that's too broad.** "Mid-market B2B companies" is not an ICP.
- **ICP built on aspiration, not data.** Define who actually buys and succeeds, not who you wish would.
- **No negative ICP.** Without exclusion criteria, the ICP doesn't help qualification.
- **ICP never refreshed.** As the product evolves, the ICP shifts. Review quarterly.
- **ICP not connected to scoring.** An ICP that doesn't change how leads are qualified has no operational value.

---

## Quality Check
Before submitting, ask:
- [ ] Is the ICP based on closed-won and retained customer data, not assumptions?
- [ ] Are firmographic, situational, and behavioural dimensions all represented?
- [ ] Is there a negative ICP that disqualifies clearly poor-fit accounts?
- [ ] Can an SDR use this to qualify a prospect in under 5 minutes?
- [ ] Does the ICP connect to a scoring model in the CRM?

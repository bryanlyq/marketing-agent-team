# Buyer Journey Mapping

## Purpose
Map the path a buyer takes from problem awareness to purchase decision — and beyond — so that marketing, content, and sales can show up at the right moment with the right message.

---

## When to Use This Skill
- Diagnosing why conversion rates are low at a specific funnel stage
- Building a content or channel strategy
- Aligning sales and marketing on handoff points
- Designing a new demand generation programme
- Launching a product into a new segment or category

---

## Briefing Checklist
Before starting, confirm:
1. **Segment** — Which buyer are we mapping? A journey built for everyone maps no one.
2. **Product/use case** — What specific purchase decision does this journey lead to?
3. **Data availability** — Do we have session data, sales call recordings, survey data to inform this?
4. **Purpose** — Is this for content planning, CRO, sales enablement, or all three?
5. **Existing map** — Does one exist? What's anecdotal vs evidenced?

---

## Journey Stages

Use the framework appropriate to the business model. Default framework:

| Stage | What's Happening | Buyer Question |
|---|---|---|
| **Unaware** | No recognition of the problem or category | — |
| **Aware** | Recognises the problem; begins casual exploration | "Is this worth solving?" |
| **Considering** | Actively evaluating options and alternatives | "What are my choices?" |
| **Deciding** | Comparing shortlisted options; building internal case | "Which is the best fit?" |
| **Purchasing** | Completing the transaction or contract | "How do I make this happen?" |
| **Onboarding** | Early adoption and value realisation | "Did I make the right choice?" |
| **Expanding** | Growing usage, upgrading, or adding seats | "How do I get more value?" |
| **Advocating** | Referring, reviewing, or championing | "Who else should know about this?" |

For B2B with complex sales cycles, map the buying committee, not just the champion.

## Process

### Step 1 — Define the Buyer
Specify which segment and persona this journey maps. A VP of Engineering in a Series B startup has a different journey than a procurement manager in an enterprise.

### Step 2 — Map Jobs, Triggers, and Emotions at Each Stage

For each stage, document:
- **Job to be done** — What is the buyer trying to accomplish?
- **Trigger** — What event or condition moves them to this stage?
- **Questions** — What are they asking? (Search queries are gold here)
- **Information sources** — Where do they look for answers?
- **Emotional state** — Anxious? Curious? Pressured? Overwhelmed?
- **Barriers** — What slows or stops their progress?
- **Moments of truth** — Which interactions most influence their decision?

### Step 3 — Identify Current Touchpoints
For each stage, audit where the business currently shows up:
- Owned: website, blog, email, product
- Earned: press, reviews, word of mouth, analyst coverage
- Paid: search ads, social ads, sponsorships

Map gaps — stages where the business is absent or underperforming.

### Step 4 — Identify Friction Points
Look for evidence of buyers stalling or dropping:
- High bounce rates on key pages
- Low open rates on nurture sequences
- Long time-in-stage in CRM
- Objections repeated in sales calls
- Negative review themes

### Step 5 — Map Recommended Interventions
For each gap or friction point, define:
- What content or experience is needed
- Which channel should deliver it
- What outcome it should drive (next-stage progression)
- How to measure its effectiveness

---

## Output Formats

### Journey Map (Visual)
Lane-based diagram showing:
- Stage across the top
- Buyer jobs, questions, and emotions in rows
- Current touchpoints
- Gaps and friction points
- Recommended interventions

### Touchpoint Audit
Table of all current touchpoints by stage, with:
- Channel
- Content/asset
- Performance data (if available)
- Assessment: fit, gap, or redundant

### JTBD Framework
For each stage:
- Functional job (what they're trying to do)
- Emotional job (how they want to feel)
- Social job (how they want to be perceived)
- Progress-making trigger
- Success metric from the buyer's perspective

### Sales Enablement Map
For B2B: maps buyer stage to sales stage, with:
- Buyer questions at each stage
- Recommended content and tools for sales to use
- Handoff triggers between SDR, AE, and CSM

---

## Common Mistakes to Avoid
- **Generic journeys.** One journey for all buyers obscures the real patterns. Segment first.
- **Building from assumptions.** Use call recordings, search data, and customer interviews — not instinct.
- **Stopping at purchase.** The post-purchase journey drives retention, expansion, and advocacy. Don't omit it.
- **Focusing on tactics, not jobs.** The journey is about what the buyer needs, not what the business wants to say.
- **Journey without ownership.** Every stage needs a team responsible for improving it.

---

## Quality Check
Before submitting, ask:
- [ ] Is the journey specific to a named segment and persona?
- [ ] Does every stage include the buyer's question, emotional state, and information sources?
- [ ] Have friction points been identified using data, not assumption?
- [ ] Is there a recommended intervention for each gap?
- [ ] Does the map extend through post-purchase into retention and advocacy?

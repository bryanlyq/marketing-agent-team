# Thought Leadership Positioning

## Purpose
Define an owned intellectual territory and build a thought leadership programme that builds category authority, earns audience trust, and differentiates the brand from competitors who say the same things.

---

## When to Use This Skill
- Brand is perceived as interchangeable with competitors
- Company has genuine expertise but isn't visible in category conversations
- Building credibility in a new market or with a new audience
- Establishing a founder, executive, or brand as a category voice
- Long-term SEO and content authority strategy

---

## Briefing Checklist
Before starting, confirm:
1. **Spokesperson(s)** — Who is the thought leader? Brand, founder, executive, or team?
2. **Audience** — Who needs to see this leadership and what do they currently think?
3. **Category context** — What conversations are already happening? Where is the gap?
4. **Business objective** — Is this for brand credibility, sales support, talent, or investor narrative?
5. **Resource commitment** — Is leadership willing to invest the time required? Thought leadership is not a campaign; it is a sustained programme.

---

## What Thought Leadership Is (and Isn't)

| Thought Leadership | Content Marketing |
|---|---|
| Challenges received wisdom | Supports existing beliefs |
| Offers a distinct point of view | Provides helpful information |
| Provokes disagreement | Avoids controversy |
| Defines or reframes the category | Describes the category |
| Built on genuine expertise | Can be outsourced without loss |

Genuine thought leadership requires a POV that some people disagree with. If everyone agrees with it, it is content marketing — valuable, but not thought leadership.

---

## The Thought Leadership POV Framework

A thought leadership programme is built on a single owned POV — a belief the brand holds that:
- Is not obvious
- Is grounded in evidence or experience
- Will be contested by at least some in the industry
- Is relevant to the decisions the ICP is making

### POV Statement Structure
> **We believe** [contrarian or non-obvious claim about the category / problem / future]
> **Because** [evidence or reasoning]
> **Which means** [implication for how the audience should think or act differently]

Example:
> "We believe most B2B demand gen programmes are structurally broken — not because of poor execution, but because they optimise for lead volume at the expense of demand quality. This means the entire MQL system incentivises the wrong behaviour, and fixing the top of the funnel won't solve a problem that sits in the definition of what a lead is."

This POV is specific, contestable, grounded, and implies a different strategic direction.

## Process

### Step 1 — Identify the Intellectual Territory
Map the whitespace in the category conversation:
- What ideas are everyone saying? (Avoid these — they are noise)
- What ideas are no one saying? (Potential whitespace)
- What ideas are being said badly or incompletely? (Improvement opportunity)
- What does the business believe that competitors don't or won't say?

Use: competitor content audits, conference session themes, LinkedIn category conversations, customer interview language.

### Step 2 — Define the POV
Write 3–5 candidate POV statements. Evaluate each against:
- Is it genuinely non-obvious?
- Is it grounded in evidence or first-hand experience?
- Does it imply a specific action or direction for the audience?
- Would some respected people in the industry push back on this?
- Does it connect to what the business sells without being a product pitch?

Select one primary POV. Subsidiary POVs can live beneath it.

### Step 3 — Define the Spokesperson(s)
Match spokesperson to audience and channel:

| Spokesperson Type | Best For | Channel Fit |
|---|---|---|
| Founder / CEO | Category narrative, company mission, investor credibility | LinkedIn, keynotes, long-form, press |
| Domain expert / VP | Technical credibility, practitioner-to-practitioner | LinkedIn, webinars, community, podcast |
| Customer champion | Social proof, peer influence | Case studies, community, social |
| Brand voice | Scaled distribution, consistent programme | Blog, newsletter, social, research |

Thought leadership works best when spokespersons are genuine — they hold and can defend the POV in a live debate.

### Step 4 — Build the Content Programme
Map the POV to content formats:

| Format | Role | Frequency |
|---|---|---|
| Long-form essay / article | Anchor the POV with depth | Monthly |
| LinkedIn posts | Build audience and spark conversation | 3–5x per week per spokesperson |
| Webinar / event talk | Live credibility; audience interaction | Monthly or quarterly |
| Podcast (guest or owned) | Reach new audiences via interview format | Monthly |
| Research report | Evidence base for POV; PR asset | Annual or bi-annual |
| Newsletter | Sustained relationship with engaged audience | Weekly or bi-weekly |
| Press / media | Third-party credibility signal | Reactive + proactive outreach |

### Step 5 — Define Distribution and Amplification
A POV without distribution reaches no one. Define:
- Which owned channels carry each piece?
- What earned media strategy supports it? (Which journalists, publications, podcasts?)
- What paid amplification is used to seed reach? (Dark social, LinkedIn thought leader ads)
- How does the sales team share and contextualise it?
- How does the community engage with and spread it?

### Step 6 — Measure Programme Health
Thought leadership metrics are slower and softer than demand gen metrics. Measure:

| Metric | What It Indicates |
|---|---|
| Branded search volume | Audience actively seeking the brand out |
| Share of voice in category | Relative presence in category conversations |
| LinkedIn follower growth and engagement rate | Audience resonance |
| Inbound media and podcast requests | External recognition of authority |
| "First heard of you through [content]" attribution | Top-of-funnel influence |
| Speaker invitations | Peer recognition of expertise |
| NPS / "how did you hear about us?" | Qualitative credibility signal |

Set a 12–18 month horizon for meaningful measurement. Thought leadership compounds slowly.

---

## Output Formats

### Thought Leadership Strategy Document
- POV statement
- Intellectual territory map (what's owned, what's contested, what's available)
- Spokesperson plan
- Content programme by format and frequency
- Distribution and amplification plan
- Measurement framework (12-month)

### POV Document ("The Manifesto")
A 500–1,500 word statement of the brand's or leader's core belief:
- The problem with conventional wisdom
- The evidence and reasoning
- The implication
- The call to think or act differently

### Speaker Brief
For conference or podcast appearances:
- Core POV and key arguments
- Three supporting data points
- Common objections and responses
- Stories and examples
- What the audience should take away

### Thought Leadership Content Calendar
Monthly plan showing:
- Long-form piece topic and outline
- LinkedIn content themes by week
- Webinar or event appearances
- Podcast outreach targets
- Press / media pitches

---

## Common Mistakes to Avoid
- **Thought leadership without a POV.** Publishing expertise without a perspective is education, not leadership.
- **POV that is a product pitch.** "We believe companies need [thing our product does]" is not a thought leadership POV. It's an ad.
- **Thought leadership with no budget for distribution.** Creating is not enough. Investment in amplification is what builds an audience.
- **Inconsistent programme.** Thought leadership requires sustained output. One great piece a year does not build authority.
- **Spokesperson who won't commit.** Ghostwriting is a tool, but if the leader never engages with comments, shares personal perspective in real time, or defends the POV in debate, the audience will sense the inauthenticity.

---

## Quality Check
Before submitting, ask:
- [ ] Is the POV specific enough that some people would disagree with it?
- [ ] Is the POV connected to business outcomes without being a product pitch?
- [ ] Is there a named spokesperson who genuinely holds and can defend the POV?
- [ ] Does the content programme have a sustained cadence, not just a launch push?
- [ ] Is there an amplification plan beyond just publishing?

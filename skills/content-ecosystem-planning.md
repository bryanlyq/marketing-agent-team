# Content Ecosystem Planning

## Purpose
Design a content system that serves multiple audiences, funnel stages, and formats efficiently — so content investment compounds over time rather than producing isolated pieces that decay in value.

---

## When to Use This Skill
- Building a content strategy from scratch
- Auditing an existing content programme that lacks coherence
- Scaling content output without proportional headcount growth
- Aligning content to ICP priorities and search demand
- Integrating SEO, thought leadership, and campaign content into one system

---

## Briefing Checklist
Before starting, confirm:
1. **Business objectives** — What does content need to achieve? (Pipeline, brand, retention, SEO?)
2. **ICP and audience** — Who is the content for? Which segments and buyer roles?
3. **Current state** — What content exists? Has it been audited?
4. **Resources** — What team, budget, and production capacity is available?
5. **Channels** — Which owned, earned, and paid channels will content feed?

---

## Content Ecosystem vs Content Calendar

| Content Ecosystem | Content Calendar |
|---|---|
| The strategic architecture: what content exists, how it connects, and what it collectively achieves | The operational schedule: what gets published, when, and by whom |
| Defined once; updated quarterly | Updated weekly/monthly |
| Answers "are we building the right content?" | Answers "are we publishing on schedule?" |

Build the ecosystem first. The calendar flows from it.

## Content Ecosystem Framework

### Layer 1 — Content Pillars
3–5 core topics that align to ICP priorities, business expertise, and search demand. Every piece of content belongs to a pillar.

Pillar selection criteria:
- Is this topic the ICP genuinely cares about?
- Does the business have genuine expertise or a unique perspective here?
- Is there search demand and/or community discussion?
- Can this pillar support multiple content formats and funnel stages?

### Layer 2 — Content Types by Funnel Stage

| Stage | Content Job | Formats |
|---|---|---|
| **TOFU** (Awareness) | Introduce the problem; attract new audience | Blog posts, social content, video, podcast, PR |
| **MOFU** (Consideration) | Build credibility; help evaluate options | Guides, case studies, webinars, comparison content |
| **BOFU** (Decision) | Reduce friction; build confidence to buy | Demos, ROI tools, customer stories, trial content |
| **Post-purchase** (Retention) | Drive adoption, advocacy, expansion | Onboarding content, feature guides, community |

A healthy ecosystem has content at every stage. Most programmes are over-indexed on TOFU and under-invested at MOFU.

### Layer 3 — Content Formats

Map formats to purpose and resource cost:

| Format | Best For | Resource Intensity |
|---|---|---|
| Blog post / article | SEO; TOFU/MOFU | Low–Medium |
| Long-form guide | Authority; MOFU | Medium–High |
| Case study | Proof; BOFU | Medium |
| Video (short) | Social reach; TOFU | Medium |
| Video (long/interview) | Thought leadership; MOFU | High |
| Webinar | Demand gen; MOFU | Medium |
| Podcast | Audience building; TOFU/MOFU | Medium |
| Email newsletter | Nurture; all stages | Low |
| Template / tool | Lead magnet; MOFU | Medium–High |
| Research report | Authority; TOFU/MOFU | High |

### Layer 4 — Content Repurposing Logic
Each pillar piece should be designed to repurpose across formats:

```
Long-form guide
    → 3–5 blog posts (extract key sections)
    → 1 webinar (present the framework)
    → 5–10 social posts (data points, quotes, insights)
    → 1 email sequence (nurture series)
    → 1 infographic (visual summary)
    → 1 sales enablement deck (for relevant stage)
```

Design for repurposing at brief stage. Don't treat repurposing as an afterthought.

## Process

### Step 1 — Audit Existing Content
Before creating new content, understand what exists:
- Inventory all published content (URL, title, format, pillar, funnel stage, traffic, conversions)
- Classify each piece: Keep / Update / Consolidate / Retire
- Identify gaps: which pillars, stages, or formats are missing?
- Identify duplication: multiple thin pieces covering the same topic

A content audit often reveals that 20% of content drives 80% of traffic. Concentrate investment there.

### Step 2 — Define Content Pillars
Select 3–5 pillars. For each, document:
- Pillar name and topic boundary
- Why it matters to the ICP
- Business expertise/POV angle
- Search demand evidence (monthly search volume for core terms)
- Competitive landscape (are competitors dominant here or is there whitespace?)

### Step 3 — Map Content to Pillars and Stages
Build the content matrix:

| Pillar | TOFU | MOFU | BOFU | Retention |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Pillar 1 | Blog post series | Webinar + guide | Case study | Feature guide |
| Pillar 2 | Social series | Comparison guide | ROI calculator | Community post |
| Pillar 3 | Podcast episode | Research report | Demo content | Onboarding email |

This matrix reveals gaps at a glance. Fill MOFU gaps first — they are the most common and most damaging.

### Step 4 — Define the Content Architecture
Map how content connects within the ecosystem:
- **Pillar pages:** Long-form cornerstone content for each pillar (SEO hub page)
- **Cluster content:** Supporting articles that link to and from the pillar page
- **Lead magnets:** Gated assets that convert organic traffic to known contacts
- **Distribution triggers:** How does each piece get promoted after publication?

### Step 5 — Define Production Workflows
For each content type, document:
- Brief template
- Review and approval process
- Production timeline (from brief to publish)
- Owner and contributor roles
- Quality standards

### Step 6 — Set Governance and Refresh Standards
- Evergreen content review cadence: every 6–12 months
- Criteria for updating vs retiring content
- Owner responsible for content health monitoring
- What triggers an unscheduled update (algorithm change, product update, competitive shift)?

## Output Formats

### Content Ecosystem Map
Visual showing:
- Content pillars as lanes
- Funnel stages as rows
- Content pieces plotted by pillar and stage
- Connections and repurposing flows

### Content Audit Report
Table of all existing content with:
- URL, title, format, pillar, stage
- Traffic and conversion data (if available)
- Assessment: Keep / Update / Consolidate / Retire
- Recommended action and owner

### Pillar Strategy (per pillar)
- Pillar definition and scope
- ICP relevance
- Search and demand evidence
- POV and editorial angle
- Content types planned
- Success metrics

### Content Gaps Analysis
- Gaps by pillar and funnel stage
- Priority ranking (impact vs effort)
- Recommended production schedule

---

## Common Mistakes to Avoid
- **Content without pillars.** Publishing without a pillar structure produces a fragmented archive that serves no audience well.
- **TOFU-heavy, MOFU-empty programmes.** Lots of awareness content with no consideration-stage assets wastes traffic.
- **No repurposing plan.** Every piece produced for a single use is an inefficient use of production budget.
- **Audit skipped.** Building on top of poor-quality existing content dilutes the ecosystem's value.
- **Pillars chosen by internal interest, not ICP demand.** Write about what the ICP is searching for and talking about, not what the product team wants to explain.

---

## Quality Check
Before submitting, ask:
- [ ] Are pillars defined by ICP interest and search demand, not internal preference?
- [ ] Does the content matrix reveal gaps at MOFU and BOFU stages?
- [ ] Is there a repurposing logic for each pillar asset?
- [ ] Has existing content been audited before new content is planned?
- [ ] Does every piece in the ecosystem have a defined distribution plan?

# Content Distribution Strategy

## Purpose
Define how every piece of content reaches its intended audience — through owned, earned, and paid channels — so that production investment is matched by distribution investment and content actually gets seen.

---

## When to Use This Skill
- Content is being produced but not getting traffic or engagement
- No consistent distribution process exists after publishing
- SEO traffic is low despite a strong content programme
- High-value content pieces are under-performing relative to effort
- Scaling content reach without proportionally scaling production

---

## Briefing Checklist
Before starting, confirm:
1. **Content type** — What content is being distributed? (Article, guide, report, video, tool?)
2. **Audience** — Who needs to see this content and where do they spend time?
3. **Objective** — Is this for brand awareness, traffic, lead generation, or sales enablement?
4. **Available channels** — What owned, earned, and paid channels are available?
5. **Budget** — Is paid amplification available? What is the budget per piece or per month?

---

## The Distribution Framework: PESO

| Channel Type | Definition | Examples |
|---|---|---|
| **Paid** | You pay for placement or promotion | LinkedIn Ads, Meta Ads, newsletter sponsorships, content syndication |
| **Earned** | Third parties share or publish your content | Press coverage, podcast features, guest posts, social shares, backlinks |
| **Shared** | Distributed through social platforms (organic) | LinkedIn posts, Twitter threads, community posts, Reddit, Slack groups |
| **Owned** | Your own channels and audience | Email newsletter, blog, website, in-app, sales enablement |

Every piece of high-value content should have a distribution plan across all four PESO channels. Most programmes over-rely on owned and shared, under-use earned, and use paid only for top-of-funnel awareness.

---

## Content Tier and Distribution Investment

Not all content deserves equal distribution investment. Tier content by strategic value:

| Tier | Description | Distribution Investment |
|---|---|---|
| **Tier 1** | Flagship content: research reports, definitive guides, major campaign pieces | Full PESO distribution; paid amplification; earned media push |
| **Tier 2** | Strong supporting content: detailed how-tos, case studies, webinars | Owned + shared + targeted paid; earned outreach for relevant backlinks |
| **Tier 3** | Regular content: blog posts, social series, short-form | Owned + shared distribution; no paid; organic reach only |

Define tier at brief stage, not after publication.

## Process

### Step 1 — Define the Distribution Plan at Brief Stage
Before a piece is produced, define how it will be distributed. This changes production decisions:
- A piece destined for PR needs different angles than a piece built for SEO
- A piece going through email needs a strong subject line from day one
- A piece going to LinkedIn as a thread needs formatting designed for that format

### Step 2 — Build the Owned Distribution Sequence
For every piece, define the owned channel sequence:

1. **Blog / website publication** (primary home)
2. **Email newsletter** — feature or link within 48 hours of publication
3. **In-product or in-app notification** (if applicable)
4. **Sales team distribution** — share in Slack/email with talking points for use in prospect conversations
5. **Repurpose for channel-native formats** (LinkedIn post, Twitter thread, email standalone)

### Step 3 — Plan Shared Distribution
For each social platform, adapt the content to be native — not just a link:
- **LinkedIn:** Long-form post or article (not just a link with a caption)
- **Twitter/X:** Thread that delivers value independently
- **Community (Slack, Discord, Reddit):** Context-first post that adds value to the specific community
- **Partner / team amplification:** Brief colleagues and partners to share with their own perspective

### Step 4 — Execute Earned Distribution
Earned distribution requires proactive outreach — it doesn't happen by itself:
- **Press / media:** Identify 3–5 journalists who cover this topic; pitch the angle, not the piece
- **Podcast outreach:** Identify 3–5 relevant podcast hosts; offer the author as a guest to discuss the POV
- **Guest content / contributor placement:** Offer an adapted version to relevant publications
- **Backlink outreach:** Identify existing articles on the topic and reach out to offer the new piece as a citation
- **Newsletter features:** Identify curated newsletters in the category and pitch the piece for inclusion
- **Influencer / creator sharing:** Share the piece with relevant practitioners who may share with their audience

### Step 5 — Allocate Paid Amplification (Tier 1 and 2)
For high-value content, paid distribution accelerates reach:
- **LinkedIn Thought Leader Ads:** Amplify an organic post from the author
- **LinkedIn Document Ads:** Promote gated guides to a targeted audience
- **Facebook / Instagram:** Effective for consumer and B2C content with high visual quality
- **Content discovery (Outbrain, Taboola):** Reach audiences on publisher sites
- **Newsletter sponsorships:** Feature the content in relevant newsletters with a matching audience
- **Reddit Ads:** Effective in specific technical or practitioner communities

Set an amplification budget per Tier 1 piece. £500–£2,000 per flagship piece is a reasonable starting point for B2B content.

### Step 6 — Set a Distribution Timeline
Most content is distributed once at publication and then abandoned. Great distribution is sustained:

| Timeframe | Activity |
|---|---|
| Day 0 | Publish on primary channel |
| Day 1 | Email newsletter feature; social post by author |
| Day 2–3 | Social repurposing (thread, carousel, quote card) |
| Week 1 | Press and podcast outreach; paid amplification activated |
| Week 2 | Sales team distribution; community sharing |
| Week 3–4 | Earned media follow-up; backlink outreach |
| Month 2–3 | Republish or syndicate to secondary platforms |
| Ongoing | Interlink from newer content; update and republish if evergreen |

## Distribution Playbooks by Content Type

### Research Report
- Full PESO treatment
- Press pitch: data angles, exclusive findings, expert commentary
- LinkedIn: launch post + weekly data highlight posts for 4–6 weeks
- Webinar: present the findings to an audience
- Email: dedicated email + monthly reference in newsletter

### Long-Form Guide
- Owned: SEO-optimised; email; sales enablement
- Earned: pitch for guest columns; offer adapted version to publications
- Paid: LinkedIn document ad to ICP audience
- Shared: LinkedIn post series; Twitter thread; community Q&A

### Case Study
- Sales team: primary distribution vehicle
- Website: customer success section
- LinkedIn: story-format post with customer quote
- Paid: retargeting for prospects in consideration stage
- PR: pitch to industry media as a success story

### Video
- Owned: YouTube, blog embed, email
- Shared: LinkedIn native upload (outperforms links); Twitter; Instagram if consumer
- Paid: LinkedIn video ad; YouTube pre-roll for remarketing

- Paid amplification recommendation

### Per-Piece Distribution Plan

- Distribution timeline
- Channel-by-channel plan
- Outreach targets
- Budget allocation
- Owner per channel

### Distribution Calendar
Overlay showing:
- All active content pieces
- Distribution actions by day/week
- Outreach pipeline status

## Common Mistakes to Avoid
- **Publish and forget.** A single social post at launch is not a distribution strategy.
- **Platform-agnostic distribution.** A blog post link on LinkedIn performs worse than a native LinkedIn post. Adapt for the platform.
- **No earned media strategy.** The most credible distribution is through third parties. It doesn't happen without proactive effort.
- **Paid amplification for weak content.** Paid distribution amplifies both good and bad content — investing in poor quality just accelerates its underperformance.
- **Distribution planned after production.** Distribution decisions must influence how content is produced.

---

## Quality Check
Before submitting, ask:
- [ ] Is the content tiered and distribution investment matched to tier?
- [ ] Is there a distribution timeline that extends beyond the publication date?
- [ ] Does the distribution plan include earned media outreach, not just owned and social?
- [ ] Are social formats adapted natively for each platform, not just link-sharing?
- [ ] Is there an owner for each distribution action?

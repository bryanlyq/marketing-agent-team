# AI Integration Planning

## Purpose
Identify high-value AI use cases across research, content, personalisation, and analytics, build adoption roadmaps that balance speed, risk, and capability, and define governance standards for AI-generated content and decisions.

---

## When to Use This Skill
- Developing an AI strategy for the marketing function
- Evaluating specific AI tools or platforms before purchase
- Building a business case for AI investment
- Setting governance policies for AI use in content or decision-making
- Upskilling the team on where and how to use AI effectively

---

## Briefing Checklist
Before starting, confirm:
1. **Business objective** — Efficiency gain, capability expansion, cost reduction, or quality improvement?
2. **Current state** — What AI tools are already in use (officially or unofficially)?
3. **Team capability** — What is the current AI literacy level across the marketing function?
4. **Constraints** — Data privacy requirements, legal restrictions, brand sensitivity, or industry regulations?
5. **Output format** — Strategy document, use case prioritisation, or governance framework?

---

## Process

### Step 1 — Map AI Use Cases Across the Marketing Function
Categorise by function and value type:

**Research and intelligence:**
- Market research synthesis and trend analysis
- Competitive monitoring and content analysis
- Customer sentiment analysis at scale
- ICP and persona development from data

**Content and creative:**
- First-draft copy generation (email, ads, landing pages, social)
- Content repurposing across formats
- Personalisation at scale (dynamic content, email personalisation)
- Brief generation and creative direction
- Image generation for testing concepts

**Campaign and performance:**
- Audience segmentation and predictive scoring
- A/B test analysis and recommendation
- Automated reporting and anomaly detection
- Campaign optimisation recommendations

**Operations and planning:**
- Meeting notes and action item extraction
- Briefing document drafting
- SEO content planning and optimisation
- Budget scenario modelling

### Step 2 — Prioritise Use Cases by Value and Risk
Score each use case:

| Use Case | Efficiency Gain | Revenue Impact | Implementation Ease | Risk Level | Priority |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| — | H/M/L | H/M/L | H/M/L | H/M/L | — |

**Risk categories:**
- **Low risk:** Internal efficiency tools, research assistance, first-draft copy with human review
- **Medium risk:** Customer-facing personalisation, automated reporting, scoring models
- **High risk:** Autonomous publishing, AI-driven budget decisions, unreviewed customer communications

Start with low-risk, high-efficiency use cases to build capability and confidence before tackling complex or high-risk applications.

### Step 3 — Build the Adoption Roadmap

**Phase 1 — Foundation (0–3 months):**
- Audit existing AI tool usage (official and shadow)
- Deploy 2–3 low-risk efficiency use cases (research, briefing, first-draft copy)
- Establish AI usage policy and quality standards
- Train team on prompt engineering fundamentals

**Phase 2 — Scale (3–9 months):**
- Integrate AI into content production workflows
- Deploy personalisation at scale in email and on-site
- Implement AI-assisted performance reporting
- Develop AI prompt libraries and standard workflows

**Phase 3 — Optimise (9–18 months):**
- Apply predictive scoring and AI-driven segmentation
- Automate routine reporting and anomaly alerting
- Integrate AI into campaign planning and budget modelling
- Evaluate custom model training for high-volume use cases

### Step 4 — Define the Governance Framework
Every AI integration requires governance standards:

**Content governance:**
- All AI-generated customer-facing content must be reviewed and approved by a human before publication
- Factual claims must be verified against primary sources
- Brand voice and messaging standards must be checked against the messaging framework
- Disclosures: define when AI-generated content must be disclosed (varies by market and context)

**Data governance:**
- What customer data can be input into external AI tools?
- Which tools are approved vs. prohibited (data privacy and compliance)?
- How is AI-generated data classified and stored?

**Decision governance:**
- Which decisions can AI assist with, and which require human judgment?
- What is the review process for AI-generated recommendations before they are acted on?
- How are AI model errors or bias incidents escalated?

### Step 5 — Measure AI Impact
Define metrics for the AI programme:
- Time saved per use case (hours/week)
- Content output volume change
- Quality scores (before/after human review)
- Cost per output change
- Error or brand violation rate

## Output Formats

### AI Integration Strategy
- Use case map and prioritisation
- Adoption roadmap (phased)
- Technology and tooling requirements
- Governance framework
- Team capability development plan

### Use Case Prioritisation Matrix
- All identified use cases scored by impact, ease, and risk
- Recommended Phase 1 use cases
- Risk register for medium and high-risk use cases

### AI Governance Framework
- Approved tools and data usage policy
- Content review and approval standards
- Disclosure requirements
- Escalation process for errors and incidents

---

## Common Mistakes to Avoid
- **Deploying AI without a governance policy.** Unauthorised AI use is already happening — structure it before it causes a brand or compliance incident.
- **Starting with high-risk use cases.** Build trust in AI outputs on low-stakes applications before using AI in customer-facing or decision-making contexts.
- **Over-automating without quality control.** AI speeds up output; it doesn't guarantee quality. Human review remains essential.
- **Ignoring data privacy implications.** Customer data input into third-party AI tools may violate GDPR or other regulations.
- **Treating AI as a cost-cutting tool only.** The highest-value AI applications are capability expansions, not just efficiency plays.

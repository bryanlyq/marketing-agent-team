# Product Launch Strategy

## Purpose
Build a go-to-market plan that positions, sequences, and activates a product launch — across audience, channel, and timeline — so it generates awareness, drives adoption, and creates commercial momentum from day one.

---

## When to Use This Skill
- Launching a new product, major feature, or category
- Repositioning an existing product for a new audience or market
- Expanding into a new geography or customer segment
- Re-launching a product after a significant change
- Planning a launch for a major event or announcement moment

---

## Briefing Checklist
Before starting, confirm:
1. **Product** — What exactly is being launched? What does it do and who is it for?
2. **Launch objective** — Trials, sign-ups, revenue, awareness, or pipeline?
3. **Timeline** — What is the hard launch date? Are there dependencies?
4. **Audience** — Who is the primary buyer? Are existing customers, prospects, and partners distinct audiences?
5. **Budget** — What is allocated for the launch? Is it fixed or flexible?
6. **Internal readiness** — Is the product, support, onboarding, and sales team ready?

---

## Launch Types

Not all launches require the same GTM investment. Match the launch type to the commercial significance:

| Launch Type | Description | GTM Investment |
|---|---|---|
| **Major launch** | New product, new category, significant market entry | Full campaign; all channels; long lead time |
| **Significant update** | Material improvement; new audience or use case | Campaign to relevant segments; owned + earned |
| **Feature release** | Incremental improvement; existing use case | Product email, changelog, in-app notification |
| **Quiet launch** | Beta or limited availability; learning mode | Minimal; direct to target group only |

A feature release treated like a major launch wastes resources. A major launch treated like a feature release wastes opportunity.

## Process

### Step 1 — Define the Launch Objective
Set one primary commercial objective, measurable within 30 days of launch:
- "Generate 500 free trial sign-ups from [ICP] within 30 days"
- "Close £200K in new business from [segment] within 60 days of launch"
- "Achieve 25% feature adoption among existing customers within 45 days"

Set secondary objectives for brand and awareness if applicable — but keep the primary objective commercial and measurable.

### Step 2 — Define the Launch Audience

Launches typically have three distinct audiences requiring different strategies:

| Audience | Objective | Key Message |
|---|---|---|
| **Existing customers** | Adoption, expansion, advocacy | "Here's how this makes your experience better" |
| **Target prospects** | Awareness, trial, pipeline | "Here's a new reason to choose us" |
| **Partners and press** | Amplification, credibility | "Here's the story; here's how to cover it" |

Build a separate content and communication plan for each audience. Using the same message for all three produces a message that works for none.

### Step 3 — Develop the Launch Narrative
The launch narrative is the strategic story that contextualises the product in the market:
- **The world before** — What problem existed before this product?
- **Why now** — What has changed to make this product possible or necessary?
- **The new way** — What does this product enable that wasn't possible before?
- **Proof** — What early evidence demonstrates the claim?
- **The invitation** — What should the audience do?

This narrative guides all launch content, from press releases to landing pages to sales talking points.

### Step 4 — Build the Launch Playbook

**Pre-launch (4–8 weeks before)**
- Internal alignment: brief sales, CS, and support teams
- Create urgency and anticipation: waitlists, early access, teaser content
- Press and influencer outreach: share story under embargo; gather coverage ready to publish
- Content production: landing page, announcement post, emails, social assets, sales enablement
- Tracking setup: confirm all conversion events are tagged and firing

**Launch day**
- All channels activate simultaneously
- Announcement blog post / press release published
- Email to prospect and customer lists
- Social posts across all active platforms
- Sales team begins outreach to warm accounts
- Press and influencer coverage goes live

**Post-launch (Week 1–4)**
- Monitor performance daily; optimise underperforming channels immediately
- Sales team follow-up on launch engagement
- Social engagement: respond to comments and conversations
- Retargeting: reach people who engaged but didn't convert
- Customer success: pro-actively introduce existing customers to the launch

### Step 5 — Define the Channel Plan
For each channel, define:

| Channel | Pre-Launch | Launch Day | Post-Launch |
|---|---|---|---|
| Email (prospect) | Teaser / waitlist | Announcement | Follow-up sequence |
| Email (customer) | Preview | Launch notification | Adoption prompt |
| SEO / blog | Primer content | Announcement post | Supporting cluster content |
| Social | Build anticipation | Launch post | Engagement + retargeting |
| PR / media | Embargo briefings | Release goes live | Follow-up story pitches |
| Paid | Audience building | Full activation | Retargeting |
| Sales | Briefing + talking points | Outreach to warm accounts | Follow-up on engaged accounts |
| Events | Teaser at industry events | — | Post-launch roadshow |

### Step 6 — Define Sales Enablement Requirements
The launch fails commercially if sales can't articulate the value:
- **One-pager:** What it is, who it's for, what problem it solves, how it's different
- **FAQ:** Answers to the 10 questions prospects and customers will ask
- **Objection handling guide:** How to address the top three objections
- **Competitive positioning:** How does this compare to alternatives?
- **Demo script:** How to show the product in 10 minutes
- **Pricing guidance:** What to say about price; what flexibility exists

### Step 7 — Set Measurement Plan
Define in advance:
- Primary KPI and target (30-day milestone)
- Secondary KPIs (awareness, engagement, trial, pipeline)
- Attribution: how will launch-generated revenue be tracked?
- Review cadence: Day 1, Day 7, Day 30 readouts
- Decision rules: what triggers a strategy change?

---

## Output Formats

### GTM Strategy Document
- Launch type and rationale
- Launch objective(s)
- Audience plan (existing customers, prospects, press)
- Launch narrative
- Channel plan by phase (pre-launch, launch, post-launch)
- Sales enablement requirements
- Measurement plan

### Launch Playbook
Operational document including:
- Week-by-week timeline with tasks, owners, and deadlines
- Asset production checklist
- Channel activation checklist
- Internal briefing schedule

### Launch Day Runsheet
Hour-by-hour schedule for the launch day:
- Who posts what, when, on which channel
- Press embargo lift time
- Social post schedule
- Sales outreach activation
- Internal announcement

### Post-Launch Review Template
- Performance vs objectives (Day 7 and Day 30)
- Channel performance breakdown
- What worked, what didn't
- Recommendations for the next launch

---

## Common Mistakes to Avoid
- **Treating launch as a single day.** Launch is a sequence: pre-launch builds anticipation; launch ignites; post-launch sustains.
- **One message for all audiences.** Existing customers, prospects, and press need different angles.
- **Sales not briefed before launch.** If sales hears about the launch from a prospect, the launch has failed internally.
- **No retargeting plan.** Launch day generates significant intent that isn't captured if retargeting isn't activated.
- **Measuring launch on day one.** Set 30-day and 90-day metrics. Most launch value accumulates in the weeks after.

---

## Quality Check
Before submitting, ask:
- [ ] Is the launch objective commercial and measurable within a defined timeframe?
- [ ] Is there a distinct content and communication plan for each audience (customers, prospects, press)?
- [ ] Is the pre-launch sequence building anticipation before the launch date?
- [ ] Has sales been briefed with a full enablement pack?
- [ ] Is tracking confirmed live before the launch date?

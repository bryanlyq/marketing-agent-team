# Lead Generation Planning

## Purpose
Design inbound and outbound lead generation programmes that consistently deliver the right volume of qualified leads to the sales team — at a cost-per-lead that supports healthy acquisition economics.

---

## When to Use This Skill
- Pipeline is insufficient to meet revenue targets
- CAC is too high relative to LTV
- Lead quality is poor — high MQL volume but low SQL conversion
- Building or rebuilding the lead generation engine
- Adding a new channel or segment to existing programmes

---

## Briefing Checklist
Before starting, confirm:
1. **Revenue target** — What pipeline volume does lead gen need to support?
2. **ICP** — Who qualifies as a lead worth pursuing?
3. **Current lead sources** — What's generating leads today and at what volume and quality?
4. **Sales capacity** — How many leads can the sales team handle per week/month?
5. **Budget** — What is the total lead gen budget, and how is it currently allocated?
6. **Funnel metrics** — What are the current lead → MQL → SQL → won conversion rates?

---

## Inbound vs Outbound

| | Inbound | Outbound |
|---|---|---|
| **Definition** | Buyer initiates contact; marketing attracts them | Marketing or sales initiates contact |
| **Mechanism** | SEO, content, paid capture, PR, referrals | Cold email, cold call, paid prospecting, LinkedIn |
| **Lead quality** | Generally higher intent — they came to you | Variable — quality depends on targeting precision |
| **Cost** | Lower cost-per-lead at scale; high upfront investment | Higher CPL typically; faster to stand up |
| **Time to result** | Months to years for organic; immediate for paid | Immediate with the right list and messaging |
| **Best used for** | Categories with active search demand | Account-based, niche segments, no search demand |

Most effective programmes blend both. Inbound fills the funnel with warm demand; outbound targets specific accounts that fit the ICP precisely.

## Lead Generation Channels

### Inbound Channels
- **Organic search (SEO)** — High-intent, lower cost at scale; 6–12 month build time
- **Paid search (PPC)** — Immediate; high intent; increasingly expensive in saturated categories
- **Content marketing** — Builds authority and organic leads over time; requires consistent investment
- **Social media (organic)** — Low direct conversion rate; valuable for brand and nurture
- **Paid social** — Effective for demand creation and remarketing; lower intent than search
- **Referral programmes** — High quality; scales with existing customer base
- **Review sites** — G2, Capterra, Trustpilot; high-intent; requires active review generation
- **Partnerships and co-marketing** — High quality; takes time to develop

### Outbound Channels
- **Cold email** — Scalable; low cost; declining effectiveness without strong personalisation
- **LinkedIn outreach** — Effective for B2B; high visibility; compliance-sensitive
- **Cold calling** — High friction; high cost; effective when combined with other touches
- **Direct mail** — High standout; low volume; effective for ABM and enterprise
- **Event and conference outreach** — High quality; high cost; strong for relationship-building

## Process

### Step 1 — Set Lead Volume Targets
Work backwards from revenue goal:

| Metric | Value |
|---|---|
| Revenue target | £X |
| Average deal size | £Y |
| Deals needed | X ÷ Y |
| Win rate | Z% |
| Opportunities needed | Deals ÷ win rate |
| SQL → opportunity rate | A% |
| SQLs needed | Opportunities ÷ A |
| MQL → SQL rate | B% |
| MQLs needed | SQLs ÷ B |

This model exposes how volume requirements and conversion rates interact. Small improvements in mid-funnel conversion rates have disproportionate impact on required MQL volume.

### Step 2 — Define Lead Qualification Criteria
Build a lead scoring model aligned to the ICP:

**Fit score (demographic/firmographic):**
- Industry match: +15
- Company size match: +15
- Geography match: +10
- Job title match: +20
- Technology stack match: +10

**Engagement score (behavioural):**
- Pricing page visit: +20
- Case study download: +15
- Demo page visit: +25
- Email click: +10
- Webinar attendance: +20

Define MQL threshold (e.g., ≥60 points). Review and recalibrate quarterly based on SQL conversion rates.

### Step 3 — Audit Current Lead Sources
For each current channel:
- Lead volume (monthly average)
- MQL conversion rate
- SQL conversion rate
- Cost-per-lead and cost-per-SQL
- Pipeline and revenue influenced

Rank channels by cost-per-SQL, not cost-per-lead. A cheaper lead that never qualifies is more expensive than a pricier lead that converts.

### Step 4 — Design the Channel Mix
Allocate investment based on:
- Short-term pipeline need (outbound and paid capture)
- Long-term cost efficiency (SEO, content, referrals)
- Segment-specific channel fit (where does the ICP actually spend time?)

Ensure no single channel represents more than 40% of MQL volume — concentration creates fragility.

### Step 5 — Build the Content and Offer Architecture
Define what leads convert on at each funnel stage:

| Stage | Offer Type | Example |
|---|---|---|
| TOFU (awareness) | Education, no commitment | Guide, checklist, newsletter, podcast |
| MOFU (consideration) | Proof and demonstration | Case study, webinar, comparison guide |
| BOFU (decision) | Direct conversion | Demo request, free trial, proposal |

Leads that convert on TOFU content need a nurture path. Don't hand them to sales immediately.

### Step 6 — Define Handoff and SLA Protocol
Align with sales on:
- MQL definition and scoring threshold
- Time-to-follow-up SLA (e.g., MQLs followed up within 24 hours)
- Lead routing rules (by geography, segment, account size, source)
- Feedback loop: how does sales flag poor-quality leads back to marketing?

## Output Formats

### Lead Gen Playbook
- Volume targets by channel and month
- Channel strategy and rationale
- Lead scoring model
- Content and offer architecture
- Handoff and SLA protocol
- Measurement and reporting framework

### Scoring Matrix
Table with:
- Fit criteria and point values
- Engagement criteria and point values
- MQL threshold
- SQL threshold

### Channel Prioritisation Matrix
| Channel | Monthly MQL Volume | CPL | Cost-per-SQL | ICP Fit | Priority |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|

### Lead Gen Roadmap (90-Day)
- Month 1: Foundation (ICP, scoring, tracking, top-priority channel activation)
- Month 2: Expansion (second-tier channels, nurture activation)
- Month 3: Optimisation (test results, reallocation, volume scaling)

## Common Mistakes to Avoid
- **Optimising for lead volume, not lead quality.** High-volume low-fit leads waste sales capacity and inflate CPL metrics.
- **No mid-funnel content.** TOFU leads without nurture don't convert; BOFU content for cold audiences doesn't engage.
- **MQL definition not agreed with sales.** If sales doesn't trust the MQL definition, the handoff breaks down.
- **No lead source attribution.** You can't optimise the channel mix if you don't know which channels are working.
- **Single-channel dependency.** One algorithm change, one policy update, one CPL spike can collapse the pipeline.

## Quality Check
Before submitting, ask:
- [ ] Are lead volume targets built bottom-up from the revenue goal?
- [ ] Is there a lead scoring model with defined MQL threshold?
- [ ] Does the channel mix include both short-term and long-term lead sources?
- [ ] Is the MQL definition agreed with the sales team?
- [ ] Is there a handoff SLA and a feedback loop from sales to marketing?

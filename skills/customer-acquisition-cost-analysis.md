# Customer Acquisition Cost Analysis

## Purpose
Calculate blended and channel-specific CAC with full cost inclusion, benchmark against industry norms and LTV targets, and identify specific levers to optimise acquisition efficiency.

---

## When to Use This Skill
- Evaluating marketing and sales efficiency
- Justifying budget allocation or headcount investment
- Diagnosing growth economics before scaling spend
- Investor reporting or financial planning

---

## Briefing Checklist
Before starting, confirm:
1. **Business objective** — Cost reduction, benchmarking, budget justification, or efficiency audit?
2. **Time period** — Which period(s) to measure: monthly, quarterly, annual, or cohort-based?
3. **Cost data** — What marketing and sales cost data is accessible?
4. **Segmentation** — Do we need CAC by channel, segment, product, or region?
5. **LTV baseline** — Is there an existing LTV figure to benchmark against?

---

## Process

### Step 1 — Define CAC Correctly
**Blended CAC** = Total Sales & Marketing Costs ÷ Total New Customers Acquired (same period)

Full cost inclusion means:
- All paid media spend
- Agency and freelancer fees
- Marketing team salaries and benefits (prorated)
- Sales team salaries and commissions
- Technology and tooling costs
- Events and sponsorships
- Content production costs

Partial CAC (excluding salaries or tooling) is misleading — flag it clearly if used.

### Step 3 — Benchmark CAC
Compare against:
- **Internal benchmarks:** Prior period CAC by channel
- **LTV ratio target:** CAC should be ≤ 1/3 of LTV (LTV:CAC ratio ≥ 3:1 is the standard benchmark)
- **Payback period:** Months of revenue to recover CAC — target varies by business model (SaaS: 12–18 months; e-commerce: 3–6 months)
- **Industry benchmarks:** Source from comparable business models and growth stages

|---|---|---|---|

| LTV:CAC ratio | — | ≥ 3:1 | — |
| CAC payback period | — | 12–18 mo (SaaS) | — |

### Step 4 — Identify CAC Drivers
Decompose CAC movements into root causes:
- **Volume effect:** More spend, more customers, same efficiency
- **Efficiency effect:** Same spend, fewer customers (CAC rising), or more customers (CAC falling)
- **Mix effect:** Shift toward higher- or lower-CAC channels

For each channel, ask:
- Is CAC rising or falling? Why?
- Is this channel at or beyond diminishing returns?
- What is the ceiling volume at current CAC?

### Step 5 — Identify Optimisation Levers

| Lever | Where It Acts | Expected Impact |
|---|---|---|
| Improve conversion rates | Reduces CAC at constant spend | High — every % improvement compounds |
| Shift channel mix toward lower-CAC sources | Reduces blended CAC | Medium — constrained by volume |
| Improve lead quality / ICP fit | Improves SQL conversion, reduces sales CAC | High in B2B |
| Reduce sales cycle length | Lowers sales team cost per acquisition | Medium |
| Invest in organic / earned channels | Reduces paid dependence over time | High long-term, slow near-term |
| Referral and partner programmes | Lowest CAC source in most models | High if designed well |

## Output Formats

### CAC Model
- Cost inputs (all categories)
- New customer counts by period and channel
- Blended and channel-specific CAC
- LTV:CAC ratio and payback period

### Channel Efficiency Report
- CAC by channel ranked from lowest to highest
- Volume at each CAC level
- Diminishing returns assessment
- Recommended channel investment shifts

### Acquisition Economics Dashboard
- Blended CAC trend (rolling 12 months)
- CAC vs LTV over time
- CAC payback period trend
- Channel mix vs CAC correlation

---

## Common Mistakes to Avoid
- **Excluding salary costs.** This artificially deflates CAC and misrepresents true economics.
- **Using a period mismatch.** Spend in Q1 often converts customers in Q2 — use cohort matching where possible.
- **Conflating leads with customers.** CAC is per acquired customer, not per lead.
- **Ignoring channel volume ceilings.** The lowest-CAC channel may not scale — report capacity alongside efficiency.
- **Treating CAC as a standalone metric.** CAC only makes sense relative to LTV and payback period.

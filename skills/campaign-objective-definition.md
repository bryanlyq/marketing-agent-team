# Campaign Objective Definition

## Purpose
Set clear, specific, and measurable campaign objectives tied to business outcomes — so every campaign has a defined reason to exist, a success metric, and a way to know if it worked.

---

## When to Use This Skill
- Starting any new campaign — brand, demand, product launch, or retention
- Campaigns that have run without a clear success metric
- Budget conversations that require justification of expected return
- Post-campaign reviews where "it went well" is the only conclusion
- Aligning marketing and sales on what a campaign is trying to achieve

---

## Briefing Checklist
Before starting, confirm:
1. **Business context** — What business problem or opportunity is this campaign responding to?
2. **Audience** — Who is this campaign trying to reach or influence?
3. **Campaign type** — Brand awareness, demand generation, product launch, retention, win-back?
4. **Budget** — What's the available budget, and is it fixed or flexible based on performance?
5. **Timeline** — When does the campaign run and when are results expected?

---

## The Objective Hierarchy

Campaign objectives must connect to business outcomes. Work from the top down:

```
Business Goal (revenue, market share, retention)
    └── Marketing Goal (pipeline, leads, awareness)
            └── Campaign Objective (specific, measurable, time-bound)
                    └── Campaign KPIs (leading and lagging indicators)
                            └── Channel Metrics (impressions, CTR, conversions)
```

A campaign objective that doesn't trace back to a business goal is a vanity exercise.

---

## SMART Objective Framework

Every campaign objective must be:

| Criterion | Meaning | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **Specific** | Named metric, audience, and outcome | "Generate demo requests from CFOs at Series B–C SaaS companies" |
| **Measurable** | A number that can be tracked | "150 qualified demo requests" |
| **Achievable** | Realistic given budget, audience, and timeline | Based on historical conversion rates and available budget |
| **Relevant** | Tied to a business need | "Supports Q2 pipeline target of £800K" |
| **Time-bound** | A defined window | "Within 8 weeks of campaign launch" |

Vague objective: "Increase brand awareness."
SMART objective: "Increase unaided brand awareness among UK SaaS CFOs from 12% to 18% by end of Q3, as measured by quarterly brand tracking survey."

## Campaign Objective Types

### Awareness Objectives
For campaigns designed to introduce the brand or category to a new audience.

- Primary metric: Reach, frequency, unaided/aided brand recall
- Secondary metrics: Share of voice, branded search volume, direct traffic lift
- Measurement method: Brand tracking surveys, branded search trends, reach reporting
- Timeframe: Effect builds over 3–6 months; not measurable in 2-week windows

### Demand Generation Objectives
For campaigns designed to generate pipeline from target accounts or segments.

- Primary metric: MQL volume, pipeline sourced
- Secondary metrics: CPL, cost-per-SQL, MQL-to-SQL conversion rate
- Measurement method: CRM attribution, MAP reporting
- Timeframe: Results within 2–8 weeks depending on sales cycle

### Lead Generation Objectives
For campaigns designed to capture contact information and initiate sales engagement.

- Primary metric: Leads, MQLs, demo requests
- Secondary metrics: CPL, lead quality score, show rate
- Measurement method: CRM and form tracking
- Timeframe: Results within 1–4 weeks

### Product Launch Objectives
For campaigns designed to drive awareness and adoption of a new product or feature.

- Primary metric: Trial sign-ups, product activations, first-use rate
- Secondary metrics: Landing page conversion rate, email click-to-activate
- Measurement method: Product analytics, CRM
- Timeframe: Measured at 30 and 90 days post-launch

### Retention Objectives
For campaigns designed to reduce churn, increase engagement, or drive renewal.

- Primary metric: Renewal rate, churn reduction, feature adoption lift
- Secondary metrics: NPS change, email engagement rate, product usage
- Measurement method: CRM renewal data, product analytics
- Timeframe: Measured at 30, 60, and 90 days

## Process

### Step 1 — Define the Business Context
What business problem is this campaign solving?
- Pipeline shortfall?
- Low awareness in a new segment?
- High churn in a specific cohort?
- Product launch requiring market education?

Name the specific business metric this campaign should influence.

### Step 2 — Set Primary and Secondary KPIs

**Primary KPI:** One metric that defines campaign success. If only one number can be measured, this is it.

**Secondary KPIs:** 2–4 metrics that provide diagnostic context. They don't define success but help explain why the primary KPI moved or didn't.

**Guardrail metrics:** Metrics that should not degrade. (e.g., "Cost-per-MQL should not exceed £350.")

### Step 3 — Set the Baseline
Before the campaign launches, measure:
- Current state of the primary KPI
- Historical benchmarks (previous campaigns, industry norms)
- Seasonal context (is this period typically strong or weak?)

You cannot measure a lift without a baseline.

### Step 4 — Define the Success Threshold
What result means the campaign succeeded?
- Minimum: The threshold below which the campaign underperformed
- Target: The expected result given budget and assumptions
- Stretch: The result that would represent exceptional performance

Use historical data and channel benchmarks to set these thresholds. Do not set targets after the campaign runs.

### Step 5 — Build the Measurement Plan
Define before launch:
- How each metric will be tracked (GA4, CRM, MAP, brand survey)
- Who is responsible for pulling and reporting each metric
- Reporting cadence: weekly during the campaign, final report at close
- Decision rules: if primary KPI is below minimum at the mid-point, what changes?

## Output Formats

### Campaign Brief (Objectives Section)
- Business objective this campaign supports
- Primary KPI and target
- Secondary KPIs and targets
- Guardrail metrics
- Baseline measurements
- Success thresholds (minimum, target, stretch)

### Objective-Setting Framework (Workshop)
For cross-functional alignment:
1. State the business goal
2. Identify the marketing lever that influences it
3. Write the SMART campaign objective
4. Define primary and secondary KPIs
5. Set baselines and targets
6. Agree measurement method and owner

## Common Mistakes to Avoid
- **Objectives set after the campaign.** Post-hoc objective-setting selects for success. Define objectives before launch.
- **Vanity metrics as primary KPIs.** Impressions and follower counts don't connect to business outcomes.
- **One objective for all stakeholders.** Brand and demand objectives require different metrics. Don't conflate them.
- **No baseline.** Measuring a 20% lift requires knowing the starting point.
- **Overly broad objectives.** "Drive growth" is not an objective. "Generate 80 MQLs from the fintech vertical at <£600 CPL" is.

---

## Quality Check
Before submitting, ask:
- [ ] Does the campaign objective connect directly to a business goal?
- [ ] Is the primary KPI specific, measurable, and time-bound?
- [ ] Is there a baseline measurement for every KPI?
- [ ] Are success thresholds (minimum, target, stretch) defined?
- [ ] Is there a measurement plan with named owners and reporting cadence?

# Customer Journey Optimisation

## Purpose
Audit the end-to-end customer experience for friction, confusion, and drop-off — and design specific interventions that smooth the path from awareness to advocacy — so that more of the value the business creates is actually delivered and retained.

---

## When to Use This Skill
- Conversion rates are low despite strong awareness or traffic
- Customers churn early despite good product-market fit
- Inconsistent experience across touchpoints (different messages in ads vs website vs sales vs onboarding)
- Customer satisfaction scores are declining without an obvious product cause
- Preparing for a major redesign of the website, onboarding, or post-purchase experience

---

## Briefing Checklist
Before starting, confirm:
1. **Scope** — Which part of the journey is in focus? Full journey or a specific phase?
2. **Audience** — Which segment or persona is being optimised for?
3. **Data available** — What analytics, session recordings, NPS data, and sales call data exist?
4. **Business objective** — Is this to reduce churn, improve conversion, or improve satisfaction?
5. **Resource constraints** — What can realistically be implemented given team and technology?

---

## Journey Optimisation vs Journey Mapping

| Journey Mapping | Journey Optimisation |
|---|---|
| Describes the current experience | Identifies and improves the friction points within it |
| Diagnostic tool | Intervention design tool |
| Output: a map of what is | Output: a prioritised roadmap of improvements |

Mapping is the prerequisite. Optimisation is the work. If there is no journey map, build one first.

---

## Journey Optimisation Framework

Optimise across three dimensions at each journey stage:

| Dimension | Question | Lever |
|---|---|---|
| **Clarity** | Does the customer know what to do next? | Messaging, UX, signposting |
| **Confidence** | Does the customer believe the product will deliver the promised outcome? | Proof, social validation, risk reversal |
| **Momentum** | Is the customer moving forward or stalling? | Friction removal, urgency, human support |

A journey stalls when any one of these is missing. Friction and confusion compound — a customer who loses clarity often also loses confidence.

## Process

### Step 1 — Map the Current Journey State

For each journey stage (Awareness → Consideration → Decision → Onboarding → Adoption → Renewal):

**Collect evidence:**
- Quantitative: conversion rates, time-in-stage, exit rates, form completion rates, feature adoption rates
- Qualitative: session recordings, NPS verbatims, exit surveys, support tickets, sales call themes, win/loss interviews

**Document for each stage:**
- What channels and touchpoints does the customer encounter?
- What is the customer trying to accomplish?
- What are the emotional states? (Curious, anxious, overwhelmed, impatient?)
- What are the observed friction points? (Where do people stall, exit, or complain?)
- What are the unmet information needs? (What questions are being asked that aren't being answered?)

### Step 2 — Score Each Stage for CCI (Clarity, Confidence, Momentum)

Rate each journey stage on a 1–5 scale:
- **Clarity:** Can the customer easily understand what they should do and why?
- **Confidence:** Does the experience give them reason to believe and trust?
- **Momentum:** Is the path forward frictionless, or are there barriers to progress?

Total score per stage (max 15). Stages scoring below 9 are priority optimisation targets.

### Step 3 — Identify Root Causes at Priority Stages

For each high-priority stage, diagnose the root cause:

| Root Cause | Examples | Evidence Source |
|---|---|---|
| **Message inconsistency** | Ads promise X; landing page delivers Y | Session recordings, bounce rate data |
| **Information gap** | Customer doesn't have what they need to proceed | Exit surveys, support tickets |
| **Trust deficit** | No proof, no security signals, no recognisable validation | Session heatmaps, conversion drop-off |
| **Process complexity** | Too many steps, form fields, or decision points | Form abandonment rate, funnel data |
| **Expectation mismatch** | What was sold doesn't match what was delivered | NPS verbatims, early churn patterns |
| **Handoff failure** | Inconsistency at transitions between teams | Sales → CS handoff, onboarding call quality |
| **Timing mismatch** | Intervention arrives too early or too late | Time-in-stage data, email engagement timing |

### Step 4 — Design Interventions

For each root cause, design a specific intervention:

**Messaging interventions:**
- Rewrite landing page hero to match ad message
- Standardise value proposition across all customer-facing touchpoints
- Create a messaging guide for sales, CS, and marketing

**Proof and confidence interventions:**
- Add customer logos and review counts at high-anxiety decision points
- Create a case study relevant to the prospect's specific use case
- Introduce a guarantee, trial, or risk-reversal mechanism

**Friction removal interventions:**
- Reduce form fields at the primary conversion point
- Introduce progressive disclosure (capture email first; qualify later)
- Streamline the onboarding checklist to the minimum required steps

**Momentum interventions:**
- Add in-app progress indicators during onboarding
- Introduce timely email or in-app prompts when behaviour stalls
- Reduce time-to-first-value with a guided "quick win" experience

**Handoff interventions:**
- Create a structured handoff protocol between sales and CS
- Brief CS on what was promised during the sale
- Introduce a kickoff call template that resets expectations consistently

### Step 5 — Prioritise Interventions
Score each intervention using ICE or a simpler impact/effort matrix:

| Intervention | Impact on Journey Stage | Effort to Implement | Priority |
|---|---|---|---|
| Rewrite hero headline | High | Low | 1 |
| Add logo bar to landing page | Medium | Very Low | 2 |
| Build dedicated onboarding email sequence | High | Medium | 3 |
| Rebuild onboarding flow in product | High | High | 6 |

Sequence: quick wins with high impact first; structural redesigns after learnings are validated.

### Step 6 — Implement, Measure, and Iterate
For each intervention, define:
- **Owner:** Who is responsible for implementation?
- **Deadline:** When will it be live?
- **Measurement:** What metric will change if the intervention works?
- **Review date:** When will the result be evaluated?

Build a 90-day optimisation roadmap with 3–5 interventions per month. Review performance monthly and adjust the roadmap based on results.

## Touchpoint Audit

For each customer-facing touchpoint, assess alignment across:

| Dimension | Questions |
|---|---|
| **Message consistency** | Does this touchpoint reinforce the same core message? |
| **Tone consistency** | Does this touchpoint sound like the same brand? |
| **Visual consistency** | Does this look like the same company? |
| **Expectation accuracy** | Does this touchpoint accurately represent what comes next? |
| **Customer effort** | Is this touchpoint easy to engage with? |

Any touchpoint that scores poorly on consistency or accuracy is a confidence and momentum risk.

---

## Output Formats

### Journey Optimisation Audit
- Journey map (as-is, evidence-based)
- CCI scoring by stage
- Root cause analysis at priority stages
- Intervention recommendations with rationale

### Optimisation Roadmap (90-day)
Prioritised list of interventions with:
- Stage targeted
- Root cause addressed
- Intervention description
- Owner
- Timeline
- Success metric

### Touchpoint Consistency Audit
Table of all touchpoints with:
- Channel and format
- Message alignment rating
- Tone alignment rating
- Recommended action

### Journey Health Dashboard
Ongoing metrics by stage:
- Conversion rate (stage entry to exit)
- Time in stage
- NPS by stage
- Top friction themes (from qualitative data)
- Intervention status (planned, in progress, complete, measuring)

## Common Mistakes to Avoid
- **Optimising individual touchpoints in isolation.** A touchpoint that performs well in isolation but is inconsistent with the journey around it still creates confusion.
- **CRO without journey context.** Improving the landing page conversion rate doesn't help if the onboarding experience causes immediate churn.
- **Qualitative without quantitative.** NPS verbatims tell you what; funnel data tells you where. Use both.
- **Fixing symptoms, not root causes.** A high bounce rate is a symptom. Message mismatch is a root cause. Fix the root cause.
- **No handoff between marketing and CS ownership.** Journey optimisation crosses team boundaries. Without cross-functional ownership, gaps at team handoffs persist indefinitely.

---

## Quality Check
Before submitting, ask:
- [ ] Is the journey map based on evidence (data + qualitative), not assumed experience?
- [ ] Is each friction point diagnosed at root cause, not just symptom level?
- [ ] Are interventions scored and prioritised — not just listed?
- [ ] Is there a measurement plan for each intervention?
- [ ] Does the optimisation roadmap cross team boundaries where needed (marketing, product, CS)?

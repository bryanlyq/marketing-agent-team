# Email Marketing Strategy

## Purpose
Build a comprehensive email programme that nurtures prospects, converts leads, retains customers, and drives expansion — through segmentation, personalisation, and lifecycle logic rather than batch-and-blast volume.

---

## When to Use This Skill
- Email programme exists but relies on one-size-fits-all broadcasts
- No structured nurture or lifecycle programme is in place
- Email performance (open rates, click rates, conversions) is declining
- Building an email programme from scratch for a new brand or product
- Integrating email into a wider demand generation or retention strategy

---

## Briefing Checklist
Before starting, confirm:
1. **Business objective** — Is this for lead nurture, conversion, retention, or expansion?
2. **Audience** — What segments and personas will the programme serve?
3. **Current state** — What email activities already exist? What data and segmentation capability is in place?
4. **Platform** — What MAP or ESP is being used? (HubSpot, Marketo, Klaviyo, Mailchimp?)
5. **Compliance** — What are the consent and data protection requirements? (GDPR, CAN-SPAM, CASL)

---

## Email Programme Architecture

A complete email strategy covers six programme types. Most brands run 2–3. A mature programme runs all six:

| Programme | Purpose | Trigger |
|---|---|---|
| **Welcome** | Set expectations; deliver first value; begin relationship | New subscriber or lead |
| **Nurture** | Educate and build trust with prospects not yet ready to buy | MQL who hasn't converted; content download |
| **Conversion** | Move warm, engaged leads to a sales conversation or purchase | Engagement threshold reached; BOFU behaviour |
| **Onboarding** | Drive product adoption; reach first value milestone | New customer or trial sign-up |
| **Retention / Lifecycle** | Sustain engagement; prevent churn; drive expansion | Behavioural triggers; time-based milestones |
| **Win-back** | Re-engage lapsed or at-risk contacts | Disengagement signal; churn or cancellation |

## Process

### Step 1 — Audit the Current Programme
For each active email type:
- What trigger activates it?
- What is the open rate, click rate, and conversion rate?
- What is the unsubscribe rate?
- Is the content personalised or generic?
- What happens at the end of the sequence?

Identify: gaps in the programme, underperforming emails (low open + low CTR), and high-performing content to replicate.

### Step 2 — Define Segmentation Logic
Email relevance is the single biggest lever for performance. Define segments based on:
- **Persona / role** — Different value propositions for different roles
- **Funnel stage** — Where they are in the buyer journey
- **Behaviour** — Pages visited, content downloaded, products viewed, emails opened
- **Lifecycle stage** — Prospect, trial, customer, churned, advocate
- **Engagement level** — Highly engaged, passive, disengaged

Never send the same email to your entire list unless the message is genuinely universal. Segmented emails generate 58% more revenue than unsegmented emails (DMA).

### Step 3 — Design Each Programme

#### Welcome Programme (3–5 emails, Days 0–14)
- Email 1 (Day 0): Deliver the expected value; set expectations for future emails
- Email 2 (Day 3): Share a relevant piece of content; introduce a key idea
- Email 3 (Day 7): Social proof or customer story relevant to their segment
- Email 4 (Day 10): A specific CTA — what's the next logical step?
- Email 5 (Day 14): Soft check-in; invite a response or feedback

#### Nurture Programme (6–10 emails, 4–8 weeks)
- Map content to buyer journey stages
- Alternate educational content with proof content (case studies, data, testimonials)
- Introduce commercial CTAs only after trust is established (typically email 4+)
- Use engagement signals to accelerate or branch the sequence

#### Conversion Programme (3–5 emails, triggered by intent signal)
- Triggered by: pricing page visit, demo page visit, trial sign-up, engagement threshold
- More direct and commercial than nurture content
- Lead with proof: case study, testimonial, or data relevant to their segment
- Include specific, low-friction CTA: demo booking link, live chat, direct rep contact
- Create urgency where genuine (not artificial)

#### Onboarding Programme (depends on product complexity)
- Milestone-based, not time-based where possible
- Email triggers: "You haven't done X yet" prompts; "Congratulations, you've done Y" reinforcement
- Bring in human touchpoints (CS intro, check-in call) for higher-value accounts
- Define the activation milestone and orient the sequence around reaching it

#### Retention / Lifecycle Programme
- Monthly or quarterly value newsletters (curated, not promotional)
- Usage-based triggers: re-engage when usage drops
- Expansion triggers: flag when customer is approaching usage limits or ready to upgrade
- Advocacy moments: invite reviews, referrals, or community participation at high-satisfaction moments

#### Win-Back Programme (3 emails, 2–4 weeks)
- Email 1: Re-engagement prompt; reference their interests or prior activity
- Email 2: New value or product update since they last engaged
- Email 3: Explicit question — "Should we part ways?" — with an easy re-opt-in or unsubscribe option
- Suppression: remove non-responders to protect deliverability

### Step 4 — Set Email Quality Standards

**Subject lines:**
- 30–50 characters optimal for mobile
- Specificity beats cleverness
- Test: curiosity, self-interest, news, and question formats
- Avoid spam trigger words (free, guaranteed, act now)

**Preheader:**
- Treat as a second subject line
- Don't leave it blank or repeat the subject line

**Body copy:**
- Lead with the most important information
- One email, one message, one CTA
- Plain text or simple HTML outperforms elaborate design in B2B
- Mobile-first: assume 60%+ of opens are on mobile

**CTA:**
- One primary CTA per email
- Button over link for clickability
- Verb + benefit: "Get the guide" not "Click here"

### Step 5 — Set Deliverability Standards
No strategy works if emails don't land in the inbox:
- Authenticate: SPF, DKIM, and DMARC records configured
- Warm new domains and IPs gradually
- Maintain list hygiene: remove bounces, suppress disengaged contacts after win-back attempt
- Monitor: sender reputation score, inbox placement rate, spam complaint rate (<0.1%)
- Segment for engagement: only send to active contacts for time-sensitive campaigns

### Step 6 — Build the Measurement Framework

| Metric | B2B Benchmark | B2C Benchmark |
|---|---|---|
| Open rate | 20–30% | 15–25% |
| Click-to-open rate | 10–15% | 8–12% |
| Conversion rate | 1–3% | 2–5% |
| Unsubscribe rate | <0.5% | <0.5% |
| Spam complaint rate | <0.1% | <0.1% |
| List growth rate | Positive month-on-month | Positive month-on-month |

Benchmarks vary by industry, list maturity, and programme type. Set internal baselines and optimise against them.

## Output Formats

### Email Strategy Document
- Programme architecture (all six types)
- Segmentation logic
- Trigger and flow logic for each programme
- Quality standards (subject line, copy, CTA)
- Deliverability protocol
- Measurement framework

### Email Flow Maps
Per programme:
- Trigger conditions
- Email sequence with timing
- Branch logic (behaviour-based)
- Exit conditions
- Handoff to sales (if applicable)

### Email Brief Template
- Programme and email number
- Audience segment
- Trigger
- Subject line (with 2 variants for testing)
- Preheader
- Core message (one sentence)
- Body copy direction
- CTA
- Tone and length guidance

### Performance Dashboard
- Open rate, CTR, CTOR by programme and email
- Conversion rate (email to desired action)
- Unsubscribe and complaint rate
- List health trend (growth, engagement, deliverability)

## Common Mistakes to Avoid
- **Batch-and-blast to the full list.** Sending every email to every contact destroys relevance and damages sender reputation.
- **One email, multiple CTAs.** Multiple calls-to-action split attention and reduce conversion. One email, one job.
- **Skipping deliverability hygiene.** A growing list with poor hygiene means fewer emails landing in the inbox over time.
- **No trigger logic.** Time-based sequences are less effective than behaviour-triggered emails. Build responsively.
- **Testing without statistical significance.** Calling a test result after 100 opens is not a test. Calculate minimum sample size before declaring a winner.

## Quality Check
Before submitting, ask:
- [ ] Does the programme architecture cover all relevant lifecycle stages?
- [ ] Is segmentation defined — will different audiences receive different content?
- [ ] Does each email in each sequence have one message and one CTA?
- [ ] Are deliverability fundamentals (SPF, DKIM, DMARC, list hygiene) addressed?
- [ ] Is there a measurement framework tracking conversion, not just open rate?

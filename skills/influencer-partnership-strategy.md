# Influencer Partnership Strategy

## Purpose
Design a structured influencer programme that reaches the right audiences through trusted voices — with clear selection criteria, partnership structures, and success metrics — rather than chasing reach or cultural cachet.

---

## When to Use This Skill
- Launching into a new audience or market where brand trust needs to be built quickly
- Organic social reach is limited and paid costs are high
- Word-of-mouth is driving acquisition but isn't being systematically cultivated
- Building a creator or ambassador programme for scale
- Testing a new channel before committing significant budget

---

## Briefing Checklist
Before starting, confirm:
1. **Audience** — Who do we need to reach? What communities do they belong to?
2. **Objective** — Awareness, credibility, trial, or conversion?
3. **Category context** — Are influencer partnerships common in this category? What precedents exist?
4. **Budget** — Total available for fees, gifting, and programme management?
5. **Brand risk tolerance** — What level of content control and approval is required?

---

## Influencer Tier Framework

| Tier | Follower Count | Characteristics | Best For |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Nano** | 1K–10K | Very high engagement; tight community; authentic | Highly targeted; low cost; community trust |
| **Micro** | 10K–100K | Strong niche authority; good engagement | Category credibility; cost-efficient reach |
| **Macro** | 100K–1M | Broad reach; more brand awareness; lower engagement rate | Scale awareness; mainstream audiences |
| **Mega / Celebrity** | 1M+ | Mass reach; aspirational; lower trust signal | Brand awareness at scale; cultural moments |

For most B2B and niche B2C brands, **micro influencers** generate the best ROI: high audience fit, genuine authority, reasonable cost, and measurable outcomes.

Bigger is not better. Relevance and trust matter more than reach.

---

## Selection Criteria

### Audience Fit (Most Important)
- Does their audience match the ICP in terms of demographics, interests, or role?
- Is the audience genuine? (Check follower growth patterns; sudden spikes indicate purchased followers)
- Where is the audience? (Geography, platform, community)

Tools: Modash, Upfluence, HypeAuditor — analyse audience demographics before outreach.

### Engagement Quality
- Engagement rate benchmark by tier: Nano: 5–10%, Micro: 2–5%, Macro: 1–3%, Mega: 0.5–1.5%
- Are comments genuine (substance) or superficial ("Great post!" patterns)?
- Does the influencer respond to their community?

### Content Quality and Alignment
- Is the content quality consistent with the brand standard?
- Does their tone, aesthetic, and perspective fit the brand?
- Do they have prior brand partnership content? How is it executed?

### Brand Safety
- Review prior content for anything that conflicts with brand values
- Check for controversial opinions, brand associations, or community complaints
- Verify they are transparent about partnerships (FTC/ASA compliance history)

### Commercial Reality
- What are their rates? (Benchmark: micro influencer Instagram post: $200–$2,000; LinkedIn post: $500–$3,000)
- Do they have an agent or manager?
- Do they have an engaged, responsive audience or primarily passive followers?

## Partnership Structures

| Structure | Description | Best For |
|---|---|---|
| **Gifting** | Product sent in exchange for potential organic content (no guarantee) | Brand awareness; low-cost seeding; consumer products |
| **Paid post** | Fixed fee for a specific deliverable (post, video, story) | Targeted reach; campaign amplification |
| **Affiliate** | Commission per sale/lead generated via unique link or code | Performance accountability; cost-efficient for conversion |
| **Ambassador** | Ongoing relationship; multiple activations; brand exclusivity in category | Sustained credibility; deeper integration |
| **Co-created content** | Brand and influencer co-create a piece together | Authentic integration; higher production value |
| **Event / speaking** | Influencer appears at, hosts, or promotes events | Premium positioning; live audience reach |

Match the structure to the objective. Awareness campaigns suit paid posts. Conversion campaigns suit affiliate. Long-term brand building suits ambassadors.

## Process

### Step 1 — Define the Programme Objective
Be specific:
- "Reach 500K impressions among UK female fitness enthusiasts aged 25–35 at a CPM below £8"
- "Generate 300 trial sign-ups from US marketing professionals through LinkedIn influencers"
- "Build brand credibility with CFOs in the FinTech sector through association with three recognised industry voices"

### Step 2 — Build the Influencer Shortlist
Identify candidates through:
- Manual search: hashtags, community participation, competitor mentions
- Influencer platforms: Modash, Upfluence, AspireIQ, Creator.co
- Existing customers and advocates: who already loves the brand?
- Community leaders: who runs relevant Slack groups, newsletters, or Discord servers?

Target 3–5x the number of influencers you intend to partner with (many won't respond or won't pass vetting).

### Step 3 — Vet and Prioritise
For each candidate, assess:
- Audience fit (demographic and psychographic analysis)
- Engagement quality (rate and comment depth)
- Content quality and brand alignment
- Brand safety (historical content review)
- Estimated rates vs budget

Rank and select the top candidates. Create a tiered pipeline: preferred, backup, long-term.

### Step 4 — Outreach and Negotiation
- Personalise every outreach: reference their specific content
- Lead with what's in it for them: audience value, product value, creative opportunity, or payment
- Be specific about what you're offering and what you're asking for
- Negotiate: deliverables, approval rights, usage rights, exclusivity, timeline

Secure a contract or written confirmation for paid partnerships before any payment or product is shipped. Include: deliverable spec, posting date, usage rights, FTC/ASA disclosure requirement, approval process, revision rights.

### Step 5 — Brief the Influencer
A strong creator brief:
- Background on the brand and product (short — respect their time)
- Campaign objective and key message
- Required disclosures (#ad, #sponsored, #gifted — per ASA/FTC rules)
- Key do's and don'ts (not a script — allow creative freedom)
- Required mentions, tags, links, or codes
- Content approval process: who reviews? What is the turnaround?
- Posting date and timing guidance

Over-controlling the brief kills authenticity. Give direction, not a script.

### Step 6 — Measure and Optimise
Track performance per influencer and per activation:

| Metric | What It Measures |
|---|---|
| Reach / impressions | Scale of exposure |
| Engagement rate | Audience resonance |
| Link clicks | Traffic driven |
| Conversions (via unique link or code) | Business impact |
| CPM / CPC / CPA | Cost efficiency |
| Audience growth on own channels | Halo effect |
| Sentiment of comments | Brand perception signal |

Compare performance across influencers to identify which profiles, tiers, and content formats perform best. Use this to refine future selection.

## Common Mistakes to Avoid
- **Prioritising reach over fit.** A 1M-follower influencer with the wrong audience is worse than a 10K-follower influencer with the right one.
- **No contract for paid partnerships.** Verbal agreements and DM-based deals create disputes and non-delivery risk.
- **Over-scripted briefs.** Audiences can tell when an influencer is reading a brand's words. Give the message; let them find the voice.
- **No disclosure.** FTC and ASA rules require clear disclosure of paid partnerships. Non-compliance creates legal risk and damages trust when uncovered.
- **Gifting as strategy.** Gifting is seeding, not a programme. It has a low conversion rate and produces unpredictable content. Treat it as awareness investment, not conversion tool.

## Quality Check
Before submitting, ask:
- [ ] Is influencer selection based on audience fit, not just follower count?
- [ ] Are partnership structures matched to the campaign objective?
- [ ] Is there a contract or written agreement for all paid partnerships?
- [ ] Does the creator brief allow creative freedom while protecting key messages?
- [ ] Are performance metrics tracking business outcomes (not just impressions)?

# Brand Reputation Monitoring

## Purpose
Design real-time and periodic brand health monitoring programmes, track share of voice, sentiment, and brand search volume over time, and build rapid response protocols for reputation risks.

---

## When to Use This Skill
- Setting up a brand monitoring programme from scratch
- A reputation incident has occurred and ongoing monitoring is needed
- Annual brand health review
- Preparing for a brand or product launch
- Before entering a new market where brand perception is unknown

---

## Briefing Checklist
Before starting, confirm:
1. **Business objective** — Reputation protection, brand health benchmarking, or competitive tracking?
2. **Brand scope** — Corporate brand, product brands, executive personal brands?
3. **Monitoring channels** — Which channels and platforms are in scope?
4. **Baseline** — Is there existing brand health data, or is this starting from zero?
5. **Response requirement** — Is this monitoring only, or does it require a response protocol?

---

## Process

### Step 1 — Define What to Monitor
Build a monitoring brief with specific tracked terms:

**Core brand terms:**
- Brand name variations (official name, common misspellings, abbreviations)
- Key product names
- CEO and key executive names
- Brand-adjacent hashtags and campaign terms

**Category and competitive terms:**
- Key competitor names (to track share of voice)
- Category terms relevant to brand positioning
- Industry event and trend terms

**Risk terms:**
- Known risk areas (e.g. product complaints, pricing controversy, past incidents)
- Crisis keywords specific to the industry

### Step 2 — Select Monitoring Channels
Not all channels require the same monitoring frequency or tools:

| Channel | Monitoring Type | Frequency | Priority |
|---|---|---|---|
| Social media (Twitter/X, LinkedIn, Instagram, TikTok) | Real-time + volume | Daily | High |
| Review platforms (G2, Trustpilot, Google, App Store) | New review alerts | Real-time | High |
| News and press | Article mentions | Daily | High |
| Blogs and forums (Reddit, Quora, industry forums) | Volume and sentiment | Weekly | Medium |
| Podcasts and video | Mention tracking | Weekly | Medium |
| Dark social (Slack communities, Discord, WhatsApp) | Qualitative signals | Periodic | Low |

### Step 3 — Set Up the Monitoring Infrastructure
**Tool categories:**
- Social listening: Brandwatch, Sprout Social, Mention, Hootsuite Insights
- Review management: Reputation.com, ReviewTrackers, native platform tools
- News monitoring: Google Alerts (free), Meltwater, Cision
- SEO-based brand monitoring: Google Search Console brand query trends, SEMrush

Assign a daily monitoring responsibility to a named team member.

### Step 4 — Define Metrics and Reporting Cadence

**Real-time metrics (reviewed daily):**
- Mention volume and velocity (spike = potential incident)
- Sentiment score and dramatic shifts
- High-reach or high-engagement negative mentions

**Weekly metrics:**
- Mention volume trend
- Sentiment breakdown (positive/neutral/negative %)
- Top themes and recurring issues
- Platform breakdown

**Monthly / quarterly metrics:**
- Share of voice vs competitors
- Brand search volume trend (Google Search Console)
- Net Promoter Score and review rating trend
- Earned media volume and quality
- Brand health survey scores (awareness, consideration, preference)

### Step 5 — Build the Rapid Response Protocol
Define the response process before an incident occurs:

**Triage levels:**

| Level | Definition | Response Time | Owner |
|---|---|---|---|
| Green | Normal volume and sentiment | No action required | Monitoring team |
| Amber | Unusual volume spike or significant negative mention | 2-hour internal review | Marketing lead |
| Red | Viral negative content, media pickup, or sustained attack | Immediate escalation | CMO + Legal |

**Response decision tree:**
- Does this require a public response?
- What is the appropriate channel and format?
- Who must approve before responding?
- What is the holding statement while a full response is prepared?

See Crisis Communication Planning skill for full crisis response procedures.

### Step 6 — Conduct Periodic Brand Health Reviews
Quarterly brand health review should include:
- Sentiment trend vs prior period
- Share of voice vs top 3 competitors
- Brand search volume trend (leading indicator of awareness)
- Top positive and negative themes
- Review of any incidents and how they were handled
- Recommendations for brand protection or reputation investment

## Output Formats

### Reputation Monitoring Framework
- Tracked terms list
- Channel coverage and monitoring tools
- Metrics definitions and reporting cadence
- Triage levels and response protocol
- Named monitoring owner

### Brand Health Dashboard
- Monthly sentiment and volume trends
- Share of voice vs competitors
- Review platform ratings and trend
- Brand search volume
- Incident log

### Brand Health Report (quarterly)
- Executive summary (3 bullets: the key so-whats)
- Sentiment and volume trends with diagnosis
- Competitive share of voice analysis
- Emerging risk themes
- Recommendations

## Common Mistakes to Avoid
- **Monitoring without a response protocol.** Knowing about an incident without knowing how to respond makes monitoring anxiety-inducing rather than useful.
- **Too many alerts without triage logic.** Undifferentiated alerts lead to alert fatigue and missed real issues.
- **Sentiment tools as a substitute for judgment.** Sentiment algorithms miss irony, industry jargon, and context — human review of flagged content is essential.
- **Ignoring review platforms.** Reviews are often the highest-visibility brand signal for prospects — monitor and respond to them.
- **Measuring volume without reach.** 10 high-follower mentions matter more than 100 low-follower ones.

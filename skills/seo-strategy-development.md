# SEO Strategy Development

## Purpose
Build a search strategy that earns sustainable organic traffic from the right audience — aligned to ICP search behaviour and funnel stage — so that content investment compounds in value over time rather than depending entirely on paid media.

---

## When to Use This Skill
- The business has no organic search presence or strategy
- Traffic is almost entirely paid and CAC is high
- Content is being produced without keyword alignment
- SEO is treated as a separate function from editorial planning
- Planning a new website, domain migration, or content overhaul

---

## Briefing Checklist
Before starting, confirm:
1. **Business objective** — Is this for traffic growth, lead generation, brand visibility, or all three?
2. **ICP** — What does the target audience search for? What problems are they solving?
3. **Current baseline** — What organic traffic, rankings, and domain authority exist today?
4. **Competitive context** — How strong is competitor organic presence in the target keywords?
5. **Resources** — What content production capacity and technical development resource is available?

---

## SEO Strategy Pillars

A comprehensive SEO strategy addresses three interdependent pillars:

| Pillar | Focus | Lever |
|---|---|---|
| **Technical SEO** | Crawlability, indexing, site performance | Site architecture, page speed, structured data, Core Web Vitals |
| **On-Page SEO** | Relevance and quality signals | Keyword targeting, content depth, internal linking, meta optimisation |
| **Authority Building** | Domain credibility signals | Backlink acquisition, digital PR, thought leadership, brand mentions |

Neglecting any pillar limits the others. Technical issues cap the benefit of great content. Weak authority limits ranking potential regardless of content quality.

## Process

### Step 1 — Conduct a Baseline Audit
Before building strategy, understand the current state:

**Technical audit (key checks):**
- Crawl budget and indexation issues
- Page speed and Core Web Vitals (especially mobile)
- Internal linking structure
- Duplicate content and canonical tags
- Structured data markup
- XML sitemap and robots.txt

**Content audit:**
- Which pages currently rank and for what?
- Keyword cannibalisation (multiple pages competing for the same query)
- Content quality: thin, outdated, or low-relevance pages
- Internal linking gaps

**Authority audit:**
- Domain Rating / Domain Authority
- Backlink profile: quantity, quality, and diversity
- Competitor authority comparison

Tools: Screaming Frog, Ahrefs, SEMrush, Google Search Console, PageSpeed Insights.

### Step 2 — Define the Keyword Strategy

**Keyword research process:**
1. **Seed keywords:** Start with the ICP's core jobs and problems (not product names)
2. **Expand:** Use keyword tools to find related queries, long-tail variants, and question-based searches
3. **Classify by funnel stage:**

| Stage | Keyword Intent | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **TOFU** | Informational | "how to reduce customer churn" |
| **MOFU** | Commercial investigation | "best customer success software" |
| **BOFU** | Transactional / navigational | "customer success software pricing" / "[brand] vs [competitor]" |
| **Brand** | Navigational | "[brand name] login" / "[brand name] reviews" |

4. **Prioritise:** Score each keyword by:
   - Monthly search volume
   - Keyword difficulty (how strong are current ranking pages?)
   - Business relevance (how closely does this map to ICP needs?)
   - Funnel stage fit

Target a mix: high-volume informational (TOFU), medium-volume commercial (MOFU), and lower-volume high-intent (BOFU). Don't chase volume at the expense of relevance.

### Step 3 — Build the Topic Architecture

Use the pillar/cluster model:
- **Pillar page:** Comprehensive, authoritative page targeting the core topic keyword
- **Cluster pages:** Supporting articles targeting related long-tail and sub-topic keywords
- **Internal links:** Cluster pages link to the pillar page; pillar page links to clusters

This architecture signals topical authority to Google and creates a hierarchy that distributes page authority effectively.

For each content pillar (from the Content Ecosystem), build a corresponding SEO topic cluster.

### Step 4 — Develop the Technical Roadmap
Prioritise technical fixes by impact and effort:

| Priority | Issue | Effort | Impact |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Fix crawl errors and indexation blocks | Low | High |
| 2 | Improve Core Web Vitals (LCP, CLS, FID) | Medium–High | High |
| 3 | Fix duplicate content and canonical issues | Medium | Medium–High |
| 4 | Implement structured data markup | Medium | Medium |
| 5 | Optimise internal linking structure | Low–Medium | Medium |

Technical SEO is a prerequisite for content investment. Resolve critical issues before scaling content production.

### Step 5 — Build the Content SEO Plan
For each target keyword cluster:
- Define the target page (existing or new)
- Write an optimised brief including: primary keyword, secondary keywords, search intent, required depth, internal link targets
- Set a production schedule
- Define success metric (target ranking position, target traffic volume)

### Step 6 — Build the Authority / Link Acquisition Programme
Domain authority is built through:
- **Digital PR:** Create linkable assets (data studies, research, tools) and pitch to press
- **Thought leadership placement:** Guest articles on high-authority publications
- **Broken link building:** Identify broken links on high-authority sites; offer your content as a replacement
- **Podcast and interview appearances:** Generate brand mentions and occasional backlinks
- **Community and forum engagement:** Build brand presence in relevant Quora, Reddit, and community spaces

Set a target for new referring domains per month. Track domain authority growth quarterly.

### Step 7 — Define Measurement Framework

| Metric | What It Measures | Tool |
|---|---|---|
| Organic sessions | Total organic traffic | GA4, GSC |
| Keyword rankings | Position for target keywords | Ahrefs, SEMrush |
| Impressions and CTR | Search visibility and click performance | Google Search Console |
| Organic conversion rate | How well organic traffic converts | GA4 |
| Domain Rating / DA | Authority growth over time | Ahrefs, Moz |
| Referring domains | Backlink profile growth | Ahrefs |
| Organic leads / pipeline | Business impact of SEO | CRM + GA4 |

Review rankings and traffic weekly. Review authority metrics monthly. Set 3, 6, and 12-month milestones.

## Output Formats

### SEO Strategy Document
- Situation assessment (baseline audit summary)
- Keyword strategy and topic architecture
- Technical roadmap
- Content SEO plan (prioritised by impact)
- Authority building programme
- Measurement framework and milestones

### Keyword Matrix
Spreadsheet with:
- Keyword
- Monthly search volume
- Keyword difficulty
- Funnel stage
- Target page (URL or TBD)
- Current ranking position
- Priority score

### Content SEO Brief Template
- Primary keyword and search intent
- Secondary and related keywords
- Suggested title and H1
- Required sections and depth
- Internal linking targets
- Competitor pages to analyse and outperform

### Technical SEO Audit Report
- Critical issues (fix within 30 days)
- High-priority issues (fix within 90 days)
- Improvement opportunities (ongoing)
- Priority rationale

---

## Common Mistakes to Avoid
- **SEO as a separate function.** SEO should be integrated into editorial planning, not bolted on after content is written.
- **Targeting keywords by volume only.** A high-volume keyword with weak ICP alignment drives irrelevant traffic.
- **Ignoring technical SEO.** Great content on a poorly crawled or slow site will underperform.
- **No internal linking.** Content in silos without internal linking underperforms and wastes authority.
- **Expecting fast results.** SEO is a 6–18 month compounding strategy. Set expectations accordingly.

---

## Quality Check
Before submitting, ask:
- [ ] Does the keyword strategy reflect ICP search behaviour, not product terminology?
- [ ] Is the topic architecture built on a pillar/cluster model?
- [ ] Are technical issues prioritised and resourced before content investment scales?
- [ ] Is there an authority-building programme, not just content production?
- [ ] Is the measurement framework tracking business outcomes (organic leads, pipeline), not just traffic?

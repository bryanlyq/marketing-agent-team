# Strategic Roadmap Analysis

## Purpose
Build 12–24 month marketing roadmaps aligned to business objectives, sequence initiatives by dependencies and strategic priority, and define milestones with clear owners and success criteria.

---

## When to Use This Skill
- Annual or mid-year marketing planning cycles
- Entering a new growth phase (new market, product, or funding round)
- Aligning the marketing team on priorities for the next 12+ months
- Presenting marketing strategy to board, CEO, or investors

---

## Briefing Checklist
Before starting, confirm:
1. **Business objective** — What does the business need marketing to achieve in the next 12–24 months?
2. **Starting point** — What is the current state of marketing capability, pipeline, and brand?
3. **Constraints** — Budget, headcount, technology, or timeline limitations?
4. **Strategic bets** — What are the highest-priority growth levers the business is committing to?
5. **Output format** — Visual roadmap, narrative strategy doc, or board presentation?

---

## Process

### Step 1 — Anchor to Business Objectives
Define the North Star: what does marketing need to deliver, and by when?

Translate business goals into marketing objectives:

| Business Goal | Marketing Objective |
|---|---|
| Grow revenue 50% in 12 months | Increase MQL volume 60% (accounting for pipeline losses) |
| Enter a new market segment | Build brand awareness and pipeline in [segment] from zero |
| Improve unit economics | Reduce blended CAC by 20% while maintaining volume |
| Prepare for Series B | Build brand credibility and thought leadership with investors |

### Step 2 — Audit Current State
Assess where the function is now:
- What is working and should be scaled?
- What is underperforming and should be fixed or stopped?
- What capability gaps exist that will constrain execution?

Use a Start / Stop / Scale framework:
- **Start:** New initiatives the roadmap introduces
- **Stop:** Activities consuming resource without sufficient return
- **Scale:** Existing programmes with proven ROI to amplify

### Step 3 — Define Initiatives
For each roadmap initiative, specify:

| Initiative | Objective | Owner | Timeline | Budget | Success Metric |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| — | — | — | — | — | — |

Group initiatives into themes (e.g. Demand Generation, Brand Building, Infrastructure, Analytics).

### Step 4 — Sequence for Logic and Dependencies
Not all initiatives are created equal. Sequence by:
- **Foundation first:** Infrastructure, tooling, and data must precede campaigns that depend on them
- **Dependencies:** Some initiatives unlock others (e.g. ICP definition before ABM programme launch)
- **Quick wins:** Early wins build credibility and prove ROI — identify 2–3 in Q1
- **Strategic bets:** Long-term plays (brand, SEO, community) that compound over time — start early even if results are slow

Map dependencies explicitly so the team understands why the sequence matters.

### Step 5 — Build the Visual Roadmap
Structure as a rolling 12–24 month timeline:

**Recommended roadmap format:**
- Rows: Initiative themes (Demand Gen, Brand, Infrastructure, Analytics)
- Columns: Quarters (Q1, Q2, Q3, Q4)
- Cells: Initiative name, owner, milestone marker
- Colour coding: Launch (green), Ongoing (blue), Review (amber)

### Step 6 — Define Milestones and Review Cadence
For each quarter, define:
- What will be live/completed by the end of the quarter?
- What is the key metric target for this period?
- What is the trigger for roadmap review or adjustment?

Schedule a formal roadmap review at the end of each quarter. The roadmap should be a living document, not a set-and-forget plan.

---

## Output Formats

### Marketing Roadmap (visual)
- 12–24 month rolling timeline
- Initiatives by theme with owners and milestones
- Dependencies mapped
- Budget allocation by quarter

### Initiative Prioritisation Matrix
- All candidate initiatives scored by impact, confidence, and effort
- Top initiatives selected for inclusion
- Deferred initiatives documented (with rationale)

### Planning Document (narrative)
- Marketing objectives tied to business goals
- Current state assessment (Start/Stop/Scale)
- Initiative summary with rationale
- Quarterly milestones and success criteria
- Resource requirements and budget summary

---

## Common Mistakes to Avoid
- **Building a roadmap without anchoring to business objectives.** A roadmap that exists for marketing's sake is not a strategy.
- **Ignoring dependencies.** Launching campaigns before the infrastructure supports them guarantees failure.
- **Over-committing.** A roadmap with 30 initiatives and a team of 3 is a roadmap to burnout, not growth.
- **Static planning.** A roadmap that's not reviewed quarterly becomes irrelevant within 6 months.
- **No success criteria.** Initiatives without measurable outcomes cannot be evaluated or defended.

---

## Quality Check
Before submitting, ask:
- [ ] Does every initiative trace back to a specific business objective?
- [ ] Are foundation initiatives sequenced before dependent ones?
- [ ] Are quick wins in Q1 to build momentum and credibility?
- [ ] Is every initiative owned, resourced, and time-bounded?
- [ ] Is there a review cadence built into the roadmap itself?

# Lifecycle Marketing Design

## Purpose
Map the full post-acquisition customer lifecycle and design targeted marketing interventions at each stage — so that activation, engagement, retention, and expansion are systematically managed rather than left to chance.

---

## When to Use This Skill
- High churn in the first 90 days despite strong acquisition
- No structured post-purchase communication programme
- Expansion revenue is below NRR targets
- Customer experience is inconsistent across segments and cohorts
- Building a CRM or MAP programme from scratch

---

## Briefing Checklist
Before starting, confirm:
1. **Business model** — SaaS, subscription, e-commerce, services? (Defines lifecycle shape)
2. **Customer segments** — Which segments have distinct lifecycle patterns?
3. **Product data** — What usage and behavioural data is available to trigger interventions?
4. **Current programmes** — What lifecycle communications already exist?
5. **Channels** — What channels are available? (Email, in-app, SMS, push, sales outreach)

---

## Lifecycle Framework

The customer lifecycle has seven stages. Each requires a different marketing posture:

```
ACQUISITION → ACTIVATION → ADOPTION → EXPANSION → ADVOCACY → RENEWAL → WIN-BACK
```

| Stage | Customer State | Marketing Goal |
|---|---|---|
| **Acquisition** | Prospect; evaluating options | Convert to customer |
| **Activation** | New customer; finding first value | Reach activation milestone quickly |
| **Adoption** | Using core features; building habits | Deepen engagement; expand use cases |
| **Expansion** | Satisfied; ready to do more | Upsell, cross-sell, seat expansion |
| **Advocacy** | High satisfaction; willing to refer | Drive referrals, reviews, and community |
| **Renewal** | Approaching contract end | Secure renewal; identify expansion opportunity |
| **Win-back** | Churned or lapsed | Reactivate where viable |

## Process

### Step 1 — Map Customer Behaviour at Each Stage
For each lifecycle stage, define:
- What does the customer look like at this stage? (Behavioural signals)
- What is their primary job or concern?
- What does success look like for them?
- What are the failure modes? (When do they stall or churn?)
- What does the data show about customers who progress vs those who don't?

This mapping requires product usage data, CRM cohort analysis, and customer interviews.

### Step 2 — Define Stage Entry and Exit Criteria
Every lifecycle stage must have defined entry and exit conditions:

| Stage | Entry Criteria | Exit Criteria (to next stage) |
|---|---|---|
| Activation | Customer signs up or purchases | Reaches defined activation milestone |
| Adoption | Has activated | Uses product in 3+ sessions per week for 30 days |
| Expansion | High usage or satisfaction signal | Upgrades, adds seats, or purchases add-on |
| Advocacy | High NPS score (9–10) or review submitted | Refers ≥1 customer or joins community |
| Renewal | 90 days before contract end | Contract renewed |
| Win-back | No product login for 30+ days or cancellation | Reactivates or confirmed lost |

Without defined transitions, customers fall through the gaps between stages.

### Step 3 — Design Interventions per Stage

#### Activation Stage
Goal: Reach first value milestone as quickly as possible.

Key interventions:
- **Onboarding sequence** (email + in-app): Step-by-step guidance toward the activation milestone
- **Human touchpoint**: CS or AE check-in call at Day 3 or Day 7 for higher-value accounts
- **Progress indicators**: In-product milestones showing how close they are to activation
- **Friction removal**: Identify and eliminate the single biggest barrier to activation
- **Intervention trigger**: If activation milestone not reached by Day X, escalate to human outreach

#### Adoption Stage
Goal: Build usage habits; expand awareness of additional value.

Key interventions:
- **Feature adoption campaigns**: Triggered when a relevant unused feature is identified
- **Use case expansion emails**: "Customers like you also use [product] for [X]"
- **Success milestone celebration**: "You've done X — here's what's possible next"
- **Educational content**: Webinars, guides, tutorials delivered based on usage profile
- **Health score monitoring**: Proactive CS outreach if engagement drops below threshold

#### Expansion Stage
Goal: Identify and convert expansion opportunities.

Key interventions:
- **Usage limit alerts**: "You're at 80% of your plan — here's how to scale"
- **ROI summary email**: Quarterly value report showing outcomes delivered
- **Upsell campaign**: Targeted offer at highest-readiness accounts
- **CS expansion play**: Account review conversation focused on growth use cases
- **New product announcement**: Relevant new feature or product introduced in context of their existing use

#### Advocacy Stage
Goal: Convert satisfied customers into active referrers and reviewers.

Key interventions:
- **NPS follow-up**: Automated prompt for promoters (9–10) to leave a review or refer a peer
- **Case study invitation**: Personal outreach to customers who achieved notable outcomes
- **Community invitation**: Introduce high-satisfaction customers to the community
- **Referral programme promotion**: Targeted communication to highest-NPS segment
- **Spotlight opportunity**: Feature them in content, awards, or speaking opportunities

#### Renewal Stage
Goal: Secure renewal and identify expansion opportunity.

Key interventions:
- **90-day renewal campaign**: Trigger sequence of value-reinforcement emails
- **QBR / business review**: Strategic conversation on outcomes and next year
- **Renewal health scoring**: Flag red/yellow/green accounts; escalate at-risk accounts
- **Early renewal incentive**: Offer for customers renewing 60+ days early
- **Champion enablement**: Give the champion internal content to justify the renewal to their leadership

#### Win-Back Stage
Goal: Identify which churned customers are viable for reactivation.

Key interventions:
- **Exit survey**: Diagnose the churn reason before outreach
- **Re-engagement sequence**: 3-email series over 3–4 weeks
- **Updated value demonstration**: Show what has changed since they left
- **Re-entry offer**: Specific incentive aligned to the churn reason
- **Suppression**: Remove non-responders after win-back sequence to maintain list health

### Step 4 — Build the Intervention Logic

For each intervention, define:
- Trigger condition (time-based, behaviour-based, or score-based)
- Audience (segment, persona, plan type)
- Channel (email, in-app message, SMS, push, sales outreach)
- Message and CTA
- Success metric
- Next action if no response

### Step 5 — Integrate Channels
Lifecycle marketing works best when channels reinforce each other:

| Segment | Email | In-App | Sales Outreach | SMS/Push |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Trial users | High frequency | Primary | Low-touch only | Feature alerts |
| SMB customers | Regular | Secondary | Reactive | Onboarding milestones |
| Mid-market | Moderate | Secondary | Proactive CS | Minimal |
| Enterprise | Low frequency | Low | Primary (CS/AE) | None |

Match channel intensity to account value. High-touch CS for enterprise; automated email for SMB.

## Output Formats

### Lifecycle Programme Map
Visual timeline showing:
- Lifecycle stages and transition criteria
- All interventions per stage
- Channel and timing for each intervention
- Branch logic (what happens if the customer doesn't respond?)

### Intervention Library
Per intervention:
- Stage and trigger
- Audience
- Channel
- Message summary
- CTA
- Success metric

### Trigger Logic Document
For marketing automation configuration:
- Trigger condition (field value, event, time elapsed)
- Audience filter (segment, tag, plan type)
- Suppression conditions (already in another programme; CS owner flag)
- Action (email, task, notification)
- Delay and branching logic

### Lifecycle Health Dashboard
- Customers by stage (and trend)
- Activation rate (% reaching milestone by Day 30/60)
- Adoption score distribution
- Expansion pipeline
- NPS by stage
- Renewal rate by health score tier

## Common Mistakes to Avoid
- **Lifecycle starts at onboarding, stops at 90 days.** The most valuable lifecycle moments are expansion and advocacy — programmes that ignore post-adoption leave significant revenue on the table.
- **One programme for all segments.** Enterprise customers need human touchpoints; SMB customers need automation. Use channel and cadence to match account value.
- **Interventions without trigger logic.** Time-based sequences are inferior to behaviour-triggered interventions. Build responsively.
- **No defined activation milestone.** "Onboarding" without a success definition produces vague content that doesn't drive behaviour.
- **Lifecycle marketing owned only by marketing.** Lifecycle success requires alignment between marketing, CS, and product. Build cross-functional ownership.

## Quality Check
Before submitting, ask:
- [ ] Is the activation milestone clearly defined and measurable?
- [ ] Does the programme cover all seven lifecycle stages, not just acquisition and onboarding?
- [ ] Are entry and exit criteria defined for each stage?
- [ ] Is channel intensity matched to account value?
- [ ] Is there trigger logic based on behaviour, not just time elapsed?

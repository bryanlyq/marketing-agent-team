# Expansion Growth Strategy

## Purpose
Identify and activate upsell, cross-sell, and referral opportunities within the existing customer base — so that existing revenue compounds through expansion rather than relying entirely on new logo acquisition.

---

## When to Use This Skill
- Net Revenue Retention (NRR) is below 110% or declining
- Expansion revenue is a small fraction of new logo revenue
- No structured upsell or cross-sell programme exists
- CS team is focused on retention but not on growth
- Building a PLG (product-led growth) expansion motion

---

## Briefing Checklist
Before starting, confirm:
1. **NRR baseline** — What is current NRR, and how does it break down by segment?
2. **Expansion vectors** — What upsell, cross-sell, and seat expansion opportunities exist?
3. **CS structure** — Who owns the expansion motion: CS, sales, marketing, or all three?
4. **Product data** — What usage signals indicate expansion readiness?
5. **Customer segments** — Which cohorts have the highest expansion potential?

---

## Why Expansion Matters

For subscription businesses, expansion is the most capital-efficient growth lever:
- CAC for an expansion sale is typically 5–7x lower than for a new logo
- Expanded customers have higher LTV and lower churn rates
- NRR above 100% means the business grows even without acquiring new customers
- Companies with NRR >120% grow faster than their acquisition cohort — the base becomes a compounding engine

If NRR is below 100%, the business is on a treadmill: it must acquire new customers just to replace the revenue lost to contraction and churn.

---

## Expansion Revenue Vectors

| Vector | Description | Mechanism |
|---|---|---|
| **Upsell** | Move customer to a higher tier or plan | Upgrade prompt, QBR, CS conversation |
| **Cross-sell** | Sell an additional product or module | Targeted campaign, in-product prompt, CS play |
| **Seat expansion** | Add users, licences, or seats | Usage limit alert, team adoption campaign |
| **Usage-based expansion** | Customer naturally grows into higher spend | Usage monitoring; proactive capacity planning |
| **Geographic expansion** | Extend contract to new markets or offices | Enterprise account planning |
| **Referral** | Customer introduces a new buying organisation | Referral programme, advocate programme |

## Process

### Step 1 — Segment the Existing Base for Expansion Potential
Not all customers are ready to expand. Build an expansion prioritisation model:

**Tier 1 (Highest priority):**
- High product engagement (usage above threshold)
- High NPS (8–10 / promoter)
- Approaching usage limits or feature ceiling
- Strong ROI demonstrated (QBR data or case study)
- Greenfield within the account (untapped departments, regions, or use cases)

**Tier 2 (Medium priority):**
- Moderate engagement
- Neutral NPS (7)
- Specific cross-sell use case identified
- New champion or stakeholder within the account

**Tier 3 (Low priority / retention focus):**
- Low engagement
- Passive or detractor NPS
- Churn risk — protect before expanding

Do not run expansion plays on Tier 3 accounts. Expansion conversations on unhappy accounts accelerate churn.

### Step 2 — Identify Expansion Triggers

Build a trigger library — specific signals that indicate a customer is ready for expansion:

| Trigger | Signal | Expansion Play |
|---|---|---|
| Usage limit reached | Customer hits 90% of plan limit | Proactive upgrade conversation |
| New feature usage | Customer activates a feature connected to a higher tier | Upgrade prompt in-product + CS email |
| Team growth | Champion mentions hiring; LinkedIn team size growth | Seat expansion conversation |
| NPS promoter | Score of 9–10 on NPS | Referral invitation + case study request |
| QBR success | Positive outcome demonstrated in quarterly review | Upsell conversation |
| New department contact | Second business unit reaches out | Cross-sell motion for adjacent use case |
| Champion departures | Champion leaves; new stakeholder | Re-onboarding + relationship expansion opportunity |

### Step 3 — Design the Expansion Plays

For each expansion vector, define a structured play:

**Upsell play:**
1. Trigger: Usage data + positive NPS or successful QBR
2. CS or AE prepares ROI summary for current plan
3. Conversation: "You've achieved [X outcome]. The next tier unlocks [Y capability] — which would allow [Z additional outcome]."
4. Offer: 30-day access to the next tier features (proof of value before full upgrade)
5. Follow-up: Email confirmation of outcomes; commercial proposal

**Seat expansion play:**
1. Trigger: Champion mentions a colleague using workarounds; or usage shows shared credentials
2. Identify which other teams or users could benefit
3. Conversation: "We're seeing [X] teams adopt this approach — would it make sense to extend this to [team/department]?"
4. Offer: Team/department pricing; onboarding support for new users

**Cross-sell play:**
1. Trigger: Customer uses adjacent workflows in the product; or mentions a problem the second product solves
2. CS briefs the appropriate AE or provides a relevant case study
3. Warm introduction email or in-product notification about the adjacent product
4. Demo or trial of the additional product in context of the customer's existing use case

**Referral play:**
1. Trigger: NPS score of 9–10; or case study published; or organic mention by the customer
2. Direct ask: "Who else in your network faces a similar challenge?"
3. Referral programme: Incentive for the customer (credit, feature access, recognition)
4. Track: Named referral + attribution; close feedback loop to the referring customer

### Step 4 — Build the Expansion Programme Infrastructure

**In-product expansion:**
- Usage limit alerts with upgrade prompts
- Feature discovery banners for higher-tier features
- Team invitation flows to drive seat expansion

**Marketing expansion:**
- Expansion email campaigns for Tier 1 and 2 customers
- Case study and ROI content to reinforce value before expansion conversations
- Referral programme communications

**CS expansion:**
- QBR template that includes expansion agenda
- Expansion play library for CS managers
- Expansion quota or target for CS team (not just retention)

**Alignment between CS and marketing:**
- Marketing creates the content and campaigns; CS delivers the conversation
- CS provides signal (NPS, usage, account health) to inform marketing targeting
- Joint expansion pipeline review monthly

### Step 5 — Measure Expansion Performance

| Metric | What It Measures |
|---|---|
| Net Revenue Retention (NRR) | Total health of the existing base including expansion and churn |
| Gross Revenue Retention (GRR) | Retention without expansion — measures pure churn |
| Expansion MRR / ARR | Revenue added from existing customers in a period |
| Upsell conversion rate | % of Tier 1 accounts that upgrade |
| Cross-sell attachment rate | % of customers who buy a second product |
| Average expansion ACV | Revenue increase per expansion event |
| Referral conversion rate | % of referrals that become customers |
| Expansion pipeline | Expansion opportunities created vs closed |

## Output Formats

### Expansion Strategy Document
- NRR analysis and segment breakdown
- Expansion vector prioritisation
- Trigger library
- Play designs by vector
- Programme infrastructure (in-product, marketing, CS)
- Measurement framework

### Account Segmentation for Expansion
Tier 1 / 2 / 3 account list with:
- Expansion potential score
- Primary trigger identified
- Recommended play
- Owner (CS or AE)

### Expansion Playbook
For CS and sales teams:
- Trigger → play mapping
- Conversation guide per play
- Supporting materials (case studies, ROI calculators, product demos)
- Objection handling

### Referral Programme Brief
- Programme mechanics (how to refer, what the incentive is)
- Eligibility criteria
- Communication plan (how and when to invite)
- Tracking and attribution

---

## Common Mistakes to Avoid
- **Running expansion plays on unhappy accounts.** Expansion conversations on at-risk customers accelerate churn, not revenue.
- **CS team measured only on retention, not expansion.** Retention-only incentives produce retention-only behaviour. Include expansion metrics.
- **No trigger logic.** Expansion plays run on schedule rather than on signal produce poor results. Build trigger-based plays.
- **Referral programme not launched.** Many businesses know referrals are high-value but never build a structured programme. The informal referral channel works; a structured programme works better.
- **Expansion and acquisition measured separately.** NRR and CAC should be evaluated together — the business that expands efficiently needs lower CAC to hit growth targets.

## Quality Check
Before submitting, ask:
- [ ] Is the customer base segmented by expansion potential before running plays?
- [ ] Are expansion triggers defined for each vector (upsell, cross-sell, seat, referral)?
- [ ] Does the CS team have expansion targets and playbooks, not just retention metrics?
- [ ] Is there a referral programme with a defined mechanic, not just an informal ask?
- [ ] Is NRR tracked and reviewed as the primary expansion metric?

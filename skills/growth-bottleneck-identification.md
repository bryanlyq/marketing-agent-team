# Growth Bottleneck Identification

## Purpose
Diagnose where growth is most constrained — awareness, conversion, retention, or expansion — and prioritise bottleneck removal by revenue impact and feasibility.

---

## When to Use This Skill
- Growth has slowed or stalled without an obvious cause
- Deciding where to invest next quarter's marketing effort
- After a period of channel or spend changes when the impact is unclear
- Preparing a growth strategy or investment case

---

## Briefing Checklist
Before starting, confirm:
1. **Business objective** — Diagnose current constraint or build a systematic growth model?
2. **Growth stage** — Early (finding PMF), scaling (replicating acquisition), or optimising (efficiency)?
3. **Data access** — Funnel metrics, cohort retention data, and channel performance data available?
4. **Time horizon** — Quick diagnostic or deep structural analysis?
5. **Output format** — Strategic brief, prioritised roadmap, or executive presentation?

---

## Process

### Step 1 — Map the Full Growth System
Growth operates across four zones. Identify the current performance level in each:

| Zone | Key Metrics | Current Status |
|---|---|---|
| Awareness | Brand reach, share of voice, organic search volume, inbound traffic | — |
| Conversion | Visitor → lead, lead → customer, trial → paid rates | — |
| Retention | Churn rate, net revenue retention, logo retention | — |
| Expansion | Upsell rate, expansion MRR, referral rate, advocacy score | — |

### Step 2 — Identify the Primary Bottleneck
Ask: "If we fixed only one thing, where would revenue grow most?"

**Awareness bottleneck signs:**
- Traffic or lead volume is the primary limiter on growth
- Conversion rates are healthy but there aren't enough people entering the funnel
- Brand is unknown in key segments or geographies
- Competitor share of voice is disproportionately high

**Conversion bottleneck signs:**
- Plenty of traffic or leads but low conversion rates
- High CPL / CAC relative to benchmark
- Long sales cycles or poor MQL → SQL conversion
- Trial-to-paid or freemium-to-paid rates are below benchmark

**Retention bottleneck signs:**
- Growth is happening but net revenue retention is below 100%
- Churn is outpacing new customer acquisition
- CAC payback period exceeds average customer lifespan
- Cohort revenue curves decline sharply after 3–6 months

**Expansion bottleneck signs:**
- Net revenue retention is <110% (SaaS benchmark for healthy expansion)
- No structured upsell or cross-sell motion exists
- Low referral rate despite high customer satisfaction
- Existing customers are underutilising product or services

### Step 3 — Triangulate the Bottleneck
Use three lenses to confirm the primary constraint:

**Funnel data:** Where does the drop-off exceed benchmark most severely?

**Cohort analysis:** Are older cohorts retaining or churning? Is expansion happening?

**Customer feedback:** What do customers say is missing, confusing, or frustrating?

### Step 4 — Quantify the Revenue Impact
For each potential bottleneck, model the revenue impact of a 20% improvement:

| Bottleneck | Current Metric | 20% Improvement | Revenue Impact (Annual) |
|---|---|---|---|
| Awareness (traffic) | — visits/mo | — visits/mo | £— |
| Conversion (trial → paid) | —% | —% | £— |
| Retention (monthly churn) | —% | —% | £— |
| Expansion (upsell rate) | —% | —% | £— |

The largest revenue impact at reasonable feasibility is where to focus first.

### Step 5 — Identify Root Causes of the Primary Bottleneck
Do not jump to solutions. First confirm the root cause:

**Awareness bottleneck root causes:**
- Budget insufficient for reach objectives
- Wrong channels for the target audience
- Message not distinctive enough to earn attention

**Conversion bottleneck root causes:**
- Traffic quality mismatch (wrong audience reaching the funnel)
- Value proposition not compelling for the buying stage
- Funnel friction: UX, form length, process complexity

**Retention bottleneck root causes:**
- Product not delivering on the promise made in acquisition
- Onboarding is insufficient to drive activation
- No early warning system for at-risk customers

**Expansion bottleneck root causes:**
- No structured commercial motion for upsell/cross-sell
- Product packaging does not create natural expansion paths
- Customer success is reactive, not proactive

### Step 6 — Define Priority Interventions
Rank by: Revenue impact × Confidence in impact × Implementation speed

Output a prioritised intervention list with owner, timeline, and expected metric movement.

---

## Output Formats

### Growth Diagnostic Report
- Current performance by growth zone
- Primary bottleneck identified with evidence
- Root cause analysis
- Revenue impact quantification
- Prioritised intervention roadmap

### Growth Bottleneck Summary (executive)
- Where the constraint is (one sentence)
- Why it's happening (2–3 root causes)
- What to do about it (3 specific actions)
- Expected impact and timeline

### Strategic Priority Brief
- Business context and growth objective
- Bottleneck identification and diagnosis
- Investment recommendation (where to focus and why)
- Success metrics and milestones

---

## Common Mistakes to Avoid
- **Solving awareness when conversion is the constraint.** More traffic into a broken funnel wastes budget.
- **Treating churn as a product problem and ignoring marketing's role.** Acquisition messaging that over-promises drives churn.
- **Focusing only on the metric that's most visible.** The loudest problem is not always the primary bottleneck.
- **Skipping quantification.** Without revenue impact modelling, priority decisions are arbitrary.
- **Confusing symptoms with causes.** "Traffic is low" is a symptom. The root cause might be wrong channel, budget, or message.

---

## Quality Check
Before submitting, ask:
- [ ] Are all four growth zones assessed, not just the most obvious one?
- [ ] Is the primary bottleneck identified with triangulated evidence?
- [ ] Is the revenue impact of improving each bottleneck quantified?
- [ ] Are root causes identified, not just symptoms?
- [ ] Do recommended interventions address root causes with specific, ownable actions?

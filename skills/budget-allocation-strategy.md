# Budget Allocation Strategy

## Purpose
Allocate marketing budgets across channels, programmes, and time periods using evidence-based principles, and build budget scenarios — base, optimistic, and conservative — to support planning decisions.

---

## When to Use This Skill
- Annual or quarterly budget planning
- Responding to a budget increase or cut
- Justifying current allocation to leadership or finance
- Entering a new channel or exiting an underperformer

---

## Briefing Checklist
Before starting, confirm:
1. **Business objective** — Revenue target, growth rate, or efficiency improvement this period?
2. **Total budget** — What is the overall marketing budget to allocate?
3. **Constraints** — Any fixed commitments (contracts, events, headcount) that must be honoured?
4. **Performance data** — Channel efficiency, CAC, ROAS, and LTV data available?
5. **Output format** — Spreadsheet model, narrative brief, or board presentation?

---

## Process

### Step 1 — Establish Allocation Principles
Define the guiding principles before allocating:

**Brand vs. demand balance (Binet & Field evidence base):**
- B2C: ~60% brand building, ~40% performance marketing
- B2B: ~46% brand building, ~54% demand generation
- Early-stage: skew demand; mature: skew brand
- Adjust based on category penetration and growth stage

**Acquisition vs. retention:**
- Industry benchmark: 70–80% acquisition, 20–30% retention
- Adjust based on churn rate and LTV payback period
- If churn > 5%/month, increase retention investment

**Fixed vs. variable spend:**
- Fixed: salaries, tools, committed agency retainers, event sponsorships
- Variable: paid media, campaign production, freelance
- Keep variable ≥ 30% of total budget for flexibility

### Step 2 — Baseline the Current Allocation
Document how budget is currently split:

| Category | Current Budget | % of Total | Historical ROAS/ROI |
|---|---|---|---|
| Paid Search | — | —% | — |
| Paid Social | — | —% | — |
| Content / SEO | — | —% | — |
| Events | — | —% | — |
| Email / Lifecycle | — | —% | — |
| Brand / PR | — | —% | — |
| Headcount | — | —% | — |
| Tools / Technology | — | —% | — |

Identify: Where is spend concentrated? Is that justified by performance data?

### Step 3 — Apply Evidence-Based Reallocation
For each category, assess:
- Is this channel at diminishing returns or below its efficient frontier?
- What does the LTV:CAC or ROAS data say about efficiency?
- Are there channels with unmet demand that should receive more budget?

Recommended reallocation logic:
- **Increase:** Channels with LTV:CAC ≥ 3:1, strong pipeline contribution, below-capacity
- **Maintain:** Channels performing at target with no clear scaling opportunity
- **Reduce:** Channels below efficiency threshold or at diminishing returns
- **Test:** New channels with strategic rationale and a defined pilot budget (typically 5–10% of total)

### Step 4 — Build Three Budget Scenarios

**Base case:** Maintain current investment levels with evidence-based reallocation

**Optimistic case:** Increased total budget — where should incremental spend go first?
- Define the priority order for incremental investment
- Model the expected return on each additional £/$ invested

**Conservative case:** Reduced total budget — what to protect and what to cut?
- Protect: highest-efficiency channels and pipeline-critical programmes
- Cut: lowest-efficiency spend, brand programmes with long payback, non-committed events

### Step 5 — Model Revenue Impact
For each scenario, project:
- Expected MQL and pipeline volume
- Expected new customer acquisition
- Expected CAC and LTV:CAC ratio
- Revenue range (optimistic / base / conservative)

Present as a scenario table with clearly stated assumptions.

### Step 6 — Define Budget Governance
- Monthly budget vs actual tracking
- Thresholds for reallocation authority (e.g. moves <10% require marketing sign-off; >10% require finance approval)
- Quarterly review to reallocate based on performance

---

## Output Formats

### Budget Allocation Model (spreadsheet)
- Current allocation baseline
- Proposed allocation with rationale
- Three scenarios with revenue impact projections
- Monthly phasing of spend

### Investment Rationale Brief
- Allocation principles applied
- Category-by-category rationale
- Trade-offs made and why
- Risk assessment by scenario

### Board / Finance Presentation
- Total budget request
- Allocation summary (chart)
- Revenue impact by scenario
- Key assumptions and risks

---

## Common Mistakes to Avoid
- **Allocating by habit, not evidence.** Prior year's budget is a starting point, not a strategy.
- **Over-indexing on short-term ROAS.** Performance channels with high ROAS often suppress brand investment that drives long-term growth.
- **No testing budget.** A budget with no experimental allocation cannot evolve.
- **Treating headcount and tools as fixed.** These are choices — review them with the same rigour as media spend.
- **Single-scenario planning.** If only one budget scenario exists, the plan has no resilience.

---

## Quality Check
Before submitting, ask:
- [ ] Are allocation decisions justified by performance data, not precedent?
- [ ] Is the brand/demand balance appropriate for the growth stage?
- [ ] Are three scenarios modelled with revenue impact projections?
- [ ] Is there a testing budget ring-fenced?
- [ ] Is budget governance (tracking, reallocation authority, review cadence) defined?

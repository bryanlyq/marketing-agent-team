# Long-Term Growth Planning

## Purpose
Build 3–5 year marketing growth plans tied to business strategy, define the capability investments, market positions, and brand equity needed to achieve long-term targets, and identify strategic bets, platform decisions, and category leadership opportunities.

---

## When to Use This Skill
- Annual strategic planning cycles (especially when CEO or board sets a multi-year vision)
- Series B, C, or growth equity fundraising rounds
- Entering a new phase of growth (geographic expansion, new product category, enterprise move)
- When near-term optimisation is no longer sufficient to hit long-term revenue targets

---

## Briefing Checklist
Before starting, confirm:
1. **Business objective** — What is the 3–5 year revenue, market share, or valuation target?
2. **Business strategy inputs** — What is the overall company strategy this must support?
3. **Current position** — What is the current brand, pipeline, and marketing capability baseline?
4. **Growth vectors** — Is growth coming from existing markets, new segments, new geographies, or new products?
5. **Output format** — Long-term strategy document, investor narrative, or internal planning brief?

---

## Process

### Step 1 — Define the Long-Term Ambition
Translate the business's 3–5 year goals into specific marketing ambitions:

| Business Goal | Marketing Implication |
|---|---|
| £100M ARR in 5 years | Build brand and demand gen engine capable of contributing 60% of pipeline |
| Category leadership position | Own the category narrative; top 3 share of voice in all key markets |
| International expansion | Build brand awareness from zero in 2 new geographies |
| Enterprise upmarket move | Build executive trust and credibility with Fortune 500 buyers |

### Step 2 — Identify the Strategic Growth Vectors
Long-term growth typically comes from some combination of:

**Market penetration:** Winning more of the existing market — requires brand scale and demand efficiency

**Market development:** Entering new geographies or customer segments — requires localisation and new ICP development

**Product extension:** Marketing new products to existing customers — requires lifecycle marketing and expansion motion

**Category creation / redefinition:** Defining a new category where the brand is default leader — requires thought leadership, narrative investment, and long-duration brand programmes

Identify which vectors are central to the plan, and size the marketing investment each requires.

### Step 3 — Define the Marketing Platform Decisions
A platform decision is a multi-year strategic bet that will shape the function's direction:

| Platform Decision | Example | Commitment Required |
|---|---|---|
| Brand vs demand balance | Increase brand investment from 20% to 40% of budget over 3 years | Patience with long payback |
| Channel platform | Build organic search as a primary acquisition channel | 18–24 months of content investment before returns |
| Community building | Build a practitioner community of 10,000+ members | 2+ year investment in community management |
| Partnership ecosystem | Build an integration and reseller network | 12+ months to recruit and enable |
| AI-driven personalisation | Build a personalisation infrastructure across all touchpoints | Technology and data investment |

Platform decisions are different from tactical choices. They require multi-year commitment and cannot be evaluated on 90-day results.

### Step 4 — Build the Capability Investment Roadmap
Define what capabilities must be built or acquired to execute the long-term plan:

| Capability | Why Needed | Build/Buy/Partner | Investment (Est.) | Timeline |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Marketing analytics function | Attribution and LTV modelling at scale | Build (hire) | — | Y1 |
| Brand research programme | Measure brand equity and awareness over time | Buy (research firm) | — | Y1 |
| Content at scale | SEO authority in target categories | Build + partner | — | Y1–2 |
| International marketing capability | Local market entry | Build + partner | — | Y2–3 |
| ABM programme | Enterprise segment penetration | Build (tools + team) | — | Y1–2 |

### Step 5 — Define Market Position Targets
Long-term growth requires deliberate market position targets, not just revenue targets:

**Brand equity targets (3–5 years):**
- Unaided brand awareness in ICP: from —% to —%
- Share of voice in category: from —% to —%
- Brand preference score: from — to —
- Net Promoter Score: from — to —

**Pipeline and commercial targets (3–5 years):**
- Marketing-sourced pipeline % of total: from —% to —%
- Organic acquisition as % of total: from —% to —%
- CAC payback period: from — months to — months

### Step 6 — Identify Strategic Bets
A strategic bet is a high-conviction, high-investment initiative where the payoff requires time and patience:

For each bet, document:
- **The thesis:** Why this will create competitive advantage
- **The investment:** Time, budget, and headcount required
- **The payoff horizon:** When results are expected
- **The kill criteria:** What signals would trigger abandoning the bet
- **The measurement proxy:** How progress is tracked before the payoff materialises

### Step 7 — Build the 3–5 Year Roadmap
Year 1: Foundation — build measurement, core capabilities, and market position infrastructure
Year 2: Scale — amplify what works, expand channels, begin international or segment expansion
Year 3+: Lead — operate as the category authority, compound brand equity, harvest retention and expansion revenue

---

## Output Formats

### Long-Term Growth Strategy
- Business ambition and marketing implications
- Growth vector analysis
- Platform decisions and rationale
- Capability investment roadmap
- Market position targets (brand and commercial)
- Strategic bets with thesis, investment, and kill criteria
- 3–5 year roadmap by year

### Market Position Roadmap
- Current vs target market position by dimension
- Investment required to close each gap
- Timeline to each position milestone

### Strategic Planning Document (investor-grade)
- Market opportunity framing
- Competitive positioning narrative
- Long-term growth levers
- Capability and investment plan
- Milestone-based success criteria

---

## Common Mistakes to Avoid
- **Treating long-term plans as extrapolated short-term plans.** Three-year plans require different investments (brand, platform, capability) than quarterly plans — they cannot be built by extending the current playbook.
- **No platform decisions.** A long-term plan with only tactical initiatives has no compound value — identify the bets that build durable advantage.
- **Over-specifying years 3–5.** Precision at 3–5 years is false confidence. Define direction and milestones; leave execution detail to annual planning.
- **No kill criteria.** Strategic bets with no exit conditions become zombie programmes. Define what would cause abandonment.
- **Disconnecting from business strategy.** A marketing long-term plan that doesn't reflect the CEO's and board's vision will be deprioritised at budget time.

---

## Quality Check
Before submitting, ask:
- [ ] Is the plan anchored to specific business goals, not just marketing activities?
- [ ] Are the growth vectors identified and sized?
- [ ] Are platform decisions documented with multi-year commitment?
- [ ] Are capability gaps identified with a build/buy/partner recommendation?
- [ ] Do strategic bets have a thesis, investment, payoff horizon, and kill criteria?

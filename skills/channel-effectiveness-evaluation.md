# Channel Effectiveness Evaluation

## Purpose
Compare marketing channels on cost-efficiency, volume, quality, and strategic fit to identify over- and under-invested channels and recommend mix shifts with supporting rationale.

---

## When to Use This Skill
- Annual or quarterly budget planning cycles
- When CAC is rising and the cause is unclear
- Evaluating whether to add a new channel or exit an existing one
- Post-campaign analysis showing significant variance across channels

---

## Briefing Checklist
Before starting, confirm:
1. **Business objective** — Budget reallocation, channel audit, or new channel evaluation?
2. **Channels in scope** — All active channels or a specific subset?
3. **Evaluation period** — What timeframe provides statistically meaningful data?
4. **Data access** — Spend, volume, conversion, and pipeline data by channel?
5. **Decision to be made** — Reallocate, add, cut, or test a channel?

---

## Process

### Step 1 — Define the Evaluation Dimensions
Assess each channel across four dimensions:

| Dimension | What to Measure |
|---|---|
| Cost efficiency | CPL, CAC, ROAS, cost per pipeline dollar |
| Volume capacity | Current volume generated; ceiling volume at current CAC |
| Lead/customer quality | LTV of customers acquired, sales cycle length, win rate |
| Strategic fit | Audience reach, brand alignment, competitive density, longevity |

No single dimension is sufficient. A low-CAC channel that produces low-LTV customers is not efficient in practice.

### Step 3 — Assess Diminishing Returns
For each high-spend channel:
- Plot spend vs results over time — is efficiency declining as spend increases?
- Identify the point at which additional spend generates proportionally less return
- A channel at diminishing returns should not receive incremental budget without testing scalability

### Step 4 — Evaluate Channel Quality (Not Just Cost)
Compare customer cohorts by acquisition channel:
- What is the churn rate of customers from each channel?
- What is the expansion revenue and LTV by channel?
- What is the sales cycle length and deal size by channel?

A channel with 2× the CAC but 3× the LTV may be the better investment.

### Step 5 — Identify Over- and Under-Invested Channels
**Over-invested signals:**
- Declining efficiency (rising CAC, falling ROAS)
- Audience saturation (frequency caps hit, declining reach)
- High spend share with below-average LTV:CAC

**Under-invested signals:**
- Strong efficiency metrics but low budget share
- Positive trends with limited investment
- Competitive whitespace the brand isn't filling
- Channels that competitors are winning in

### Step 6 — Model the Reallocation
For proposed budget shifts, model the expected impact:
- What volume and revenue is projected from increased investment in Channel A?
- What is the expected efficiency curve as spend scales?
- What volume is lost from reducing Channel B, and at what cost?

Present a base case and sensitivity analysis.

---

## Output Formats

### Channel Effectiveness Report
- Channel comparison table (all evaluation dimensions)
- Efficiency trend analysis (12-month view)
- Quality analysis (LTV and win rate by channel)
- Over/under-investment identification
- Budget reallocation recommendation with rationale

### Media Mix Recommendation
- Current vs recommended channel mix (% of budget)
- Expected volume and efficiency outcomes
- Channels to increase, maintain, reduce, or test
- Phased implementation plan

### Channel Test Brief (for new channels)
- Hypothesis: why this channel has potential
- Test budget and timeline
- Success criteria (break-even CAC, minimum volume)
- Go/no-go decision criteria

---

## Common Mistakes to Avoid
- **Evaluating channels on a single metric.** Cost alone ignores quality; volume alone ignores efficiency.
- **Confusing attribution with incrementality.** Attribution credit ≠ causal impact. Verify with holdout tests where possible.
- **Cutting channels because of attribution model bias.** Awareness channels appear weak under last-touch — don't cut them on that basis alone.
- **Ignoring volume ceilings.** The most efficient channel may not scale — model capacity, not just rate.
- **Evaluating channels in isolation.** Channels interact — cutting one can reduce the performance of others.

- [ ] Is the reallocation modelled with projected volume and efficiency outcomes?
